## The 7 Rectification Methods

### Method 1: Life Event Matching (Most Common)
**Extracted to**: marriage.md, career.md, children.md, health.md, death.md, relocation.md, finance.md, education.md, spiritual.md, birth.md

Work backward from known life events (marriage, career changes, geographic moves, major health events, family deaths, financial milestones, spiritual awakenings, educational milestones) to determine what birth time would produce those events at their actual dates.

### Method 2: Physical Appearance Matching
**Extracted to**: birth.md

The ascendant (1st house) influences physical appearance — height, build, complexion, facial shape, voice. Matching appearance to ascendant characteristics supports rectification.

### Method 3: Personality Matching
**Extracted to**: birth.md

Ascendant strongly influences personality — communication style, emotional patterns, career inclinations, relationship patterns. Personality assessment helps determine which rising sign fits.

### Method 4: Dasha Event Matching
**Extracted to**: All event files

If significant life events correspond to specific Mahadasha or Antardasha activations, this verifies the underlying Dasha timing and reveals the birth time.

### Method 5: Transit Event Matching
**Extracted to**: All event files

Major life events often correspond to significant transits over natal chart points. Matching events to known transits reveals probable birth time.

### Method 6: Navamsa Chart Verification
**Extracted to**: marriage.md, birth.md, children.md, death.md, relocation.md, finance.md, spiritual.md

The D-9 (Navamsa) chart is highly sensitive to birth time — ±15 minutes can change the Navamsa Lagna sign. Marriage, children, and other events matching Navamsa patterns verify birth time.

### Method 7: Body Part Indicators
**Extracted to**: health.md, birth.md

Classical Vedic texts associate physical marks, moles, and scars with specific planetary and house positions. These support rectification as a secondary method.

## When Is Chart Rectification Needed?

- Birth time unknown
- Approximate birth time (e.g., "approximately 8 PM")
- Rounded birth times (nearest quarter-hour)
- Chart inconsistencies — chart doesn't match known life patterns
- Major discrepancies — life events don't fit chart timing
- Cross-border birth records with time zone/DST issues
