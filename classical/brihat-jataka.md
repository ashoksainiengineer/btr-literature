# CHAPTER I: Definitions and Elementary Principles (Zodiacal)

## Key Divisions Used in BTR

### The 12 Signs of the Zodiac (Rasi)

The (twelve) signs of the Zodiac, commencing with the first point of Aries and of (the asterism of) Aswini, and consisting, each, of nine stellar quarters and forming a circle, are respectively the head, face, breast, heart, belly, navel, abdomen, genital organ, two thighs, two knees, two ankles and the two feet of Kalapurusha.

| Rasi | Sign | Part of Body | Nature |
|------|------|-------------|--------|
| Mesha | Aries | Head | Movable, Male, Fiery |
| Vrishabha | Taurus | Face | Fixed, Female, Earthy |
| Mithuna | Gemini | Breast | Common, Male, Airy |
| Kataka | Cancer | Heart | Movable, Female, Watery |
| Simha | Leo | Belly | Fixed, Male, Fiery |
| Kanya | Virgo | Navel | Common, Female, Earthy |
| Tula | Libra | Abdomen | Movable, Male, Airy |
| Vrischika | Scorpio | Genital organ | Fixed, Female, Watery |
| Dhanus | Sagittari | Two thighs | Common, Male, Fiery |
| Makara | Capricorn | Two knees | Movable, Female, Earthy |
| Kumbha | Aquarius | Two ankles | Fixed, Male, Airy |
| Meena | Pisces | Two feet | Common, Female, Watery |

### Dwadasamsa (Twelfth-part division)

A Dwadasamsa contains 2.5 degrees. Each sign has 12 Dwadasamsas named after the 12 signs of the Zodiac.

### Navamsa (Ninth-part division)

The Navamsa is a crucial divisional chart used extensively in BTR for verification.

### Drekkanas (Drekkana or Decanates)

Each sign of 30 degrees has 3 Drekkanas of 10 degrees each. Used in Ch. XXVII for detailed description of birth circumstances.

# CHAPTER II: Definitions and Elementary Principles (Planetary)

## Planetary Natures Used in BTR Verification

### Planets and their Essences (for Kalapurusha)

| Planet | Essence | Gender | Nature |
|--------|---------|--------|--------|
| Sun | Soul | Male | Malefic |
| Moon | Mind | Female | Benefic (waxing) |
| Mars | Strength | Male | Malefic |
| Mercury | Speech | Hermaphrodite | Benefic (if with benefics) |
| Jupiter | Knowledge & Health | Male | Benefic |
| Venus | Desire | Female | Benefic |
| Saturn | Sorrow | Male | Malefic |

### Planetary Colors (used for birth circumstance verification)

- Sun: Red and dark-brown
- Moon: White
- Mars: Red and white (not tall)
- Mercury: Green like bent grass
- Jupiter: Yellow like molten gold
- Venus: Neither very white nor very black
- Saturn: Black

> **BTR Application**: The color of a person will be that of the planet most powerful at the time of his birth (Note to Stanza 4).

### Dikbala (Directional Strength) for Verification

- Mercury and Jupiter: Powerful when in the East (rising sign)
- Sun and Mars: Powerful when on the South (10th sign)
- Saturn: Powerful when in the West (setting sign)
- Moon and Venus: Powerful when in the North (4th sign/Patala)

### Kalabala (Temporal Strength)

- Moon, Mars and Saturn: Strong at night
- Mercury: Strong both day and night
- Sun, Jupiter, Venus: Strong by day
- Malefic planets: Strong during waning Moon (Krishna paksha)
- Benefic planets: Strong during waxing Moon (Shukla paksha)

### Naisargikabala (Natural Strength Order)

Saturn, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Moon, Sun — each naturally stronger than the immediately preceding planet.

# CHAPTER IV: On Nisheka Kala (Time of Conception)

## BTR Relevance: Determining Conception Time → Predicts Birth Time

### Stanza 1: Conception Conditions

The menses that appear monthly, because of Mars and the Moon, bring about conception when the Moon is in one of the Anupachaya signs. If the Moon be otherwise and be aspected by a benefic male planet, there will be sexual union between a woman and her husband.

> **Notes**: Menses appear when Moon is aspected by Mars (Moon occupies 4th, 7th or 8th from Mars). Conception follows if Moon is in Anupachaya signs (any sign EXCEPT the 3rd, 6th, 10th, and 11th from the ascendant in the woman's nativity).

### Stanza 3: Conception Assurance (Planetary Positions)

If, at the time of sexual union, the Sun, the Moon, Venus and Mars be in their Navamsas, or if Jupiter occupy the rising sign or the 5th or the 9th sign from it, such union will produce a child.

### Stanza 11: Sex Determination at Conception/Birth

If the rising sign, the Sun, Jupiter and the Moon be powerful and occupy the odd (male) signs and the odd Navamsas, the issue will be a male child; if they occupy the even (female) signs and the even Navamsas, the issue will be a female child.

- Sun and Jupiter in odd signs → male child
- Moon, Venus and Mars in even signs → female child
- Sun and Jupiter in male double-bodied Navamsas (Gemini, Sagittari) aspected by Mercury → male twins
- Venus and Mars in female double-bodied Navamsas (Virgo, Pisces) aspected by Mercury → female twins

### Stanza 21: Dwadasamsa Method — Predicting Birth Time from Conception (**BTR-CRITICAL**)

Find out the number of the particular Dwadasamsa occupied by the Moon at the time (of conception or query) in any Zodiacal sign. Note the Zodiacal sign whose name the Dwadasamsa bears. Count from the **next sign** as many signs as the number of Dwadasamsas by which the Moon may have advanced in any particular sign. When the Moon comes to such last sign, the birth of the child will occur.

Again, the birth will occur by day or by night according as the rising Navamsa at the time is a day or a night Navamsa. The hour of birth from sun-rise or sun-set may be calculated from the portion of the rising Navamsa that may have risen above the horizon.

> **Example from Commentary**: Suppose Moon occupies the 8th Dwadasamsa of sign Aquarius. This Dwadasamsa is known as the Dwadasamsa of Virgo (Kanya). The sign next to Virgo is Libra (Tula). As Moon occupies the 8th Dwadasamsa, the 8th sign from Libra is Taurus. The child will be born when the Moon passes through sign Taurus.
>
> The particular asterism at birth can be discovered from the Moon's advance in the Dwadasamsa. If Moon occupies the middle of the 8th Dwadasamsa in Aquarius, the middle of Taurus is in the 2nd quarter of Rohini — so at birth Moon occupies Rohini.
>
> If rising Navamsa is Sagittari (night sign), birth occurs at night. Time from sunset is calculated proportionally from the portion of Sagittari Navamsa risen above the horizon.

### Stanza 22: Delayed Delivery

If at conception, the Navamsa of Saturn (Makara or Kumbha) rises and Saturn occupies the 7th house from the rising sign, delivery occurs after three years. If the Navamsa of Cancer rises and Moon occupies the 7th from rising, delivery after twelve years.

> **BTR Rule**: Yogas relating to birth of children with extra/defective organs apply to BOTH conception AND birth time. Yogas relating to miscarriage apply to conception alone.

# CHAPTER V: On Matters Connected with Birth Time

## BTR Relevance: Birth Circumstance Verification

### Stanza 1: Father's Presence at Birth

If at birth the rising sign is not aspected by the Moon, the father will not be nearby. If Sun occupies the 9th or 8th house:
- Movable sign → father in foreign country
- Fixed sign → father in own country
- Common sign → father returning to country

### Stanza 2: Father Absence

If Saturn in rising sign, or Mars in setting sign, or Moon between Mercury and Venus — father not near.

### Stanza 6: Legitimacy Verification

If Jupiter fails to aspect both the ascendant sign and the Moon, or the Moon in conjunction with the Sun, or if Moon be in conjunction with Sun accompanied by a malefic — the issue will be illegitimate.

### Stanza 8-9: Birth Location — Water

- Moon full and in Cancer, Mercury in rising sign, Jupiter in 4th → birth in a boat
- Watery signs rising and Moon in 7th → birth in a boat
- Watery sign rising with Moon in watery sign, or full Moon aspecting rising sign, or Moon in 10th/4th/Lagna → birth on banks of waters

### Stanza 10: Birth Location — Prison/Ditch

- Moon in rising sign, Saturn in 12th aspected by malefic → birth in prison
- Scorpio or Cancer rising, occupied by Saturn and aspected by Moon → birth in a ditch

### Stanza 12: Birth Location by Aspect

- Biped sign rising, Saturn in it aspected by Mars → cremation ground
- Aspected by Venus and Moon → beautiful agreeable spot
- Aspected by Jupiter → Agnihotra Sala (sacred fire place)
- Aspected by Sun → King's palace, temple or cow shed
- Aspected by Mercury → artist's working room

### Stanza 13: Birth Location — Sign Nature

- Movable rising sign/Navamsa → birth on roads
- Fixed → within a building
- Vargottama Navamsa → in mother's own house

### Stanza 16: Parental House

- Powerful paternal planet → birth in father's house
- Powerful maternal planet → birth in mother's house
- Three benefics in depression → at foot of walls/trees
- Three planets in single sign fail to aspect Lagna and Moon → in forest

### Stanza 17: Delivery Conditions

- Moon in Navamsa of Saturn or 4th from Lagna, or aspected by Saturn, or in watery Navamsa, or same house as Saturn → birth in dark spot
- Three+ planets in Neecha signs → birth on bare ground
- Direction of birth (head/leg first) determined by how the rising sign reaches the horizon
- Malefics in 4th or 7th from Moon → mother suffers much from travail

### Stanza 18: Delivery Room — Oil Lamp Method (**BTR-Useful**)

The oil (quantity) shall be determined from the Moon:
- Moon at beginning of sign → oil full
- Moon at middle → oil half
- Moon at end → no oil

The wick from the rising sign:
- Beginning of sign rising → wick long and unburnt
- Middle → half burnt
- End → nearly wholly burnt

The fixed/movable nature of the lamp from sign of Sun:
- Fixed sign → lamp fixed
- Movable → carried in hand
- Both → swinging lamp

### Stanza 19: House Description from Planets

- Saturn powerful → old rebuilt house
- Mars powerful → partly burnt house
- Moon powerful → newly built house
- Sun powerful → wood-built but weak
- Mercury → built by several builders
- Venus → beautiful new house with pictures
- Jupiter → strong built house

### Stanza 20: Delivery Room Direction

- Aries, Cancer, Libra, Scorpio, Aquarius ascendant → eastern portion
- Sagittari, Pisces, Gemini, Virgo → northern portion
- Taurus → western portion
- Capricorn, Leo → southern portion

### Stanza 23: Body Structure at Birth

The structure of the body shall be determined from the lord of the rising Navamsa or the most powerful planet. The color from the Navamsa occupied by the Moon. The size of body parts follow the signs representing them, beginning from the rising sign (head).

# CHAPTER XXVI: On Lost Horoscopes — THE BTR CHAPTER

## Complete BTR System for Discovering Birth Time from Prasna (Query)

This is the single most important chapter in all of classical Jyotisha for Birth Time Rectification.

### Stanza 1: Determine Ayana (Six-Month Period)

When a person is ignorant of the time of his conception or birth, the time of birth shall, when duly questioned about, be determined from the **Prasna Lagna** (the rising sign at the time of query).

- If the 1st half of the rising sign is cut by the horizon → month of birth in **Uttarayana** (Capricorn to Gemini)
- If the 2nd half is cut by the horizon → month in **Dakshinayana** (Cancer to Sagittari)

### Stanza 2: Determine Jupiter's Sign, Year, and Ritu

If at the time of query the **first Drekkana** of a sign rises:
- Sign occupied by Jupiter at birth = Prasna Lagna itself

If the **2nd Drekkana** rises:
- Jupiter's birth sign = 5th sign from Prasna Lagna

If the **3rd Drekkana** rises:
- Jupiter's birth sign = 9th sign from Prasna Lagna

**Year of birth**: Determined from appearance of person. As Jupiter takes 12 years to go around the heavens, age = number of Jupiter circuits × 12 + signs from birth sign to query sign.

**Alternative**: If belly is touched at time of query, Jupiter is in 5th round. Age = 4 × 12 = 48 + years to move from birth sign to query sign.

**Ritu of birth**: Determined by planet in Prasna Lagna or its Drekkana rising:
- Sun → Greeshma (Gemini-Cancer)
- Venus → Vasanta (Aries-Taurus)
- Mars → Greeshma (Gemini-Cancer)
- Moon → Varsha (Leo-Virgo)
- Mercury → Sarat (Libra-Scorpio)
- Jupiter → Hemanta (Sagittari-Capricorn)
- Saturn → Sisira (Aquarius-Pisces)

### Stanza 3: Ritu/Month/Date Determination

If Ritu does not agree with Ayana, take the alternative planet's Ritu:
- Moon ↔ Venus are alternatives
- Mercury ↔ Mars are alternatives
- Jupiter ↔ Saturn are alternatives

**Month of birth**:
- 1st half of rising Drekkana → first month of Ritu
- 2nd half → second month of Ritu

**Date of birth**: Determined from portion of half-Drekkana risen above horizon. A Drekkana = 10°, half = 5°, a 30th = 10 minutes. Every 10 minutes of rising half-Drekkana represents 1 day.

### Stanza 4: Tithi and Ghatika

The number of degrees passed over by the Sun in any sign = the lunar day (Tithi) at birth.

- Prasna Lagna is a day sign → birth occurred at night
- Prasna Lagna is a night sign → birth occurred by day

> **Note**: The length of day/night of birth is represented by the rising sign. Using the Lagna Sphuta and Rule of Three, we arrive at the exact time of birth from sun-rise or sun-set.

### Stanza 5: Moon's Sign at Birth from Query

The sign occupied by the Moon at birth will be either:
- The Prasna Lagna, or
- The 5th house from it, or
- The 9th house from it

Whichever is most powerful. If difficult to determine, use the part of body of Kalapurusha touched by the person at time of query, or other indications (creatures seen, sounds heard).

### Stanza 6: Moon's Sign — Another Method

The sign occupied by the Moon at birth will be as many signs removed from the Moon at query as the Moon is removed from the rising sign at query.

> **Example**: Scorpio rising, Moon in Pisces at query. From Scorpio to Pisces = 5 signs. 5th from Pisces = Cancer. Cancer = Moon's sign at birth.

If rising sign is Pisces → Pisces = Moon's sign at birth.

### Stanza 7: Rising Sign at Birth

The rising sign at birth = the sign of the lord of the rising Navamsa at time of query.

**OR**: The rising sign at birth = as many signs removed from Prasna Lagna as the Drekkana of the Sun is removed from the rising Drekkana at query.

> **Example**: 2nd Drekkana of Gemini rising, Sun in 3rd Drekkana of Sagittari. Number of Drekkanas between = 20. 20th sign from Gemini = Capricorn (20−12=8th from Gemini).

### Stanza 8: Shadow Method (Sanku) for Determining Lagna

Multiply the Sphuta (Longitude) of the planet (or most powerful planet) in the rising sign by the number of inches in the shadow length of a 12-inch Sanku (vertical rod) placed in the Sun. Divide by 12. The remainder gives the rising sign at birth.

> **Example**: Longitude = 4s 10° 20', shadow = 4.5 inches. Product = 19s 16° 30'. Divide by 12 → remainder = 7s 16° 30'. Lagna at birth = 8th sign from Aries = Scorpio.

**From posture of querent**:
- Seated → 7th house from rising sign = Lagna at birth
- Lying → 4th house from rising = Lagna
- Standing → 10th house = Lagna
- Rising from seat → rising sign itself = Lagna

### Stanzas 9-10: Nakshatra Determination at Birth

If rising sign is Taurus or Leo → multiply Lagna Sphuta by 10
If Gemini or Scorpio → multiply by 8
If Aries or Libra → multiply by 7
If Virgo or Capricorn → multiply by 5
If remaining signs → multiply by the number of the sign from Aries

For planetary factors:
- Jupiter → multiply by 10
- Mars → multiply by 8
- Venus → multiply by 7
- Remaining planets → multiply by 5

If several planets occupy rising sign, multiply by factor for each, add products.

Multiply sum by 7. Add 9 if movable sign, subtract 9 if common sign, leave as-is if fixed sign. Divide by 27 → remainder = Nakshatra at birth (counted from Aswini).

### Stanza 11-14: Complete Time Determination

Multiply the sum (from stanza 9) by **10, 8, 7, 5** respectively, add/subtract 9 as necessary:

**Product × 10** → Divide by 120 → age; by 6 → Ritu from Sisira; by 2 → first/second month of Ritu

**Product × 8** → Divide by 2 → fortnight (Paksha); by 15 → lunar day (Tithi)

**Product × 7** → Divide by 2 → day/night (remainder 1 = day, >1 = night); by 27 → Nakshatra from Aswini

**Product × 5** → Divide by number of Ghatikas in day/night → Ghatika from sunrise/sunset; by 12 → rising sign; by 2 → Hora; by 9 → rising Navamsa

### Stanza 15: Name Method

Multiply by two the number of Matras in a person's name. Add the length of shadow (of 12-inch Sanku in inches). Divide by 27. Remainder = Nakshatra at birth from Dhanishta.

> **Note on Matras**: Short vowel = 1 Matra, long vowel = 2 Matras, consonant = 1/2 Matra.

### Stanza 16: Direction (Dik) Method

| Direction | Number |
|-----------|--------|
| East | 2 |
| South-East | 3 |
| South | 14 |
| South-West | 10 |
| West | — |
| North-West | — |
| North | — |
| North-East | — |

Multiply by 15 the number for the direction the querent faces, add the number of persons facing that direction, divide by 27. Remainder = Nakshatra from Dhanishta.

### Stanza 17: Conclusion

"Thus has been stated by me various processes for the construction of a lost horoscope. Intelligent pupils shall study them carefully and use them."

# BTR Summary: Varahamihira's Complete Process

## Step 1: Capture Prasna Lagna
Record the exact rising sign, degree, and minute at the time the person asks about their birth time.

## Step 2: Determine Macro Timeframe
- Use Stanza 1 to determine Uttarayana/Dakshinayana (half-year)
- Use Drekkana of Prasna Lagna + Jupiter's position to determine Ritu (season)

## Step 3: Determine Month and Date
- Use half-Drekkana method (Stanza 3): each 10' of rising Drekkana = 1 day
- Cross-check with planetary indicators for Ritu

## Step 4: Determine Tithi
- Sun's degree in its sign = Tithi number (Stanza 4)

## Step 5: Reconstruct Birth Chart
- Use Jupiter's position from Drekkanas (Stanza 2) for birth Lagna
- Use Moon position from Prasna (Stanzas 5-6)
- Use Sanku shadow method (Stanza 8) for Lagna cross-verification
- Use Nakshatra computation (Stanzas 9-10) for finer birth details

## Step 6: Ghatika (Time) Determination
- From product × 5 method (Stanza 14): divide by Ghatikas in day/night
- Use proportion of rising sign that has risen above horizon

## Step 7: Verification
- Cross-check Nakshatra from multiple methods (name, direction, computation)
- Verify postural indications (standing/seated/lying)
- Verify against known event planetary periods

# Application Notes for Modern BTR

The Prasna-based BTR system of Chapter XXVI is directly applicable to modern BTR:

1. **The Prasna moment** is the time the BTR query is made — capture exact Lagna
2. **Jupiter's position** at birth relative to Prasna Lagna is the key anchor point
3. **Multiple cross-verification methods** are provided intentionally — the astrologer should use at least 3 methods and find convergence
4. The **Name method** (Stanza 15) is especially useful when no query chart is available
5. The **Shadow/Sanku method** (Stanza 8) can be replaced with modern methods but the principle of using an external measure to determine Lagna remains
6. The Dwadasamsa method in Chapter IV provides a way to work backward from known birth time to conception or forward from known/estimated conception to birth
7. Birth location and circumstance details (Chapter V) serve as **verification markers** — e.g., a birth location contradicting computed planetary positions indicates time error
