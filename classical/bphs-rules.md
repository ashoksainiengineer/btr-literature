## Chapter 28: Ishta & Kashta Balas (Shadbala / Planetary Strength)

### BTR Relevance

In BTR, planets connected to major life events through Dasha/Bhukti should have adequate Shadbala. A planet with deficient Shadbala cannot deliver its promised effects, regardless of what the cuspal sub-lord signifies.

### Minimum Shadbala Requirements

| Planet | Required Shadbala (Rupas) |
|--------|--------------------------|
| Sun | 6.5 Rupas |
| Moon | 6.0 Rupas |
| Mars | 5.0 Rupas |
| Mercury | 7.0 Rupas |
| Jupiter | 6.5 Rupas |
| Venus | 5.5 Rupas |
| Saturn | 5.0 Rupas |

> **Rule**: "If a planet has the required Shadbala, it will prove favourable to the native by virtue of its strength. However, Saturn's extreme strength will give long life as well as miseries."

### Minimum Constituent Bala Requirements

Planets are grouped into three categories for minimum constituent strengths (in Virupas):

| Bala Component | Group A (Jup, Merc, Sun) | Group B (Moon, Venus) | Group C (Mars, Saturn) |
|----------------|--------------------------|----------------------|------------------------|
| Sthana Bala | 165 | 133 | 96 |
| Dig Bala | 35 | 50 | 30 |
| Kala Bala | 50 | 30 | 40 |
| Cheshta Bala | 112 | 100 | 67 |
| Ayana Bala | 30 | 40 | 20 |

> **Rule**: "Even if the Shadbala Pinda is short of the sums stipulated, certain minimum requirements of the constituent strengths will make the planet considerably favourable."

### BTR Application

When testing candidate birth times:
1. Identify the Dasha/Bhukti lord(s) running during each major life event
2. Verify the Dasha lord has minimum required Shadbala (per table above)
3. If a Dasha lord connected to a major event has severely deficient Shadbala, the candidate time is questionable
4. Cross-verify with constituent Bala minimums for deeper analysis

## Chapter 29: Bhava Padas (Arudhas)

### BTR Relevance

The Arudha (Pada) system is critical for BTR because:
- **Dara Pada** (7th Arudha) verifies marriage/partnership events
- **Karma Pada** (10th Arudha) verifies career events
- **Putra Pada** (5th Arudha) verifies children events
- **Dhana Pada** (2nd Arudha) verifies financial events
- **Roga Pada** (6th Arudha) verifies health events
- **Marana Pada** (8th Arudha) verifies death/transformation events

### The 12 Arudhas

| # | Name | Also Called | Arudha of |
|---|------|-------------|-----------|
| 1 | Lagna Pada | Pada | 1st house |
| 2 | Dhana Pada | — | 2nd house |
| 3 | Vikrama Pada | Bhratru Pada | 3rd house |
| 4 | Matru Pada | Sukha Pada | 4th house |
| 5 | Mantra Pada | Putra Pada | 5th house |
| 6 | Roga Pada | Satru Pada | 6th house |
| 7 | Dara Pada | Kalatra Pada | 7th house |
| 8 | Marana Pada | — | 8th house |
| 9 | Pitru Pada | — | 9th house |
| 10 | Karma Pada | — | 10th house |
| 11 | Labha Pada | — | 11th house |
| 12 | Vyaya Pada | — | 12th house |

### Arudha Calculation Rule

> "In calculating the Arudha Pada for a house, note the number of signs its lord moved away from that particular sign. Count so many signs from the lord in question. The sign so arrived will be the Arudha for the sign under consideration."

**Formula**: Arudha = (Lord's position from house) signs counted from lord's position

### Special Exceptions (Slokas 4-5)

1. **Same house exception**: If Arudha falls in the same house, count 10 signs therefrom as actual Pada
2. **7th house exception**: If Arudha falls in the 7th from original house, count 4th from original house as actual Pada
3. **Lord in 4th exception**: If lord of a bhava is in the 4th from that bhava, the house occupied by the lord itself IS the Arudha (no counting needed)

### Key Arudha Interpretations for BTR

**From Lagna Pada (Slokas 23-37):**
- Jupiter, Venus and Moon in 7th from Lagna Pada → great wealth
- Exalted planet in 7th from Lagna Pada → affluence and fame
- Mercury in 2nd from Arudha Lagna → lordship over the country
- Venus in 2nd from Lagna Pada → poet or orator

**Dara Pada (7th Arudha) for Marriage:**
- Dara Pada in angle/trine from Lagna Pada + both strong → rich and famous
- Dara Pada in 6th/8th/12th from Lagna Pada → poverty
- Lagna Pada and Dara Pada mutually angular/trinal → amity between couple
- Lagna Pada and Dara Pada in 6th/8th/12th → mutual enmity

**Karma Pada (10th Arudha):**
- When evaluating career events, Karma Pada position relative to Lagna Pada provides additional verification alongside 10th CSL

## Chapter 30: Upa Pada (Gauna Pada)

### BTR Relevance

Upa Pada is **critical for marriage BTR verification** — it specifically governs happiness from spouse and progeny.

### Calculation

> "Upa Pada is calculated for the bhava following the natal ascendant. This Upa Pada is also called Gauna Pada."

The commentator interprets this as:
- **Odd sign ascending**: Upa Pada = Pada of the 12th house
- **Even sign ascending**: Upa Pada = Pada of the 2nd house

### Rules for Spouse/Progeny Happiness

1. **Upa Pada conjunct/aspected by benefic** → full happiness from progeny and spouse ✓
2. **Upa Pada in malefic sign or aspected by/conjunct malefic** → ascetic, may go without spouse
3. **If (2) but with benefic aspect/conjunction** → spouse deprivation averted
4. **Sun is NOT a malefic if exalted or in friendly sign**; he IS malefic if debilitated or in enemy's sign

### 2nd from Upa Pada Rules

1. **2nd from Upa Pada = benefic sign or aspected by/conjunct benefic** → same good results for wife and sons
2. **Planet in 2nd from Upa Pada in debilitation sign/amsa or conjunct debilitated/malefic planet** → destruction of wife
3. **Planet in 2nd from Upa Pada in exaltation** → many charming and virtuous wives
4. **Gemini as 2nd from Upa Pada** → many wives
5. **2nd from Upa Pada or Uparudha occupied by own lord or lord in other own house** → death of wife at advanced age

### Wife from Upa Pada (Slokas 13-15)

1. **7th lord or Venus in own house** → loss of wife only at later stage
2. **Lord of Upapada or constant significator of wife in exaltation** → wife from noble family
3. **Lord of Upapada or constant significator of wife debilitated** → reverse
4. **2nd from Upapada related to benefic** → wife beautiful, fortunate, virtuous

## Chapter 31: Argala (Planetary Intervention)

### BTR Relevance

Argala rules help understand why certain planetary combinations produce or block events at specific times.

### Formation Rules

| Position from House/Planet | Function |
|---------------------------|----------|
| 4th, 2nd, 11th | Cause Argala (intervention) |
| 10th, 12th, 3rd | Obstruct Argala |
| 5th | Also an Argala place |
| 9th | Counteracts Argala from 5th |

If the Argala-causing planet is stronger than the obstructing one → Argala prevails
If more Argalas than obstructing planets → Argala prevails
3+ malefics in 3rd → Vipareeta Argala (harmless, very favourable)

**Nodes**: Due to retrograde motion, count Argalas and obstructions in reverse manner.

**Degree of effect**: One planet = limited effects; two = medium; more than two = excellent

> "The Argala effects will be derived in the Dasa periods of the Rasi or planet concerned."

## Chapter 32: Karakatwas (Planetary Significations)

### BTR Relevance

Karakatwas (both Chara and constant) are essential for mapping planets to life events during BTR. When a planet is Dasha lord during an event, its Karakatwa must match the event type.

### Chara Karakas (Variable Significators)

Based on longitude (highest to lowest degrees in a sign, excluding sign count):

| Karaka | Order | Signification |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| Atmakaraka | 1st (highest degrees) | Self, soul, overall destiny |
| Amatyakaraka | 2nd | Career, ministers, advisers |
| Bhratrukaraka | 3rd | Siblings |
| Matrukaraka | 4th | Mother |
| Pitrukaraka | 5th | Father |
| Putrakaraka | 6th | Children |
| Gnatikaraka | 7th | Relatives, clan |
| Dara/Streekaraka | 8th (lowest degrees) | Spouse, marriage |

### Constant Significators (Sthira Karakas)

| Planet | Constant Karakatwa |
|--------|-------------------|
| Sun (or stronger of Sun/Venus) | Father |
| Moon (or stronger of Moon/Mars) | Mother |
| Mars | Sister, brother-in-law, younger brother, mother |
| Mercury | Maternal relatives |
| Jupiter | Paternal grandfather, sons |
| Venus | Husband (for female), wife (for male) |
| Saturn | Sons |
| Ketu | Wife, father, mother, parents-in-law, maternal grandfather |

### House-Based Significations

| Planet | House from Planet | Signifies |
|--------|------------------|-----------|
| Sun | 9th | Father |
| Moon | 4th | Mother |
| Mars | 3rd | Brothers |
| Mercury | 6th | Maternal uncle |
| Jupiter | 5th | Sons |
| Venus | 7th | Wife |
| Saturn | 8th | Death, longevity |

### BTR Application

When verifying events against candidate birth times:
- The Dasha lord running at event time should have Karakatwa connection to that event
- e.g., for childbirth event → Dasha lord should be Jupiter (Putrakaraka) or Saturn (constant significator for sons)
- For marriage → Dasha lord should be Venus (Dara/Streekaraka)
- Cross-reference with both Chara and constant Karakas for full picture

## Chapter 34: Yoga Karakas (House Lordship Effects per Ascendant)

### BTR Relevance — CRITICAL

This is the **most important chapter for BTR** because:
- It defines which planets are benefic/malefic/Yogakaraka for EACH ascendant
- BTR candidate testing must consider that a planet's role changes based on the ascendant
- If candidate time A gives Jupiter as Yogakaraka but the native experienced Jupiter-Dasha negatively, that time is wrong
- If candidate time B gives Jupiter as malefic, it matches the native's life experience

### General Rules for Lordships

1. **Kendradhipatya Dosha**: Natural benefics owning angles become tainted (not fully benefic)
2. **Malefic owning angle**: Does NOT become auspicious by angular lordship alone; must also own a trine to be Yogakaraka
3. **Trinal lords** (5th, 9th): Primary wealth-givers
4. **7th and 10th lords**: Primary happiness-givers
5. **3rd, 6th, 11th lords**: Classified as evil
6. **12th, 2nd, 8th lords**: Act according to association
7. **Moolatrikona house**: Has prime importance over other owned house
8. **Hierarchy**: 10th lord > 9th lord > (others) ... 11th lord most evil, 8th lord most evil in second group

### Per-Ascendant Planet Classification

| Ascendant | Benefics | Malefics | Yogakarakas | Killers (Marakas) |
|-----------|----------|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Aries** | Jupiter, Sun | Saturn, Mercury, Venus | — | Venus (independent); Saturn, Mercury if associated |
| **Taurus** | Saturn, Sun (Merc somewhat) | Jupiter, Venus, Moon | Saturn | Jupiter, Moon, Venus group |
| **Gemini** | Venus (only) | Mars, Jupiter, Sun | — | Moon (dependent on association) |
| **Cancer** | Mars, Jupiter, Moon | Venus, Mercury | Mars | Saturn, Sun |
| **Leo** | Mars, Jupiter, Sun | Mercury, Venus, Saturn | Mars | Saturn, Moon |
| **Virgo** | Mercury, Venus | Mars, Jupiter, Moon | Venus (with Mercury) | Venus (also) |
| **Libra** | Saturn, Mercury | Jupiter, Sun, Mars | Moon + Mercury | Mars; Jupiter if associated |
| **Scorpio** | Jupiter, Moon | Venus, Mercury, Saturn | Sun + Moon | Venus + other malefics |
| **Sagittarius** | Mars, Sun, Mercury | Venus (only) | Sun + Mercury | Saturn; Venus (if related to Saturn) |
| **Capricorn** | Venus, Mercury | Mars, Jupiter, Moon | Venus | Mars + other malefics |
| **Aquarius** | Venus, Saturn | Jupiter, Moon, Mars | Venus | Jupiter, Sun, Mars |
| **Pisces** | Mars, Moon, Jupiter | Saturn, Venus, Sun, Mercury | Mars + Jupiter | Saturn, Mercury; Mars (only if instigated) |

### BTR Application

For each candidate birth time:
1. Determine the ascendant
2. Look up the planet classification for that ascendant
3. For each major life event, verify that the Dasha/Bhukti lord at that time belongs to the appropriate category:
   - Positive events (marriage, promotion, childbirth) → Dasha lord should be benefic/Yogakaraka OR significator of the relevant house
   - Negative events (job loss, death, illness) → Dasha lord may be malefic/killer OR significator of denial houses
4. **Cross-check**: If a benefic planet's Dasha produced consistently negative results, the candidate ascendant is likely wrong

### Yoga-Karaka Notes (Ch 34, Slokas 30+)

> "Planets become Yogakarakas if they are in mutual angles identical with own signs, exaltation or friendly sign."

**Rule**: Even a person of mean birth will become a king with such Yogakarakas. Effects should be declared considering the number of such planets and the order the native belongs to.

## Chapter 39: Raja Yogas

### BTR Relevance

Raja Yogas determine the native's status and power level. In BTR, a candidate time that produces appropriate Raja Yogas matching the native's actual life status is more likely correct.

### Karakamsa-Based Raja Yogas

Raja Yogas are evaluated from TWO reference points:
1. **Karakamsa Lagna** (Navamsa occupied by Atmakaraka)
2. **Natal Lagna**

**Key Players**:
- Atmakaraka + Putrakaraka (from Chara Karaka scheme)
- Natal Ascendant Lord + 5th Lord

> "The effects due to such association will be full, or a half or a quarter according to their strengths."

### BTR Application

For public figures or high-status individuals:
- The 5th lord has been given **very high importance** by the sage — equated with or even exceeding the 9th lord
- Both Karakamsa and natal Lagna must produce coherent Raja Yoga indications
- If a candidate time produces strong Raja Yogas but the native's life shows no such status, that time is suspect — and vice versa

# Chapter 22 — Effects of the Tenth & Eleventh Houses

## Combinations for Fame (Yogas)

**Source:** BPHS Ch.22, Verses 19-21
**Rule:**
- Fame is conferred if the **Moon is in the 10th** while the **10th lord is in a trine from the 10th** and the **ascendant lord is in the ascendant's angle** (kendra).
- Similar effects if the **11th lord is in the 10th** while the **10th lord is strong** and in **aspect to Jupiter**.
- Fame will come if the **10th lord is in the 9th** as the **ascendant lord is in the 10th** and the **Moon is in the 5th** from the ascendant.

**Yoga Type**: Raja Yoga / Fame Yoga
**Houses/Planets**: 10th house, 10th lord, 11th lord, Ascendant lord, Moon, Jupiter

## Chapter 22 — Effects of the 11th House (Gains/Aya)

**Source:** BPHS Ch.22, Verses 1-11

**Verse 2:** Should the **11th lord be in the 11th itself** or be in an **angle or trine**, there will be many gains. If the **11th lord is exalted**, though in **combustion**, there will still be many gains.

> **Key Dignity Rule:** Exaltation overcomes combustion for the 11th lord — gains are not destroyed even if the planet is combust, provided it is exalted.

**Verse 3:** If the **11th lord is in the 2nd** while the **2nd lord is in an angle** along with **Jupiter**, the gains will be great.

**Verse 4:** If the **11th lord is in the 3rd** while the **11th is occupied by a benefic**, the native will gain **2000 Nishkas** in his **36th year**.

**Verse 5:** If the **11th lord is conjunct a benefic** in an **angle or trine**, the native will acquire **500 Nishkas** in his **40th year**.

**Verse 6:** The native will own **6000 Nishkas** if the **11th is occupied by Jupiter** while the **2nd and 9th** are respectively taken by the **Moon and Venus**.

**Verse 7:** Should **Jupiter, Mercury, and the Moon** be in the **11th from the 11th** (i.e., 9th from the ascendant), the native will be endowed with **wealth, grains, fortunes, diamonds, ornaments**, etc.

**Verse 8:** One will gain **1000 Nishkas** in his **33rd year** if the **11th lord is in the ascendant** and the **ascendant lord is in the 11th**.

**Verse 9:** If the **11th lord is in the 2nd** as the **2nd lord is in the 11th**, one will amass **abundant fortunes after marriage**.

**Verse 10:** If the **11th lord is in the 3rd** as the **3rd lord is in the 11th**, one will gain **wealth through co-born** and be endowed with **excellent ornaments**.

**Verse 11:** There will be **no gains** in spite of numerous efforts if the **11th lord is in fall**, in **combustion**, or be in the **6th/8th/12th with a malefic**.

> **Key Denial Rule:** The 11th lord in debilitation, combustion, or dusthana (6/8/12) with a malefic denies gains entirely.

**Houses/Planets**: 11th lord, 2nd lord, 3rd lord, Jupiter, Mercury, Moon, Venus; kendra/trikona houses
**Dasha Pattern**: Gains manifest in specific years (33rd, 36th, 40th) corresponding to planetary periods

# Chapter 23 — Effects of the Twelfth House (Vyaya)

**Source:** BPHS Ch.23, Verses 1-14

**Verses 1-4:**
- There will be **expenses on good accounts** if the **12th lord is with a benefic**, or be in **own house**, or be **exalted**, or if a **benefic occupies the 12th**.
- One will own **beautiful houses and beds** and be endowed with superior scented articles and pleasures if the **Moon** happens to be the **12th lord** and be **exalted** or be in **own sign/Navamsa** or in the **11th/9th/5th** in Rasi/Navamsa.
- The said native will live with **rich clothes, ornaments**, be **learned and lordly**.

**Verses 5-6:**
- If the **12th lord is in the 6th/8th** or be in **enemy's Navamsa**, **debilitation Navamsa**, or in **8th in Navamsa**, one will be **devoid of happiness from wife**, be **troubled by expenses** and deprived of general happiness.
- If the **12th lord is in an angle/trine**, the native will **beget a spouse**.

> **Key Dignity Rule:** Navamsa dignity of the 12th lord matters — enemy's Navamsa or debilitation Navamsa causes marital distress and financial waste.

**Verse 8:** Planets in the **visible half of the zodiac** (180° from ascendant backwards) will give **explicit results**; those in the **invisible half** confer **secret results**.

**Verse 9:** If **Rahu is in the 12th** along with **Mars, Saturn, and the Sun**, the native will go to hell. Similar effect if the **12th lord is with the Sun**.

**Verse 10:** If there is a **benefic in the 12th** while its **lord is exalted** or is **conjunct or aspected by a benefic**, one will attain **final emancipation (moksha)**.

**Verse 11:** One will **wander from country to country** if the **12th lord and 12th house are with malefics** and be in aspect to malefics.

# Chapter 26 — Evaluation of Planetary Aspects (Drishti)

**Source:** BPHS Ch.26, Verses 1-10

## Types of Aspects
- **Sign-based** (rasi drishti) and **Planetary** (graha drishti)
- **All planets aspect the 7th fully**
- **Special aspects:** Saturn → 3rd & 10th; Jupiter → 5th & 9th; Mars → 4th & 8th
- Aspect intensity: 3rd/10th = 1/4, 5th/9th = 1/2, 4th/8th = 3/4, 7th = full

## Aspectual Evaluation (Drishti Kona)

**Computation:** Deduct aspected from aspecting planet's longitude. If sum > 6 signs, deduct from 10 signs. Convert to degrees, divide by 2 = Drishti Kona.

**Tiered Calculation:**
- >5 signs: Multiply degrees × 2
- >4 signs: Deduct from 5 signs (150°) → result = Drishti value
- >3 signs: Deduct from 4 signs (120°), halve + 30
- >2 signs: Add 15 to degrees
- >1 sign: Divide degrees by 2

## Saturn's Special Aspect Consideration (V.9-10)
- If Saturn is aspecting: Find difference. If >1 sign, multiply degrees × 2.
- If sum > 9 signs, degrees to elapse are doubled.

> **Key Dignity Rule:** Aspects have graded strength — not all aspects are equal.
