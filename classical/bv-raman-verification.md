## 1. The 3 BTR Verification Rules

### Rule 1: Nakshatra Verification (Birth Constellation Check)

> *"Multiply the time of birth in vighatis by 4 and divide the product by 9. The remainder must give the ruling constellation when counted from Aswini, Makha or Moola."* — B.V. Raman

**Formula:**
```
(Vighatis × 4) ÷ 9 → remainder → count from Aswini/Makha/Moola
```

**Steps:**
1. Convert elapsed time from sunrise to birth into vighatis (1 ghati = 24 min, 1 vighati = 24 sec)
2. Total vighatis = (ghatis × 60) + vighatis
3. Multiply total vighatis by 4
4. Divide by 9 — the **remainder** (not quotient) determines the nakshatra
5. Count remainder from Aswini (Group 1: Aswini→Aslesha), Makha (Group 2: Makha→Jyeshta), or Moola (Group 3: Moola→Revati) — whichever group contains the known Janma Nakshatra

**Worked example:** Birth 7:42:44 PM, Sunrise 6:25:32 AM → 33 ghatis 53 vighatis → 2033 vighatis × 4 = 8132 ÷ 9 = remainder 5 → 5th from Aswini = Mrigasira ✓

**Rectification when rule fails:** Work backwards — determine what vighati value produces the correct remainder for the known nakshatra, adjust birth time accordingly.

**Three Nakshatra Groups:**

| Remainder | From Aswini (G1) | From Makha (G2) | From Moola (G3) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Aswini | Makha | Moola |
| 2 | Bharani | Pubba | Poorvashadha |
| 3 | Krittika | Uttara | Uttarashadha |
| 4 | Rohini | Hasta | Sravana |
| 5 | Mrigasira | Chitta | Dhanishta |
| 6 | Aridra | Swati | Satabhisha |
| 7 | Punarvasu | Visakha | Poorvabhadra |
| 8 | Pushyami | Anuradha | Uttarabhadra |
| 9 (or 0) | Aslesha | Jyeshta | Revati |

### Rule 2: Lagna Verification (Ascendant Check)

> *"Multiply the number of ghatis from birth by 6 and add the longitude of the Sun (the number of degrees passed in the sign). Divide the sum by 30. The quotient plus 1 counted from the Sun's sign will give the rising sign."* — B.V. Raman

**Formula (Method A — Ghatis):**
```
(Ghatis × 6 + Sun° in sign) ÷ 30 → quotient + 1 from Sun's sign = Ascendant
```

**Formula (Method B — Hours):**
```
(Hours from sunrise × 15 + Sun° in sign) ÷ 30 → quotient + 1 from Sun's sign = Ascendant
```

**Steps:**
1. Compute ghatis from sunrise to birth (same elapsed time as Rule 1)
2. Multiply ghatis by 6 (or hours by 15)
3. Add the Sun's longitude in its current sign (degrees only)
4. Divide by 30 → keep the **quotient**
5. Add 1 to the quotient
6. Count that many signs from the Sun's current sign → should equal Ascendant

**Worked example:** 33.88 ghatis × 6 = 203.28 + 24° (Sun in Cancer) = 227.28 ÷ 30 = quotient 7 → 7+1=8 signs from Cancer = Aquarius ✓

**Caveat:** These are approximate checks to verify the **sign**, not to calculate exact degrees.

### Rule 3: Trinal Relationship Check

> *"The 5th or the 9th sign from the house occupied by the lord of the sign in which the Moon is placed becomes the Janma Lagna or the ascendant. The 7th from the sign occupied by the lord of the Moon's sign or the 5th or the 9th from the similar sign and in some cases the sign where the Moon is at radix itself becomes the ascendant."* — B.V. Raman

**Procedure:**
1. Identify the Moon's sign in the birth chart
2. Find the lord of that sign and note which sign the lord occupies
3. The Ascendant should be one of:
   - **Primary:** 5th or 9th sign from the lord's position (Dharma Trikona)
   - **Secondary:** 7th sign from the Moon's sign lord
   - **Tertiary:** 5th or 9th from that 7th sign
   - **Special case:** The Moon's own sign (Moon in Lagna configuration)

**Astrological rationale:** Houses 1, 5, 9 form the Dharma Trikona — the Ascendant lord and Moon's sign lord should be in a harmonious relationship.

**Usage:** Less mechanical than Rules 1 & 2. Allows multiple valid Ascendant signs. Best used as a filtering tool — failure strongly suggests error, but consistency is not absolute proof.

## 2. The Prenatal Epoch Theory

> *"According to Varahamihira, there is an intimate connection existing between the planetary positions at conception and those at birth. Based on this, the Westerners have developed their so-called Prenatal Epoch Theory. The Moon is the source of impregnation."* — B.V. Raman

### Core Principle
The Moon at conception occupies the degree of the Ascendant at birth, or vice versa. The Dwadasamsa (D-12) of the Moon at conception provides a mathematical link to the birth moment.

### Varahamihira's Rule (Brihat Jataka IV.26)
> *"Find the number of Dwadasamsas occupied by the Moon at the time of conception. Note the zodiacal sign whose name the Dwadasamsa bears. Count from the next sign as many signs as the number of Dwadasamsas by which the Moon may have advanced in any particular sign. When the Moon comes to such last sign, the birth of the child will occur."*

### Dwadasamsa Reference
Each sign of 30° is divided into 12 Dwadasamsas of 2°30' each. The 1st Dwadasamsa bears the name of the sign itself, the 2nd takes the next sign, etc.

## 3. Hindu Time Units Reference

| Hindu Unit | Equivalent | Relation to Clock Time |
|---|---|---|
| Ghati (Nadika) | 60 Vighatis | 24 minutes |
| Vighati (Pala) | 60 Prativighatis | 24 seconds |
| Prativighati | — | 0.4 seconds |
| 1 Day (sunrise to sunrise) | 60 Ghatis | 24 hours |
| 1 Hour | 2.5 Ghatis | 60 minutes |

**Key conversions:**
- Hours → Ghatis: Ghatis = Hours × 2.5
- Ghatis → Hours: Hours = Ghatis × 0.4
- Minutes → Vighatis: Vighatis = Minutes × 2.5
- Total Vighatis: (Ghatis × 60) + Vighatis

**Critical:** Day begins at **sunrise**, not midnight. Births before sunrise use previous day's sunrise.

## 4. Practical BTR Workflow (Raman's Method)

1. **Rule 1** (Nakshatra check) — confirm birth time consistent with birth star
2. **Rule 2** (Lagna check) — verify Ascendant sign
3. **Rule 3** (Trinal check) — confirm Lagna-Moon lord relationship
4. **Check Navamsa Lagna** — test stability within ±15 minute window
5. **Test Dasa transitions** — verify against known life turning points
6. **Verify with transits** — check Saturn returns, Jupiter transits, etc.
