**Author:** Kalyana Varma (King of Vyagrapada region, flourished between Varahamihira and Bhattotpala, ~6th-10th century CE)
**Editor:** V. Subrahmanya Sastri, B.A.
**Publisher:** Nirnaya Sagar Press, Bombay (3rd Edition, 1928)
**Source:** https://archive.org/details/dli.csl.7888 (Central Secretariat Library, Government of India)
**Retrieved:** 2026-05-24
**OCR:** ABBYY FineReader 11.0 (Extended OCR) — 144KB extracted text
**Total Chapters:** 56 (as per colophon of Kankanhalli manuscript); 54 chapters recovered in this edition (Ch 55-56 on Nashta Jataka missing)
**Language:** Sanskrit with English translation

## Overview

Saravali is a comprehensive classical treatise on Hindu astrology (Jyotisha) composed by King Kalyana Varma. It claims to elaborate on topics briefly covered in Varahamihira's Brihat Jataka and draws from Yavana (Greek/Hellenistic) sources, rejecting superfluous doctrines while incorporating essential wisdom. The work is heavily cited by later authorities: Bhattotpala (10th c.), Balabhadra (Horaratna), Vaidyanatha Dikshita (Jatakaparijata), and Mallinatha. Two missing chapters (55-56) are believed to have covered **Nashta Jataka** (Lost Horoscopy), making this one of the most important classical sources for BTR.

# CHAPTER 3: Rasi Description — Varga Rules for BTR

## Vargothamamsa (Ch 3, Sloka 13)
The first Navamsa of a Movable Rasi, the 5th in a Fixed Rasi and the 9th in a Common (Dual) Rasi are called Vargothamamsa. If natal Lagna is in such Vargothama Navamsa, the native becomes important. **BTR Application:** Vargothama Lagna is a verification criterion — if rectified birth time produces Vargothama Lagna, the time is more credible.

## Varga Calculation (Ch 3, Sloka 18)
Convert longitude into minutes of arc, multiply by the Varga figure, divide by 1800. Result reveals the required Varga. **BTR Application:** This is the mathematical foundation for all divisional chart calculations used in multi-varga BTR verification.

## Day/Night Signs (Ch 3, Sloka 24)
- **Night Signs (Prishtodaya — rising with hind part):** Gemini, Cancer, Capricorn, Aries, Taurus, Sagittarius
- **Day Signs (Sirshodaya — rising with head):** Leo, Libra, Scorpio, Aquarius, Pisces, Virgo
- **Ubhayodaya (both):** Pisces
- **BTR Application:** Day/night birth verification — Prishtodaya rising → night birth; Sirshodaya rising → day birth.

## Gandanta (Ch 3, Slokas 20-21)
Ending portions of Cancer, Scorpio and Pisces are Gandanta. One born in Gandanta will not survive (or loses mother, or ends dynasty). **BTR Application:** If birth time places Lagna in Gandanta degrees < 3°40' of these signs, verify against known longevity/maternal data.

# CHAPTER 8: Conception (Adhanadhyaya) — BTR Rules

## Menstruation Timing (Ch 8, Slokas 1-3)
As a result of Moon's transit in Anupachaya Rasis (1, 2, 4, 5, 7, 8, 9, 12) and in aspect of Mars, a female obtains menses. **BTR Application:** The conception window is astronomically determinable and constrains the birth window via the known gestation period.

## Sex Determination (Ch 8, Slokas 14-15)
If Lagna, Moon, Jupiter and the Sun are strong and in male Rasis and male Navamsas → male child. If in even Rasis and even Amsas → female child. **BTR Application:** Sex of child (known fact) must match the Nisheka/Adhana chart's indication, constraining the conception time.

## Twin Birth Rules (Ch 8, Sloka 16)
- Sun + Jupiter in Gemini or Sagittarius aspected by Mercury → two male children
- Venus + Mars + Moon in Pisces or Virgo aspected by Mercury → two female children

## Triplet Birth Rules (Ch 8, Slokas 23-26)
Seven specific Mercury-in-Common-Navamsa conditions for triplets (see full text). **BTR Application:** Multiple birth indications in Adhana chart constrain conception time.

## Time of Delivery (Ch 8, Slokas 44-45)
- Sun in Movable Sign at Nisheka → delivery in 10th month
- Sun in Fixed Sign → delivery in 11th month
- Sun in Dual Sign → delivery in 12th month
- Same rules apply to the Nisheka Lagna sign type

## Natal Moon from Adhana Moon (Ch 8, Slokas 46-48)
- **Badarayana's view:** Natal Ascendant in 7th from Adhana Lagna; Natal Moon in 7th from Adhana Moon
- **Kalyanavarma's accepted view:** Note the Dwadasamsa of Moon at Adhana. When Moon is so many signs away from Adhana position (in a month when delivery is possible), delivery occurs.
- **BTR Application:** Allows forward-computation of birth time from conception time.

## Day/Night Fraction for Delivery (Ch 8, Sloka 50)
According to Nisheka Lagna being day sign or night sign, delivery will be after so much fraction of night or day having passed. **BTR Application:** Constrains the time-of-day of birth.

## Conception-to-Birth Time Calculation (Ch 8, Slokas 51-52)
The difference between sunrise and birth (natal Ascendant), weekday, fortnight, Muhurta, month etc. should be guessed from the Adhana chart. These details must first be ascertained before predictions are issued. **BTR Application:** Explicit BTR directive — the classical texts establish that birth time must be verified before prediction.

# CHAPTER 9: Conditions at Birth — BTR Verification Rules

## Sirshodaya/Prishtodaya Delivery (Ch 9, Sloka 2)
- Sirshodaya Rasi at Lagna → head-first birth
- Prishtodaya Rasi → legs-first birth
- Ubhayodaya Rasi → hands-first position
- Benefic aspect → comfortable delivery; otherwise difficult
- **BTR Application:** Known delivery position can verify Lagna sign type.

## Delivery Place (Ch 9, Slokas 3-4)
- Dual Rasi/Navamsa → delivery on the way
- Fixed Rasi/Ansa → delivery in own place
- Same Navamsa Rasi → delivery in own house
- **BTR Application:** Known birthplace context can verify Lagna nature.

## Birth Attendants Count (Ch 9, Slokas 21-23)
Number of planets between Moon and Lagna → number of attendants. Planets in invisible half → inside attendants; visible half → outside attendants. **BTR Application:** If known, this can verify relative Moon-Lagna positions.

## Mother/Father Presence (Ch 9, Slokas 28-30)
- Ascendant without Moon aspect → father absent
- Sun in 9th in Movable Rasi → father in foreign place
- **BTR Application:** Known family circumstances at birth can verify the chart.

## Physical Appearance (Ch 9, Slokas 45-47)
Physical appearance corresponds to Navamsa Lagna or strongest planet or Lord of Navamsa occupied by Moon. Nature corresponds to Trimsamsa occupied by Sun at birth. **BTR Application:** Known physical traits can cross-verify the divisional chart positions.

# CHAPTER 10: Evils at Birth (Arishtadhyaya) — Longevity & Death BTR Rules

## Three Kinds of Evils (Ch 10, Sloka 3)
Preordained, accidental, and Yogaja (planetary combinations). The third kind is dealt with below.

## Key Longevity-Shortening Combinations
| Slokas | Combination | Effect |
|--------|------------|--------|
| 4 | Jupiter in 8th in Mars' house + aspected by Sun/Moon/Mars/Saturn | Death in 3 years |
| 5 | Saturn retrograde in Mars' house + Moon in 1st/8th/angle aspecting Mars | Lives 2 years |
| 6-7 | Saturn joins Sun + Moon at birth | Life span 9 years |
| 8-9 | Malefic in 8th owned by Venus + aspected by other malefics | Lives 1 year |
| 10-11 | Mercury in Cancer in 6th/8th + aspected by Moon | Life up to 4 |
| 12-13 | Birth star identical to Ketu's rising star | Lives 2 months |
| 14-15 | Asc in Nigala/Sarpa/Pakshi/Pasa decanate + malefic occupied | Lives up to 7 years |
| 16-17 | Malefics in Angles+Trines, benefics in 1st/8th/12th, sunrise birth | Dies at once |
| 93-97 | Jupiter + Venus in Angle, no malefics in Trines/8th | 108+ years |
| 98 | Weak Moon with malefics in 8th/7th/12th/1st/5th/9th unaspected by benefics | Immediate death |

## Balarishta Indicators (Ch 10, Slokas 39-44)
- Sun in 7th + Saturn/Mars in Ascendant
- Sun in Ascendant + Saturn/Mars in 7th
- Saturn/Mars joining Moon unaspected by benefics
- Malefics in Ascendant, 8th, 7th, 4th, 10th + decreasing Moon in 12th
- Moon, Sun, Saturn, Mars together in Ascendant/12th/9th/8th unaspected by Jupiter

## Timing Infant Death via Moon (Ch 10, Slokas 99-103)
When powerful infant mortal combinations exist, death occurs in first year if Moon with strength passes over natal position of another qualified planet, or its own Janma Rasi, or natal Ascendant (aspected by malefics).

## Fateful Degrees of the Moon (Ch 10, Slokas 111-114)
Moon capable of inflicting death if at these degrees at birth:
| Sign | Degree | Sign | Degree |
|------|--------|------|--------|
| Aquarius | 21 | Leo | 5 |
| Taurus | 9 | Scorpio | 23 |
| Aries | 8 | Cancer | 12 |
| Libra | 4 | Capricorn | 20 |
| Virgo | 1 | Sagittarius | 18 |
| Pisces | 10 | Gemini | 22 |

**BTR Application:** If native is alive and healthy, these Moon degrees should NOT be present at rectified birth time (unless infant death actually occurred).

## Jupiter's Death Timing (Ch 10, Slokas 115-118)
Jupiter in specific houses proves fatal at specific ages:
| Jupiter in | Fatal age |
|-----------|-----------|
| 3rd | 5 |
| 4th | 10 |
| 5th | 46 |
| 7th | 20 |
| 9th | 30 |
| 10th | 40 |
| 11th | 50 |
| Ascendant | 50 |

**BTR Application:** If native died at a known age, check if Jupiter is in the corresponding house at the rectified birth time.

## Marana Pada (Ch 10, Sloka 104 — Notes)
Marana Pada = Rasi counted so many signs away from the 8th Lord's Rasi as the 8th Lord is away from the 8th house. If waning Moon or Lagna Lord is in Marana Pada and aspected by all malefics (but not benefics), life span is only 3 years.

# CHAPTER 40: Calculation of Longevity — Rectification Rules

## Selection of System (Ch 40, Slokas 2-3)
| Strongest among | Use |
|----------------|-----|
| Ascendant | Amsayurdaya |
| Sun | Pindayu |
| Moon | Nisargayu |
| None | Jeevasarma's system |

**BTR Application:** If native's actual lifespan is known, the longevity system that best fits the lifespan should be consistent with the rectified birth time.

## Rectifications for Planetary Contributions (Ch 40, Slokas 6-9)
- Planet in Vargothama, own Sign, own decanate, or own Navamsa → **double** the contribution
- Retrograde or exalted → **treble** the contribution
- Chudamani Acharya's rule:
  - Planet in Angle → each rectification considered
  - Planet in Panaphara (2nd, 5th, 8th, 11th) → only two multiplications
  - Planet in Apoklima (3rd, 6th, 9th, 12th) → only highest taken
- Varaha Mihira's view (disputed): only the highest multiplication

## Reductions (Ch 40, Slokas 10-12)
- Planet in inimical Sign → ⅓ of final figure taken
- Combust in Sun → only half computed (exception: Venus and Saturn exempt)
- **Vyayadi (Chakrardha) Harana:**
  - Malefic in 12th → loses ALL contribution
  - Malefic in 11th → loses half
  - Malefic in 10th → loses third
  - Malefic in 9th → loses fourth
  - Malefic in 8th → loses fifth
  - Malefic in 7th → loses sixth
  - Benefics in these places lose half of what malefics lose
  - If 2+ planets in one House, only the strongest suffers reduction

## Pindayu Base Contributions (Ch 40, Slokas 13-16)
Planets at deep exaltation contribute: Sun=19, Moon=25, Mars=15, Mercury=12, Jupiter=15, Venus=21, Saturn=20 years.

**BTR Application:** Pindayu calculation provides a numerical longevity estimate that can be cross-checked against actual lifespan at death. This is a powerful BTR validation tool.

## Ascendant Contribution in Pindayu (Ch 40, Sloka 17)
If Navamsa Lagna Lord is strong → years = Navamsas past. If Lagna Rasi Lord is stronger → years = Rasis past from Aries.

## Reduction for Rising Planets (Ch 40, Slokas 18-19)
If malefics occupy Ascendant: Convert Ascendant to degrees, minutes. Multiply by years contributed by malefics. Divide by 21,600. Reduce from each malefic's contribution. If malefics in Ascendant are aspected by benefics, only half reduction applies.

## Nisargayu Base (Ch 40, Sloka 20)
Sun=20, Moon=1, Mars=2, Mercury=9, Jupiter=18, Venus=20, Saturn=50 years.

## Full Span of Life (Ch 40, Sloka 21)
Full span = 120 years and 5 days, enjoyed by native with Pisces Ascendant + 9th Navamsa + Mercury in 25° Taurus + all others at deep exaltation points.

# CHAPTER 52: Lost Horoscopy — PRIMARY BTR CHAPTER

This is the most directly BTR-relevant chapter in Saravali. It provides **7 distinct methods** for reconstructing lost birth data from the Prasna (query) chart. All methods use the Prasna Lagna (Ascendant at time of query) as the starting point.

## Method 1: Ayana (Half-Year) Determination (Sloka 1)

```
Rule: Ayana via Query Ascendant Degree
Source: Saravali Ch.52, Sloka 1
Rule: If the Ascendant at the time of query is within the first 15° of a Sign → birth was in
Uttarayana (Jan-Jun). If beyond 15° → Dakshinayana (Jul-Dec).
BTR Application: First-order filter — narrows birth to a 6-month window.
```

## Method 2: Birth Ritu (Season) from Prasna Lagna (Slokas 2-3)

```
Rule: Season from Prasna Lagna Planet
Source: Saravali Ch.52, Slokas 2-3
Rule: The strongest planet in Prasna Lagna indicates the Ritu (season) at birth. If none in
Ascendant, the Lord of the ascendant decanate is Ritu ruler. If clash between Ayana and Ritu,
interchange Ritu Lords: Moon↔Venus, Mercury↔Mars, Jupiter↔Saturn. Month decided by
first/second half of rising decanate. Degrees elapsed in decanate → knowledge of natal Tithi.
BTR Application: Narrows birth to 2-month season, then ~15-day window within it.
```

## Method 3: Jupiter's Birth Placement (Sloka 4)

```
Rule: Jupiter's Birth Sign via Query Ascendant
Source: Saravali Ch.52, Sloka 4
Rule: (Query Ascendant × 2) ÷ 5 → gives natal Jupiter's placement. Adjust by ± multiples of
12 to match actual Jupiter position, guessing the native's age approximately.
BTR Application: Cross-verification — computed Jupiter sign must match actual birth Jupiter.
```

## Method 4: Day/Night Birth from Query Ascendant Nature (Sloka 4)

```
Rule: Day/Night Birth Determination
Source: Saravali Ch.52, Sloka 4
Rule: If query Ascendant is in a Day Sign → birth was during night. If night sign → birth was
during day. (Inverted rule: Prasna Lagna nature is opposite of birth nature.)
BTR Application: Quick binary filter — eliminates wrong 12-hour window.
```

## Method 5: Natal Lagna from Query Ascendant (Slokas 4-9)

### Sub-Method 5a: Trinal Selection
```
Rule: Natal Rasi from 5th/9th of Query Ascendant
Source: Saravali Ch.52, Slokas 4-9
Rule: Out of query Ascendant, the 5th and 9th therefrom indicate the natal Rasi (Moon Sign).
The strongest of the three (query Lagna, 5th, 9th) prevails.
```

### Sub-Method 5b: Body-Part Touching
```
Rule: Moon Sign via Body Touch
Source: Saravali Ch.52, Slokas 4-9
Rule: The parts of the body (head, face, chest, etc.) touched by the querist at query time
indicate the Moon Sign at birth, per standard Aries→Pisces body mapping.
```

### Sub-Method 5c: Query Moon Distance
```
Rule: Natal Moon via Query Moon Distance
Source: Saravali Ch.52, Slokas 4-9
Rule: Distance between query Ascendant and query Moon added to query Moon = position of
natal Moon. Exception: If Moon is in Pisces at query, natal Moon is also in Pisces.
```

### Sub-Method 5d: Navamsa Lagna as Natal Ascendant
```
Rule: Navamsa Lagna at Query = Natal Ascendant
Source: Saravali Ch.52, Slokas 4-9
Rule: The Navamsa Lagna at the time of query may itself be the natal Ascendant.
```

### Sub-Method 5e: Decanate Count
```
Rule: Natal Ascendant via Sun-Decanate Count
Source: Saravali Ch.52, Slokas 4-9
Rule: Number of decanates between query Ascendant and query Sun, when added to query
Sun, yields the natal Ascendant.
```

### Sub-Method 5f: Chayangula (Shadow Length)
```
Rule: Natal Ascendant via Shadow Length
Source: Saravali Ch.52, Slokas 4-9
Rule: Longitude of planet in query Ascendant × Chayangula (shadow length at query time)
÷ 12. Remainder indicates natal Ascendant.
```

## Method 6: Personal Name → Birth Star (Sloka 12)

```
Rule: Birth Star from Name's Matras
Source: Saravali Ch.52, Sloka 12
Rule: Multiply the Matras (syllabic units) of the given name by 2. Add Chayangula length.
Divide by 27. Remainder counted from Dhanishta → birth star.
BTR Application: Cross-verification — computed star should match actual birth Moon star.
```

## Method 7: Composite Mathematical Method (Slokas 13-15)

```
Rule: Universal BTR Formula
Source: Saravali Ch.52, Slokas 13-15
Rule: Note query Ascendant longitude. Multiply by factor based on sign:
  - Leo/Taurus: × 10
  - Gemini/Scorpio: × 8
  - Aries/Libra: × 7
  - Virgo/Capricorn: × 5
  - Cancer: × 4, Sagittarius: × 9, Aquarius: × 11, Pisces: × 12

If Jupiter occupies Ascendant, also multiply Ascendant (in minutes) by 10.
If Mars occupies, multiply by 8. If Venus, by 7. Others: by 5.
ADD both products together. Then:
  - Multiply by 10 → divide by 120 → year; by 2 → Ritu; appropriate → month
  - Multiply by 7 → divide by 15 → Tithi
  - Multiply by 5 → arrive at Navamsa Lagna and Ishta Ghati (birth time)
  - Addition or reduction of 9 for anomalistic answers

BTR Application: This is Kalyanavarma's single most powerful BTR formula — it computes
year, Ritu, month, Tithi, Navamsa Lagna, AND Ishta Ghati (birth time) from the query
Ascendant alone.
```

## Moon-Based Lost Horoscopy (Slokas 17-21)

```
Rule: Moon-Based BTR — Complete Method
Source: Saravali Ch.52, Slokas 17-21
Rule: Just as Ascendant degree helps trace back lost details, the Moon Sign may also be used:
1. Day/Night inversion: If query Moon in a night Sign → natal Moon was in a day Sign (and vice versa)
2. Quarter observation: Note which quarter of the Sign the query Moon occupies →
   proportionately understand natal Moon during day/night
3. Rasi multiplication: Multiply query Moon by same Rasi multiplication factor as Method 7
4. Planetary multiplier: Apply planetary multiplier if applicable (as Method 7)
5. Resultant product (sum of both) × 7 ÷ 27:
   - Remainder = birth star counted from Aswini
   - If rectification by adding 9 → count from Moola
   - If subtraction of 9 → count from Makha
6. Pre-division product processed as before → Ritu, month, Tithi at birth

BTR Application: This provides a SECOND independent calculation of birth data using the
Moon instead of the Ascendant. Results should converge.
```

## Author's Caveat (Sloka 16)

```
Rule: Kalyanavarma's Warning
Source: Saravali Ch.52, Sloka 16
Rule: "I have explained lost horoscopic rules, as expounded by Yavana, Bhadasana etc. But
some of their rules are ambiguous. The student better understand using his judgement."
BTR Application: Classical authority explicitly acknowledges that BTR rules require
astrologer's judgement — BTR is not purely mechanical.
```
