## Overview

Horasara ("Essence of Hora/Astrology") is a classical Sanskrit treatise on predictive astrology. Prithuyasas, son of the legendary Varahamihira, wrote this text to simplify and distill the principles of his father's works into practical, verse-form rules. It is one of the most respected classics in Indian astrology, quoted extensively by later authors like Mantreswara (Phaladeepika), Kalyana Varma (Saravali), and Vaidyanatha (Jataka Parijata).

The work contains 32 chapters covering Rasi characteristics, planetary nature and strengths, conception and birth, evils at birth (Arishta), longevity determination, Dasha effects, Ashtakavarga, planetary yogas, Rajayogas, effects of planets in houses, conjunctions, adverse combinations, female horoscopy, death, lost horoscopy, Nakshatra qualities, Chandra Rasi results, birth in various Amshas, Nakshatra Jataka, and general horoscopic canons.

## BTR-Relevant Rules

### 1. Surya and Candra as Parent Significators (Ch. 5 — Arishtas)

**Rule:** Surya and Candra represent father and mother respectively for living beings. After assessing strength and weakness of these two Grahas, predictions about parents should be made.

**Day/Night Variation (per Brihat Jataka Ch. IV, Sloka 5, quoted in notes):**
- **Day birth:** Surya = father, Sukra = mother
- **Night birth:** Sani = father, Candra = mother

**Affliction to Father:**
- Surya receiving Drishti from malefics, joining them, or hemmed between malefics → evil to father
- Exclusively bad Grahas in 6th and 8th, or 4th and 8th from Surya without benefic help → evil to father

**Affliction to Mother:**
- Candra waning with only malefics in 5th or 9th from her, without benefic association → child deprived of mother
- Sani and Mangala in a Kendra of Candra (jointly or separately) and in one Navamsha → child will have two mothers

**Father Away at Birth:**
- Surya in a movable Rasi in both Rasi and Navamsha charts → father away at birth. Cancelled if Surya aspects Lagna.
- Same rule via Candra → father away at birth

**Father Sick at Birth:**
- Dharma and Bandhu Bhavas with malefic occupation, Lagna lord weak and yuti with Mangala or receiving his Drishti → father sick at birth
- Sani in Simha Navamsha receiving Drishti from Surya → father in miserable state at birth
- Sani in Randhra or Vyaya Bhava and in Simha Navamsha simultaneously → same

### 2. Illegal Birth Indicators (Ch. 5 — Arishtas, Notes)

The following combinations indicate birth outside wedlock (compiled from Parashara Hora, Saravali, Jataka Parijata, Jataka Tatwa):

1. Candra in Bandhu Bhava from Lagna receiving Drishti from malefics or inimical Grahas
2. Lords of Sahaja, Ari, Dhana, and Putra Bhavas in Lagna (birth through servants)
3. Malefics in Lagna, benefics in Yuvati Bhava, Sani in Karma Bhava
4. Candra in Lagna and Mangala with Sukra in Sahaja Bhava
5. Surya in Lagna and Rahu in Bandhu Bhava
6. Surya and Candra in Lagna, or Surya and Mangala in Yuvati Bhava
7. Lagna with Mangala and Rahu, while Surya and Candra are in Yuvati Bhava
8. Kendras without Grahas
9. All Grahas in Dhana, Ari, Randhra, and Vyaya Bhavas
10. Lords of Lagna and Yuvati Bhava together
11. Exchange between Lagna lord and Bandhu lord (otherwise a Raja Yoga)
12. Lagna or Candra not falling in the Vargas of Guru
13. Specific Tithi-Nakshatra combinations
14. Surya, Candra, and Guru in fall, one in Lagna
15. Karakamsha related to only malefic Grahas

**Cancellation:** These Yogas stand cancelled if:
- Kendra has a Graha, or
- Benefics give Drishti to Lagna or Candra, or
- Lagna's lord in Lagna, or
- Guru in Bandhu Bhava, or
- Mangala giving Drishti to Guru

### 3. Conception Rules (Ch. 4 — Nisheka Janmadhyaya)

**Menstruation/Conception Timing:**
- Candr in transit through an Upachaya Bhava (other than 3rd, 6th, 10th, 11th) in a female's chart, coming into Drishti or yuti with Mangala → leads to menses and is congenial for conception
- A girl attains puberty when Candr transits Lagna, Dhana, Bandhu, Putra, Yuvati, Randhra, Dharma, or Vyaya Bhava from natal Lagna and receives Drishti from transit Mangala
- Candr in Sahaja, Ari, Karma, or Labha Bhava → ovum cannot cause conception (Anupachaya is indicated)
- Different condition: same Candr in Sahaja, Ari, Karma, or Labha from Lagna receiving Drishti from a male benefic (only Guru) → conception possible

**Conception Window:**
- Thirteen nights after menstrual bathing (5th through 17th day) → women become fit to conceive
- Does not apply to female children, old women (no menstrual course), sickly or barren women

**For a Male Child:**
- Surya, Candr, Sukra, and Guru in own Rasi or Navamsha → leads to birth of a son
- These Grahas in Lagna (at intercourse) receiving Drishti from or yuti with Mangala → same
- Yuti of Lagna lord and Putra lord; their mutual aspect or exchange
- Strong Sani in odd Bhavas other than Lagna
- Budha in Putra Bhava without yuti/aspect to others
- All male Grahas in Ari and/or Labha Bhava, Putra lord strong
- All Grahas in odd Rasis
- Conception Lagna, Guru, luminaries, and Sukra strong in odd Rasi → son

**For a Female Child:**
- Candr and Sukra chiefly important → birth of a female

**Twin Birth Combinations (from Saravali Ch. 8, quoted in notes):**
- Surya in quadruped Rasi and other Grahas in dual Rasis with strength → twin birth
- Both Candr and Sukra in even Rasis or in dual Navamshas → twin females
- Budha in Lagna, Mangala and Guru in odd Rasis → twin males
- Lagna and Candr in even Rasis receiving Drishti from a strong Graha → twins in womb (one female, one male)
- Candr and Sukra in even Rasis, Guru, Mangala, Budha, and Lagna in odd or dual Rasis with strength → twins

### 5. Effects of Grahas in Bhavas (Ch. 21)

**Surya in Various Bhavas (BTR Verification Rules):**

| Surya in | Birth Time Signature |
|----------|---------------------|
| Lagna (sunrise birth) | Not quiet/calm, few sons, unkind, fond of war, weak-sighted, speaks less, lives abroad, happy |
| Dhana Bhava (2nd) | Facial diseases, wealthy, royal wrath, theft accusations |
| Sahaja Bhava (3rd) | Irascible, strong, death among relatives, famous |
| Bandhu Bhava (4th) | No money of own, distressed, wise, valorous |
| Ari Bhava (6th) | King's minister, leader, learned, famous |
| Yuvati Bhava (7th) | Playful, not attached to wife, unquiet disposition |
| Randhra Bhava (8th) | Eyesight maimed, few children, sickness and fame both |

| Karma Bhava (10th) | Attends royal duties, brave, warring, widely famous |

- In other Rasis identical with Lagna → Candr does not give speech, wisdom, wealth, and strength

### 6. Dasha Verification Rules (Ch. 8-18)

**Dasha Order:**
- Dasha effects should be predicted from the balance of Janma Dasha obtained
- Effects should be calculated from the stronger of Candr and Lagna at birth

**Bhukti Calculation:**
- To obtain Bhukti period: multiply the years of two Grahas mutually and divide by 120

**Transit Trigger:**
- Surya in transit reaching the Rasi occupied by the Bhukti lord at birth → effects are marked to the particular Dasha

**Rahu/Ketu Results:**
- Ketu's results = like Mangala's
- Rahu's effects = like Sani's

**Dasha Timing of Results:**
- Surya and Mangala → results revealed in the beginning
- Budha, Guru, and Sani → effects at the end
- Candr → results while rising
- Sukra → effects depend on its motion

### 7. Longevity/Ayurdaya Rules (Ch. 7)

**Method of Jeevasarma (quoted):**
1. Note the longitudinal distance between a Graha's position and its deep fall degree
2. Convert to minutes and divide by 21600 → quotient = years contributed
3. Remainder × 12 ÷ 21 → months
4. Latest remainder × 21600 → days
5. Latest remainder × 60 → Ghatis

**If Graha is past debilitation toward exaltation**: calculate distance from deep fall to its position.

**Pinda Reduction Rules:**
- Contribution of a Graha should be **halved** if: yuti with another Graha, debilitated, or eclipsed
- Loss of **one third** if: posited in enemy's Bhava, in visible half of Zodiac, lost in war between Grahas, or in Pata range of luminaries
- When repeated deductions warranted, only the highest should be done

### 8. Ashtakavarga Rules (Ch. 17)

**Trinal Reduction (Trikon Shodhana):**
1. Draw Rasi chart with benefic dots of Ashtakavarga
2. Apply Trikon reductions (triangular houses)
3. Apply Ekadhipathya Shodhana (reduction to pairs of Rasis with common lords)

**Rasi Multipliers from Mesha onwards:** 7, 10, 8, 4, 10, 5, 7, 8, 9, 5, 11, 12

**Graha Multipliers:** Guru=10, Mangala=8, Sukra=7, Budha=5, others (Surya, Candr, Sani)=5

**Shodya Pinda to Longevity:**
- Rasi figure + Graha figure = Shodya Pinda
- Pinda × 7 ÷ 27 = years of longevity by the Graha
- Remainder × 27 = months; remainder × 30 ÷ 27 = days; remainder × 60 ÷ 27 = Ghatis
- 27 years = 1 Mandala (excess over 27 rejected per Graha)

### 12. Jataka Lakshanas — Horoscopic Canons (Ch. 32)

**Methodological Rules for Astrologers:**
1. The Jyotishi should be in a clean, lonely place without movement of people, comfortably seated
2. Salute preceptor, Ganesha, and all nine Grahas
3. Maintain silence with lofty notions, face the Sun-God
4. Note all omens as instructed by Siddhasena (weapon's hit, quarrel, fall, cry, grief = ominous; portend death, separation, loss of wealth)
5. Calculate positions after knowing: age, month, natal Nakshatra, Lagna, Hora, etc.
6. Note number of days elapsed since beginning of Kali Yuga
7. Make calculations regarding Dreshkanas, Navamshas, Dwadashamshas, Trimshamshas

**Graha Calculation Rules:**
- To know Navamsha position: multiply longitude by (?) and divide by 12
- For longitude: multiply Graha longitude by 12, add longitude again, divide by 12 → reveals Rasi and Amsha sphutas
- For Candr: add 1/11 of Surya's longitude + 1/4 for other Grahas
- If Sukra and Budha are combust, rising after heliacal setting, or retrograde → add/subtract by doubling Surya's longitude

**Final Words:**
> "The Rasi Mandala (firmaments), the Grahas and the movements are well effective. The Jyotishi and the Kala Purush shall be duly presented. The entire work is constructed by me after studying the several Shastras of the ancestors. The essence has been abridged for increasing the wisdom of the readers and should there be anything inconsistent, the noble may forgive me."

## Key BTR Principles in Horasara

1. **Graha in Bhava as Birth Signature** (Ch. 21): Each planet's placement gives specific birth/life signatures that can verify the correctness of birth time
2. **Parental Significators** (Ch. 5): Day/night variation in father/mother indicators must be checked against known parent details
3. **Dasha Verification** (Ch. 8-18): Dasha events should align — verify from stronger of Lagna/Candra
4. **Transit Triggers** (Ch. 8): Surya transit over Bhukti lord's natal position activates dasha results
5. **Longevity Cross-Check** (Ch. 7): Ayurdaya calculation should match actual lifespan
6. **Sex Determination at Birth** (Ch. 4): Rules for male/female/twin births
7. **Arishta Verification** (Ch. 5-6): Evils at birth should match known life difficulties
8. **D-9 Cross-Verification** (Ch. 30): Navamsha effects amplify/confirm Rasi chart
