## Adhyaya I — Properties of Signs

### Parts of Kalapurusha Represented by Signs (Sloka 8)

| Sign | Body Part | Sign | Body Part |
|------|-----------|------|-----------|
| Mesha (Aries) | Head | Thula (Libra) | Groins |
| Vrishabha (Taurus) | Mouth | Vrischika (Scorpio) | Private parts |
| Mithuna (Gemini) | Breast | Dhanus (Sagittarius) | Two thighs |
| Kataka (Cancer) | Heart | Makara (Capricorn) | Two knees |
| Simha (Leo) | Belly | Kumbha (Aquarius) | Two calves |
| Kanya (Virgo) | Hip | Meena (Pisces) | Two feet |

> **BTR Application:** In all horoscopes, the several parts of the body referred to above should be understood to be represented by the signs beginning from the Lagna. Use to verify body structure against known physical characteristics.

### Sign Classifications (Sloka 13)

- **Short signs:** Mesha, Vrishabha, Kumbha
- **Even length:** Makara, Mithuna, Dhanus, Meena, Kataka
- **Long signs:** Vrischika, Kanya, Simha, Thula
- **Male/Female:** Alternate from Mesha onwards
- **Fierce/Auspicious:** From Mesha taken in order
- **Moving/Fixed/Dual:** Respectively from Mesha

### Upachaya & Anupachaya Houses (Sloka 55)

> **Key Principle:** The 3rd, 6th, 10th and 11th houses are **Upachaya** (houses of increase/growth). The remaining houses — 1st, 2nd, 4th, 5th, 7th, 8th, 9th, 12th — are **Anupachaya** (houses of suffering).

These houses if occupied or aspected by their lords, or by Mercury, Venus or Jupiter, but unoccupied and unaspected by malefics, produce auspicious effects.

## Adhyaya III — Birth & Conception

### Male & Female Determination (Sloka 21)

- Lagna, Sun, Jupiter and Moon strong in an odd sign and odd Navamsa → **Male child**
- Lagna and same planets in even sign and even Navamsa → **Female child**
- Jupiter and Sun in odd sign → Male (irrespective of Navamsa)
- Moon, Venus, Mars in even sign → Female (irrespective of Navamsa)
- Mercury aspecting these five planets in dual sign Navamsas → **Twins**

### Conception Timing (Sloka 43)

Key rule for determining birth time from conception or vice versa:
1. Find the Dwadasamsa of the sign the Moon occupies
2. Find the Rasi this Dwadasamsa belongs to
3. Count from this sign as many Rasis as the Dwadasamsa number
4. When the Moon is in this found Rasi in the delivery month, birth occurs

### Factors for Legitimacy (Sloka 48, 58)

- Moon in a sign owned by a benefic planet or in Jupiter's Varga → legitimate child
- Jupiter must aspect Lagna or Moon for legitimacy
- If Jupiter does not aspect Lagna and Moon, and Moon is with Sun and malefic → child begotten by another

### Birth Place Determination (Sloka 66)

- **Moveable sign strong:** Birth in building far from father's native place
- **Immoveable sign strong:** Birth in father's own house
- **Dual sign strong:** Birth in an outhouse
- **Vargottama Navamsa:** Birth in maternal grandfather's house

### Delivery Location (Sloka 61-65)

- Watery Lagna with full Moon → birth near water
- Saturn in watery Lagna aspected by Mercury → pleasure house; by Sun → temple; by Moon → saltish/sandy ground
- Human Rasi Lagna with Saturn aspected by Mars → cemetery/cremation ground
- Venus & Moon aspecting Saturn → lovely place
- Jupiter aspecting Saturn → house with sacred fires
- Sun aspecting → palace/temple/cow house

### Life Period Classification (Sloka 3)

| Term | Period |
|------|--------|
| Balarishta | First 8 years |
| Yogarishta | Up to 20th year |
| Alpayus (Short life) | Up to 32 years |
| Madhyayus (Middle life) | Up to 70 years |
| Purnayus (Full life) | Up to 103 years |

### Fatal Combinations for Children (Key Slokas)

**Sloka 4:** Even if Jupiter occupies Lagna, if Moon is in 6th, 8th or 12th aspected by Rahu → child dies.

**Sloka 5:** Moon in Gandanta portion of Nakshatra aspected by malefic → early death.

**Sloka 21:** Waning Moon in Lagna + malefic in Kendra or 8th → child dies. If benefics in 5th or 6th aspected by malefics → death within month.

**Sloka 22:** Saturn, Mars, Sun together in 8th or 6th, unaspected by benefics → instant death of newborn.

**Sloka 28:** Moon in Lagna, 6th, 8th or 12th + malefic aspect + no benefic aspect + Kendras empty of benefics → newborn dies.

**Sloka 32:** Two malefics one in Lagna, other in 7th + Moon with malefic no benefic aspect → death soon.

**Sloka 35:** Moon in 6th or 8th + malefic aspect → quick death. Moon aspected by benefics → lives 8 years. Mixed benefic/malefic aspect → lives 4 years.

**Sloka 37:** Saturn in 12th, Sun in 9th, Moon in 1st, Mars in 8th → fatal unless Jupiter strongly aspects.

## Adhyaya V — Length of Life (Ayurdaya)

### Types of Ayurdaya (Sloka 1 of Adhyaya 6)

Eight kinds described by Parashara and others:
1. **Nisargaja** — Natural (based on planetary years)
2. **Pindaja** — Aggregate (based on planetary positions)
3. **Amsaja** — Based on Navamsa
4. **Rasmija** — Based on planetary rays
5. **Chakraja** — Based on zodiacal wheel
6. **Nakshatraja** — Based on birth star
7. **Dayaja** — Based on weekday
8. **Ashtakavargaja** — Based on Ashtakavarga

### When to Apply Each (Sloka 28-33)

- **Lagna predominant** → Amsajayurdaya
- **Sun predominant** → Pindaja
- **Moon predominant** → Nisargaja
- **Mercury predominant** → Rasmija
- **Mars predominant** → Bhinnashtakavargaja
- **Venus predominant** → Kalachakraja
- **Jupiter predominant** → Nakshatraja
- **Saturn predominant** → Samudayashtakavargaja

### Planetary Reductions (Sloka 7-11)

Reductions to Ayurdaya occur when:
- Planet is in depression, eclipsed by Sun, or in inimical house
- Malefic planets present in Lagna
- Planets present in 12th, 11th, 10th, 9th, 8th, 7th bhavas cause reduction
- When several planets unite in one bhava, reduction applies to strongest only
- If benefic aspects Lagna, reduction is by half

### Death Timing Clues (Sloka 59-71)

- Time of death can be determined through specific Dasa period, year, month and day
- Cause of death from 22nd Drekkana from Lagna
- Nature of death from planets occupying 7th from Mandi
- Planet(s) causing death identified through specific combinations

## Adhyaya X — Ashtakavarga (Benefic Dot System)

### Introduction (Slokas 1-2)
Ashtakavarga is a system of benefic and malefic dots calculated from the 7 planets and Lagna. Each planet moving from its birth position produces effects varying with its progress through the 12 Rasis. The 8 seats (7 planets + Lagna) form the basis for calculating all good and evil effects.

### Benefic Houses by Planet (Slokas 3-9)

**Sun:** Auspicious in 1st, 2nd, 4th, 7th, 8th, 9th, 10th, 11th from itself. Mars & Saturn similarly.

**Moon:** Auspicious in 3rd, 6th, 10th, 11th from Lagna; 2nd, 3rd, 5th, 6th, 9th, 10th, 11th from Mars.

**Mars:** Auspicious in 1st, 3rd, 6th, 10th, 11th from Lagna; various positions from other planets.

**Mercury:** Auspicious in 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, 5th, 6th, 9th, 11th from Venus; 6th, 8th, 11th, 12th from Jupiter; etc.

**Jupiter:** Benefic in 1st, 2nd, 4th, 7th, 8th, 10th, 11th from Mars; 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, 7th, 8th, 9th, 10th, 11th from Sun; etc.

**Venus:** Benefic in specific positions from each planet and Lagna.

**Saturn:** Benefic in specific positions from each planet and Lagna.

### Gochara Interpretation (Sloka 3-9)
> **BTR Application:** A planet's transit effect is benefic, mixed, or malefic according as the number of benefic dots in the Rasi representing the bhava is greater than, equal to, or less than 4.

**Key Principle:** 5+ benefic dots → benefic effect; 4 dots → mixed; 3 or fewer → malefic. Dots short of minimum indicate untoward effect despite favorable position. Dots in excess indicate good despite unfavorable position.

### Upachaya Effect Enhancement (Sloka 10-12)
- **Upachaya houses (3, 6, 10, 11):** Planets in these houses with more benefic dots enhance good effects
- **Apachaya houses (1, 2, 4, 5, 7, 8, 9, 12):** Planets here reduce good effects even with many dots
- Good/bad places determined with respect to Lagna or Moon at birth, NOT transit positions

### Ashtakavarga Ayurdaya (Sloka 45-47)
> **BTR Application:** Determination of longevity through Bhinnashtakavarga — used to verify if birth time produces correct life-span.

### Sarvashtakavarga (Sloka 52-71)
The aggregate Ashtakavarga of all planets reveals general information about a person's life.

## Adhyaya XI — Effects of 1st & 2nd Bhavas

### General Bhava Strength Principles (Sloka 1-12)

**Sloka 1:** Bhavas occupied or aspected by Venus, Mercury, Jupiter, or their own lord, but unaspected by malefics, produce happy results.

**Sloka 3:** A planet in depression or inimical sign destroys the bhava it owns. But in Moolatrikona, exaltation, or friendly sign, it advances the same.

**Sloka 4:** When a bhava has its lord in 6th, 8th or 12th, or is occupied by the lord of these three, it suffers annihilation — unless aspected by a benefic.

**Sloka 5:** If the lord of a bhava occupying a Kendra or Trikona from Lagna possesses abundant strength, aspected by benefics or in exaltation, the bhava flourishes.

## Adhyaya XVIII — Dasas & Antardasas

### Importance of Dasas (Sloka 1)

**Sloka 1:** Sages say good and evil come in accordance with the good and bad Dasa one passes through. The essence of what sages have said is faithfully quoted.

### Vimshottari Dasa Scheme (Sloka 3)

**Sloka 3:** Count stars from Krittika in groups of nine. The planets presiding over the dasas belonging to the nine stars in each group, with their years:

| Planet | Dasa Years |
|--------|-----------|
| Sun | 6 |
| Moon | 10 |
| Mars | 7 |
| Rahu | 18 |
| Jupiter | 16 |
| Saturn | 19 |
| Mercury | 17 |
| Ketu | 7 |
| Venus | 20 |

### When Planets Prove Propitious (Sloka 4)

Planets prove propitious when they have:
1. Predominant strength
2. Progress towards exaltation
3. More than average benefic dots corresponding to bhavas they dominate
4. Occupy 10th, 11th, and 1st bhavas
5. Attained exaltation and other benefic vargas
6. Possess six-fold strength (Shadbala)

### When Planets Produce Evil (Sloka 5)

Planets produce evil when:
1. They own the Rasi occupied by Mandi
2. They occupy bhavas containing Mandi
3. They have fewer than average benefic dots
4. They are in inimical, depressed, or eclipsed signs
5. They are in bhava-sandhi or with malefic planets
6. They occupy Rasi-sandhi portions

### Dasa Quality Determination (Sloka 6)

Ascertain Lagna at commencement of a Dasa. If its lord is in:
- Lagna, 3rd, 6th, 10th, or 11th from it, OR
- Lagna included in Saptavarga of Dasa lord, OR
- Friendly/benefic planet in Lagna

→ The Dasa proves auspicious.

### Moon's Role (Sloka 7-9)

**Sloka 7:** Moon produces beneficial effects when in house of Dasa lord's friend, its exaltation, 7th, Trikona, or Upachaya houses from Dasa lord.

**Sloka 8:** Moon in favorable positions promotes prosperity in the bhava represented by the Rasi it occupies. In unfavorable positions, that bhava suffers damage.

**Sloka 9:** It is the Moon that produces good and evil experienced as mental states (pleasure and pain).

### Ishta-Kashta Phala (Sloka 10-11)

**Sloka 10:** Good and evil in Dasas determined in accordance with Ishta (benefic rays) and Kashta (malefic rays) of the Dasa lord.

**Sloka 11:** When Ishta greatly exceeds Kashta → good experienced. When Kashta predominates → evil suffered. When equal → mixed effects.

### Dasa Effects on Bhavas (Sloka 171-173)

**Sloka 171:** In the main Dasa and sub-Dasa of the lord of 2nd or 7th from any bhava, destruction thereof occurs through (1) planet occupying it, (2) planet aspecting it, or (3) its bhava-karaka.

**Sloka 172:** If benefic planets occupying a Trikona, 2nd, and 11th are strong, they produce much happiness in their Dasas and Antardasas.

**Sloka 173:** Jataka Parijata consists of 18 Adhyayas, covering the whole subject from zodiacal signs to the fruit of Dasas, composed under the auspices of the Sun and other planets.

### Colophon (Sloka 174-177)

**Sloka 174:** The celestial tree of horoscopes — Jataka Parijata — has 18 branches (Adhyayas), full of bright stars, displaying the fruits they bear.

**Sloka 175-176:** Summary of all 18 Adhyayas covering: signs, planets, conception, childhood ills, longevity, marring yogas, Raja yogas, conjunctions, Mandi, Ashtakavarga, bhavas 1-12, female horoscopy, Moon's place, and Dasas.

**Sloka 177:** The talented and illustrious Vaidyanatha, son of the illustrious Venkatadri, composed this guide under the auspices of the Sun and other planets.
