# Bhrigu Sutras (भृगु सूत्र) — Planets in Houses

**Attributed to:** Maharshi Bhrigu
**Source:** Bhrigu Sutras (English translation with commentary), Ranjan Publications
**Structure:** 115 pages — Effects of each planet (Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn, Rahu, Ketu) in each of the 12 houses
**BTR Relevance:** EXTREMELY HIGH — Contains specific age-based event timings, house signification rules, and dignity modifiers essential for birth time rectification verification

---

## Overview of Methodology

The Bhrigu Sutras present a **planet-in-house predictive system** where the effects of each graha are detailed by its placement in each bhava. Key BTR-relevant features:

1. **Age-Based Event Triggers** — Many sutras specify exact ages (e.g., "in his 25th year") for specific life events, providing precise BTR verification points
2. **Dignity Modifiers** — Effects change based on exaltation (uccha), debilitation (neecha), own sign (swakshetra), friendly/enemy signs
3. **Aspect Modifiers** — Benefic aspects (Jupiter, Venus) mitigate negative effects; malefic aspects (Saturn, Mars, Rahu, Ketu) aggravate
4. **Lordship Considerations** — A planet's effect depends partly on which houses it rules
5. **Upachaya Principle** — Malefics in 3rd, 6th, 10th, 11th can give beneficial results

## SECTION 5: BTR PRINCIPLES FROM THE COMMENTARY

### 5.1 Karako Bhava Nashaya (The Karaka Destroys the Bhava)

> "Karako Bhava Nashaya" — The significator planet (karaka) can destroy the house it signifies if placed in that house.

**Examples from Bhrigu Sutras:**
- Mars (karaka for brothers) in 3rd house (house of brothers) → can deny brothers
- Jupiter (karaka for children) in 5th house → can cause difficulty with children
- Sun (karaka for father) in 9th house → can cause inimical relations with father

**BTR Application:** When evaluating charts for BTR, this principle provides a counter-intuitive check: a karaka in its own house does NOT guarantee positive results.

### 5.2 Upachaya House Principle

> Malefics in upachaya houses (3rd, 6th, 10th, 11th) give beneficial results.

- A malefic in 3rd makes the native courageous and wealthy
- A malefic in 6th destroys enemies but may cause disease
- A malefic in 10th gives professional success (if not otherwise afflicted)
- A malefic in 11th gives wealth

### 5.3 Dusthana (Evil House) Principle

> The 6th, 8th, and 12th houses are evil houses (dusthana). Planets in these houses lose their good significations.

- Lord of a good house in dusthana → significations of that house suffer
- Lord of dusthana in dusthana → Vipreet Raja Yoga (can be very beneficial)
- Benefics in dusthana → effects mitigated but not eliminated
- Malefics in dusthana → effects aggravated

### 5.4 Bhavat Bhavam (House from House) Principle

The text frequently uses the principle that each house's effects can be seen from the corresponding house counted from it:
- 5th = 9th from 9th (fortune from fortune)
- 7th = 4th from 4th (mother, conveyances from 7th)
- 3rd = 12th from 4th (loss of mother / step-mother)
- 8th = 12th from 9th (loss of fortune, father's longevity)

### 5.5 Benefic Aspect Cancellation

> If a malefic combination is aspected by a benefic (Jupiter or Venus), the evil effects are cancelled or greatly mitigated.

This appears consistently across all planetary placements. For BTR, this means:
- Even with a difficult planetary placement, benefic aspect can explain why an event was less severe than expected
- The Dasha lord of the event period should ideally have some connection to the benefic planet

## SECTION 7: SPECIAL PRINCIPLES FOR BTR

### 7.1 Planetary Conjunction Effects

- **Sun + Mercury** → Mercury combust → speech defects (stammering)
- **Sun + Mars** → 5th house → loss of children through enemy action
- **Sun + Rahu/Ketu** → 5th house → loss of children through serpent curse
- **Mars + Ketu** → 7th house → sexual intercourse with menstruating women
- **Mars + Saturn** → 7th house → sexual perversions (oral)
- **Mercury + Jupiter** → 2nd house → proficiency in mathematics
- **Venus + Moon** → 11th house → conveyances, education, lands
- **Saturn + Ketu** → 7th house → sexual perversions

### 7.2 Waxing vs Waning Moon

- **Waxing Moon (Shukla Paksha)** — Benefic; enhances positive effects
- **Waning Moon (Krishna Paksha)** — Malefic; aggravates negative effects
- **Full Moon (Purnima)** — First-class benefic; eliminates many negative effects
- **Moon in own sign Cancer** — Enhances longevity of mother and native

### 7.3 Combustion (Moudhya)

- A planet within 8.5° of the Sun is "combust" and loses its power
- Mercury combust → speech defects, loss of intellectual ability
- Venus combust → loss of relationship harmony

### 7.4 Rahu/Ketu Acting as Agents

> "Rahu and Ketu act as agents to the planets they are conjoined with. If not conjoined, they give results of the planet that aspects them. If neither conjoined nor aspected, they represent the lord of the house they are placed in."

From Uttarakalamrita (quoted in text):
- Rahu/Ketu with strength produce effects similar to the lord of the house they occupy
- In Kendra/Trikona or with Kendra/Trikona lords → become auspicious
- Rahu ~ Saturn, Ketu ~ Mars (traditional equivalence)

## SECTION 8: RECTIFICATION VERIFICATION WORKFLOW

When using Bhrigu Sutras for BTR verification:

1. **Collect known life events** with exact ages
2. **For each event**, identify the relevant house (see Section 1)
3. **Check the planet in that house** — does the text's predicted age-trigger match the actual event age?
4. **Check functional benefic/malefic status** — does the planet's dignity explain the outcome quality?
5. **Verify Dasha** — the Dasha lord at the event age should relate to the relevant house/planet
6. **Check benefic aspect cancellation** — if the text predicts a negative event that didn't occur, look for Jupiter/Venus aspect
7. **Consider Upachaya/Dusthana principles** — a planet in 6th/8th/12th may behave differently than expected

### 9.3 Kapoor's Commentary on Key BTR Principles

#### 9.3.1 WHY Specific Ages Are Given (Kapoor's Explanations)

Kapoor's commentary provides the reasoning behind each age-based prediction:

1. **Sun in 2nd → 25th year (loss of wealth by Government):**
   > "The second house is significator of wealth. The Sun represents Government. Therefore affliction of the house of wealth by the Sun causes loss of wealth by the wrath of Government. The author says that this event takes place in the 25th year. This can be verified only by practical experience."

2. **Sun in 8th → 10th year (head injury):**
   > "His suffering from head injury in the 10th year is a matter for verification by practical experience, but accidents is one of the significations of the eighth house and the head injury might be caused by some sort of accident."

3. **Sun in 4th → 32nd year (congenial environment):**
   > "The statement that the native will do well in the 32nd year of his life and will attain high position etc. can be verified by practical experience. We have found it correct in only one case so far."

4. **Moon in 2nd → 18th year (commander-in-chief):**
   > "It is hardly likely that any person at the tender age of 18 can be capable of and suitable for such a high appointment. We think the interpretation is that the native will get a distinguished appointment by the king or Government. If Taurus or Cancer assists the Moon, the native may achieve in his young age a distinct superiority in the matter of wealth, position and status."

5. **Moon in 7th → 32nd year (marriage):**
   > "Our views are that a benefic Moon will give good effects in the seventh house. The marriage will be delayed only if there is some malefic influence on her."

#### 9.3.3 The "Lord of the House" Principle

Kapoor repeatedly emphasizes:
> "No house can prosper unless its lord is well placed."

**Key BTR Application:** When verifying a chart, don't just check planet-in-house effects — also check the dignity and placement of the lord of that house. A strong lord can override negative planet placement, and vice versa.

**Examples from commentary:**
- Sun in 4th: "If the lord of the 4th house is strong and well placed, the native will enjoy the comforts of his own land, house and native place."
- Sun in 5th: "If the lord of the 5th is well placed and strong there will be no death of sons."
- Sun in 6th: "If the lord of the sixth is weak, there will be destruction of enemies."

#### 9.3.4 Upachaya House Principle (Kapoor's Interpretation)

Kapoor confirms and elaborates:
> "It is an accepted principle of Hindu Astrology that malefics in upachaya houses, namely 3, 6, 10 and 11 give beneficial results."

But he adds a crucial caveat:
> "According to the learned author, while the Sun will make the native valorous and wealthy [in 3rd house], he will destroy brothers particularly when he is in association with a malefic."

**BTR Note:** Upachaya placement of malefics gives generic benefits BUT can simultaneously destroy specific significations of that house. Both effects must be evaluated.

#### 9.3.5 Karako Bhava Nashaya (Kapoor's Examples)

Kapoor provides additional examples:

1. **Sun in 5th:** "The Sun being not a significator of children can deprive the native of progeny unless he is the lord of the 5th house and is afflicted."
2. **Mars in 3rd:** "Mars is the natural significator (karaka) of brothers... Mars in third is said to give ill health to the native and cause domestic disturbances."
3. **Sun in 3rd → Two mothers:** "The third house being 12th to the 4th house, it will be the house of loss for the 4th house (house of mother). Hence it is likely that the native's mother will die and his father after remarriage will provide him with another mother."

### 9.5 Rahu/Ketu: The Uttarakalamrita Agent Principle (Most Complete Version)

Kapoor quotes Uttarakalamrita at length in the Rahu/Ketu section. This is the most complete classical statement of the principle:

> "Rahu and Ketu act as agents to the planets to which they are conjoined. If they are not conjoined with any planet, they give the results of the planet who aspects them. Only when they are neither conjoined with, nor aspected by any planet, they represent the lord of the house in which they are posited."

**Priority Hierarchy for Rahu/Ketu Effects:**
1. **Conjunction (strongest):** Rahu/Ketu give results of the planet(s) they conjoin
2. **Aspect (moderate):** If not conjoined, give results of the aspecting planet
3. **House Lord (weakest):** If neither conjoined nor aspected, give results of the lord of the house they occupy

**Additional Uttarakalamrita Principles (quoted by Kapoor):**
1. Rahu/Ketu with strength → effects similar to the lord of an inauspicious house and the house they occupy or planet they conjoin
2. Lords of Kendra and Kona (trikona) houses, if not conjoined with Rahu/Ketu → produce beneficial results
3. Rahu/Ketu in Kendra/Trikona or with Kendra/Trikona lords → become auspicious
4. If one node and a trikona lord are in 9th or 10th, OR one node in 9th and other in 10th → prosperity yoga
5. Conjunction of either node with a trikona lord → beneficial
6. If a planet related to a node is inauspicious → the Bhukti of the yogakaraka planet will prove adverse
7. If the lord of 9th also owns 8th or 12th, OR if lord of 9th and 10th conjoin with lords of 9th or 12th → auspiciousness of the yoga is lost

**BTR Application:** When Rahu/Ketu are present in a house relevant to a life event, identify their "agent" (conjoined planet → aspecting planet → house lord) and verify THAT planet's Dasha was active at the time of the event.
