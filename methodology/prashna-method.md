## Overview

The Prashna (Prasna / Horary) Method is used when a person's birth time — and sometimes even birth date — is completely unknown. The central premise: the moment a person feels compelled to consult an astrologer is itself a cosmically significant event, and the planetary positions at that moment encode information about the person's birth chart.

> *"Birth means the birth of the mind and not merely the body; similarly the very inclination to know the future, raised in the mind of a man, is given expression to, not at any chosen moment but at random — rather in a sudden manner."*
> — B.V. Raman

The astrologer records the exact time of the query, the direction from which the querist approaches, and other notable details. This becomes the **Prasna Lagna** (query ascendant), from which the birth chart is reconstructed through a systematic nine-step process.

## Section 2: The Full 9-Step Reconstruction Process

### Step 1: Ayana (Half-Year) Determination

```
## Rule: Ayana (Half-Year) from Prasna Lagna Half
**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology Ch.28 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-28-Unknown-Birth-Times.html
**Rule:** Prasna Lagna in first half of sign (0°–15°) → birth in Uttarayana (Jan–Jun). Prasna Lagna in second half (15°–30°) → birth in Dakshinayana (Jul–Dec). Cuts possibilities in half immediately.
---
```

### Step 2: Jupiter's Birth Position

```
## Rule: Jupiter's Birth Sign via Rising Dwadasamsa
**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology Ch.28 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-28-Unknown-Birth-Times.html
**Rule:** The rising Dwadasamsa (2.5° division) at query time indicates which sign Jupiter occupied at birth, counting from Prasna Lagna. 1st Dwadasamsa rising → Jupiter in 1st sign; 4th Dwadasamsa rising → Jupiter in 4th sign; etc. Jupiter's ~12-year orbit (1 year per sign) makes it the ideal cosmic clock.
---
```

### Step 3: Age Determination

```
## Rule: Age via Body-Part Touching + Jupiter Cycle
**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology Ch.28 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-28-Unknown-Birth-Times.html
**Rule:** Maximum lifespan = 120 years. Jupiter's 12-year orbit → 10 cycles. Body divided into 10 zones (feet→head). Querist's spontaneous touch reveals current cycle. Exact age = (completed cycles × 12) + (number of signs Jupiter has traversed from birth sign to current sign).
---
```

### Step 4: Season (Ruthu) Determination

```
## Rule: Season (Ruthu) from Drekkana Ruler
**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology Ch.28 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-28-Unknown-Birth-Times.html
**Rule:** The ruler of the rising drekkana (10° division) at Prasna Lagna, or the planet in the 1st house, determines birth season:
- Sun/Mars → Greeshma (Summer, May–Jun)
- Venus → Vasantha (Spring, Mar–Apr)
- Moon → Varsha (Monsoon, Jul–Aug)
- Mercury → Sarat (Autumn, Sep–Oct)
- Jupiter → Hemantha (Early Winter, Nov–Dec)
- Saturn → Sisira (Late Winter, Jan–Feb)
---
```

### Step 5: Lunar Day (Tithi)

```
## Rule: Lunar Day (Tithi) from Sun's Degrees
**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology Ch.28 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-28-Unknown-Birth-Times.html
**Rule:** The lunar day of birth = the number of degrees the Sun has passed in its current sign at query time. (e.g., Sun at 29°50' Pisces → 30th lunar day / Amavasya).
---
```

### Step 6: Day/Night Birth

```
## Rule: Day/Night Birth from Prasna Lagna Nature
**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology Ch.28 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-28-Unknown-Birth-Times.html
**Rule:** Inverted rule: nocturnal Prasna Lagna (Cancer, Scorpio, Pisces) → daytime birth. Diurnal Prasna Lagna (Aries, Gemini, Leo, Libra, Sagittarius, Aquarius) → nighttime birth.
---
```

### Step 7: Exact Birth Time

```
## Rule: Exact Birth Time from Rising Arc-Minutes
**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology Ch.28 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-28-Unknown-Birth-Times.html
**Rule:** Formula: (rising arc-minutes / 1800) × (day/night length in vighatis) = birth time in vighatis. Convert: ÷ 60 → ghatikas; × 24 min → modern time. For daytime birth: time after sunrise. For nighttime birth: time after sunset.
---
```

### Step 8: Lunar Month

```
## Rule: Lunar Month from Moon's Navamsa
**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology Ch.28 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-28-Unknown-Birth-Times.html
**Rule:** The lunar month of birth is determined from the navamsa (D-9) occupied by the Moon at query time.
---
```

### Step 9: Birth Lagna Selection

```
## Rule: Birth Lagna Selection
**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology Ch.28 — B.V. Raman
```

---

## Section 4: Season/Month/Day Subdivision

```
## Rule: Month within Season via Drekkana Half
**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology Ch.28 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-28-Unknown-Birth-Times.html
**Rule:** Each season = 2 months. First half of drekkana (0°–5°) → first month. Second half (5°–10°) → second month.
---
```

```
## Rule: Day-of-Month Arc-Minute Formula
**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology Ch.28 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-28-Unknown-Birth-Times.html
**Rule:** 10 arc-minutes = 1 calendar day. Day of month = (arc-minutes passed in relevant half-drekkana) / 10. Half-drekkana = 300 arc-minutes = ~30 days.
```

## Section 6: Cross-Checks and Verification

```
## Rule: Ayana-Season Cross-Check with Substitution
**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology Ch.28 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-28-Unknown-Birth-Times.html
**Rule:** Season from Drekkana ruler must be consistent with Ayana from Step 1. If contradiction: substitute Moon→Venus, Mercury→Mars, Jupiter→Saturn, and use substitute's season. Example: Moon→Varsha (monsoon) contradicts Uttarayana (Jan–Jun) → substitute Venus→Vasantha (spring).
```

```
**Rule:** All nine steps must be internally consistent. Ayana, Jupiter position, drekkana season, lunar day, and diurnal/nocturnal indicators must converge. Conflicts trigger substitution rules.
```
