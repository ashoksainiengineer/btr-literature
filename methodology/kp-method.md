> This means a house cusp can move from one Sub-Lord to another in as little as 5-6 minutes. Sometimes less."

## 3. PHASE 1: PREPARATION

> **Source section**: Phase 1: Preparation

> "Rushing into JHora without preparation is the most common mistake. You'll waste hours clicking around, generating random charts, and getting more confused than when you started."
>
> "Spend 30-60 minutes on preparation. It pays off tenfold."

### 3.1 Data Collection Checklist

**Your Approximate Birth Time:**
> "Gather every piece of evidence:
> - Birth certificate (most common, but least accurate)
> - Hospital records (sometimes more detailed than the certificate)
> - Your mother's memory ('It was just after the morning news started')
> - Father's recollection
> - Any notes or diaries from the time"
>
> "Note the source reliability. A time from your mother's clear memory ('I remember the clock showing 10:22 when they handed you to me') is more reliable than a birth certificate that says '10:30.'"
>
> "Establish your uncertainty range. If the certificate says 10:30 and your mother vaguely remembers 'mid-morning,' your range might be 9:45 AM to 11:00 AM."

**Your Exact Place of Birth:**
> "City/town name
> Country
> Ideally: The specific hospital or location"
>
> "JHora has an extensive atlas, but verify the coordinates are correct. A wrong longitude by even 0.5 degrees will throw off your cusps."
>
> "For Indian births: Verify the coordinates JHora shows against Google Maps. Indian towns with similar names exist in different states. 'Fatehpur' in UP is not 'Fatehpur' in Rajasthan."

### 3.2 The Life Event Audit

> "This is where most people under-prepare. You need a detailed list of significant life events — not vague memories, but specific dates."
>
> "Create a simple document (Excel, Notepad, whatever works) with three columns:
> - Date
> - Event Type
> - Nature (Positive/Negative)"

**Example table:**
| Date | Event Type | Nature |
|---|---|---|
| June 15, 2012 | Marriage | Positive (7th house) |
| March 2015 | Job Loss | Negative (10th house) |
| November 2017 | Child Born | Positive (5th house) |
| August 2019 | Father's Death | Negative (9th house) |
| February 2021 | Major Promotion | Positive (10th house) |

**What qualifies as a "major" event?**
> - Marriage or serious relationship milestones
> - Job changes (new job, promotion, termination)
> - Children's births
> - Major illnesses or surgeries (yours)
> - Deaths in immediate family
> - Significant travel or relocation
> - Major financial events (inheritance, bankruptcy, lottery)
> - Education milestones (graduations, failing exams)
> - Legal matters (lawsuits, settlements)
> - Property transactions

> "Aim for at least 5 major events. More is better. 8-10 events gives you excellent cross-verification."
>
> "Be honest about dates. 'I think I got married in June 2012' is less useful than your marriage certificate's exact date. Dig out documents if needed."

**House Correspondence Reference:**
> - Marriage/Partnership: 7th house
> - Career/Profession: 10th house
> - Children: 5th house
> - Father: 9th house (some say 10th)
> - Mother: 4th house
> - Health crises: 6th house
> - Sudden events/accidents: 8th house
> - Travel abroad: 9th or 12th house
> - Property: 4th house
> - Education: 4th and 9th houses

## 5. PHASE 3: THE RECTIFICATION PROTOCOL (STEP-BY-STEP)

> "Now we execute. This is the systematic process to find your true birth time."

### Step 1: Define the Range of Uncertainty

> "Based on your data collection, establish the search window."

**Conservative approach:**
> "If birth certificate says 10:30 AM
> Assume actual time could be anywhere from 10:10 AM to 10:50 AM (±20 minutes)"

**If birth time is very uncertain:**
> "Mother says 'sometime in the morning after breakfast'
> Your range might be 8:00 AM to 11:00 AM
> This is harder but still workable"

**Creating Candidate Charts:**
> "In JHora, you'll create multiple charts for the same birth date and place, but with different times:
> - Candidate A: 10:10 AM
> - Candidate B: 10:15 AM
> - Candidate C: 10:20 AM
> - Candidate D: 10:25 AM
> (and so on, in 5-minute increments)"

**How to do this efficiently in JHora:**
> "1. Create your first chart (10:10 AM) normally via **File → New → Birth Chart**
> 2. To test different times quickly, simply go to **Edit → Birth Data** or double-click the chart
> 3. Change the time, click OK, and observe how the cusps shift
> 4. Note down each time and its corresponding Ascendant position and 7th Cusp Sub-Lord"
>
> "For wide uncertainty ranges, start with 10-minute increments to narrow down, then switch to 5-minute increments, then 1-minute increments as you get closer."

### Step 2: Apply the Ruling Planets Filter (The First Elimination)

> "This step eliminates clearly wrong candidate times."

**The Principle:**
> "Your natal Ascendant (Lagna) Sign-Star-Sub should connect to the current Ruling Planets."

**The Process:**
> "1. Look at each candidate time's Ascendant position in JHora
> 2. Note the Sign Lord, Star Lord, and Sub Lord of that Ascendant
> 3. Check if these planets appear in your Ruling Planets list"

**Example from the guide:**
> "Your current Ruling Planets are: Sun, Mercury, Venus, Saturn
> Candidate A (10:10 AM) has Ascendant in Virgo, Hasta, Sub of Moon
> - Sign Lord: Mercury ✓ (matches RP)
> - Star Lord: Moon ✗ (not in RP)
> - Sub Lord: Moon ✗ (not in RP)
> - **Verdict: Weak match, but not eliminated (Mercury matches)**
>
> Candidate B (10:25 AM) has Ascendant in Virgo, Chitra, Sub of Venus
> - Sign Lord: Mercury ✓ (matches RP)
> - Star Lord: Mars ✗ (not in RP)
> - Sub Lord: Venus ✓ (matches RP)
> - **Verdict: Strong match — keep this candidate**
>
> Candidate C (10:40 AM) has Ascendant in Libra, Swati, Sub of Jupiter
> - Sign Lord: Venus ✓ (matches RP)
> - Star Lord: Rahu ✗ (not in RP)
> - Sub Lord: Jupiter ✗ (not in RP)
> - **Verdict: Weak match**"

**Decision criteria:**
> "Eliminate candidates with zero or very weak Ruling Planet connections. Keep candidates with 2 or more matches."

**Important caveats:**
> "- Retrograde planets among the RPs are often 'rejected' — they may not apply
> - If one RP planet appears twice (e.g., Mercury is both Day Lord and Moon Star Lord), it's especially significant
> - This filter is probabilistic, not absolute. Don't eliminate a candidate solely because of RP mismatch — but do rank RP-matching candidates higher"
>
> "After this step, you should have narrowed down from perhaps 8-10 candidates to 3-5."

### Step 3: The Physical Appearance Check (Quick Verification)

> "This is a rough but useful filter."

**The principle:**
> "The Ascendant Sub-Lord's physical significations should broadly match your appearance."

**Planet physical characteristics:**
> - **Sun**: Medium build, authoritative bearing, balding tendency, wheat complexion
> - **Moon**: Round face, soft features, fair complexion, tendency toward weight
> - **Mars**: Athletic or muscular, ruddy complexion, angular features, marks or scars
> - **Mercury**: Youthful appearance, slim build, expressive face, medium height
> - **Jupiter**: Large frame, tendency toward weight, full features, jovial expression
> - **Venus**: Attractive features, symmetrical face, pleasant appearance, good hair
> - **Saturn**: Thin or bony, tall, dry skin/hair, aged look, dark complexion
> - **Rahu**: Unusual or foreign look, tall, patchy skin
> - **Ketu**: Thin, marked or scarred, unusual eyes

**Example application:**
> "You're a large, jovial person who's always struggled with weight. Jupiter is the likely suspect. If Candidate A gives you an Ascendant Sub-Lord of Saturn (thin, bony), that's a red flag. If Candidate B gives Jupiter as Ascendant Sub-Lord, it aligns better."

> "**This is a soft filter.** Many factors affect appearance, and Ascendant is only one. Use this to add or subtract confidence from candidates, not to eliminate them entirely."

## 7. COMMON MISTAKES AND TROUBLESHOOTING

> **Source section**: Common Mistakes and Troubleshooting

### Mistake 1: Forcing Events into Wrong Houses

> "A 'job change' isn't always a 10th house matter. Consider:
> - Job change due to **relocation**: 3rd house (short move) or 12th (foreign)
> - Job change due to **marriage**: 7th house (followed spouse)
> - Job change due to **higher education**: 9th house
> - 'Job change' that's really a **business start**: 7th house (self-employment)
>
> If an event doesn't fit the obvious house, reconsider what actually happened. Your 'career break' might have been primarily a 'separation from home' (4th house matter) that happened to involve a job."

### Mistake 2: The Day Start Confusion

> "In Vedic astrology, the day traditionally starts at **sunrise**, not midnight.
>
> If someone was born at 2:00 AM on March 15th, the **weekday** for Day Lord purposes might still be March 14th's day (if sunrise on March 15th is at 6:00 AM).
>
> **In JHora:** Check how the software handles this. Most modern calculations use the calendar day, but verify the Day Lord makes sense for your location and time."

### Mistake 3: Over-Rectifying

> "Some practitioners try to rectify to the exact second. This is unnecessary and often counterproductive.
>
> KP works with sub-divisions spanning roughly 1°28′. Even with perfect rectification, you have a 'confidence zone' of about 4-6 minutes where the sub-lords remain the same.
>
> **Target accuracy: ±2 minutes is excellent. ±1 minute is the practical limit.**
>
> Trying to get '10:17:32 AM' is false precision. Stop when all events fit and the Dasha confirms."

### Mistake 4: Using Too Few Events

> "If you only use 2-3 events, multiple candidate times might 'pass' all tests by coincidence. With 5+ events, wrong times get eliminated more reliably.
>
> If you're stuck between two times and both pass all your events, find another life event to use as a tiebreaker."

### Mistake 5: Wrong Coordinates

> "A surprisingly common error. If your birth city has multiple locations (common in India), or if JHora's atlas entry is wrong, your rectification will converge on a meaningless time.
>
> **Always verify:** Google your birth city + 'coordinates' and compare with what JHora shows. Ensure the timezone is also correct — India uses IST (+5:30) but JHora should handle this automatically if the location is set correctly."

### Mistake 6: Ignoring Retrograde RPs

> "When capturing Ruling Planets, if a planet is retrograde, it's often considered 'weak' or 'rejected' as an RP. Some practitioners skip retrograde planets entirely when using the RP filter.
>
> If you're getting poor RP matches with all candidates, check if a key RP planet is retrograde — and try the analysis without it."

### Mistake 7: Confusing Cuspal Sub-Lord Significations

> "A common confusion: The 7th Cusp Sub-Lord doesn't need to BE Venus or Jupiter (traditional marriage planets). It needs to SIGNIFY houses 2, 7, or 11.
>
> Saturn as 7th CSL can absolutely allow marriage — if Saturn signifies 2, 7, or 11 in that particular chart. Conversely, Venus as 7th CSL can deny marriage — if Venus signifies 6, 10, or 12.
>
> **It's about house signification, not planetary nature.** "

### What If No Candidate Time Works?

> "If you've tested 10+ candidate times and none passes all events:
>
> 1. **Widen your range dramatically.** The recorded time might be off by more than 20 minutes. Some hospital times are wrong by an hour or more.
> 2. **Re-examine your event classifications.** That 'career success' might have been primarily a 'windfall' (8th house) or 'foreign connection' (12th house) that happened to involve your job.
> 3. **Check for recording errors in events.** Is your marriage date definitely correct? Was your child born in 2017 or 2018?
> 4. **Verify your JHora settings again.** Especially Ayanamsa — switching from KP New to Lahiri changes every single cusp position.
> 5. Consider that one 'event' might not have happened as you remember. Memory is imperfect. That 'job loss' might have been a voluntary resignation (different house significations)."

## Rule: Practical Limitations of BTR
**Source:** jagannathhora.com BTR Workshop
**URL:** https://jagannathhora.com/birth-time-rectification-workshop/
**Rule:** Several limitations constrain what rectification can achieve:
1. **Resolution limits:** Can realistically narrow birth time to within a few minutes. Greater precision is usually not achievable because multiple nearby times may produce similar RP alignment and life event correspondence.
2. **Insufficient data:** Rectification works better with more known life events. A person with few major datable events provides less verification material. Young people with limited event history are harder to rectify.
3. **Subjectivity:** Judging which time "best" aligns with events involves interpretation. Different practitioners may reach slightly different conclusions from the same data.
4. **Compounding uncertainty:** If the original time is very wrong (hours off rather than minutes), the rectification search space becomes large. Finding the correct time among many possibilities grows difficult.
**Recommendation:** Rectified times should be treated as more accurate than the original but not as certainly correct. Further verification through subsequent predictions and events continues to test and refine the rectification.

## Appendix A: Nakshatra-Based Vighati Rectification Method
**Source:** Gautam Verma KP BTR Guide
**URL:** https://www.gautamcrystals.com/post/kp-astrology-birth-time-rectification-methods-by-gautam-verma-use-our-tools-for-free-on-the-webs
**Retrieved:** 2026-05-24

### Core Principle
This classical method uses the Moon Nakshatra and base Nakshatra to rectify birth time. The Vighati (a unit of time where 1 Vighati = 24 seconds) relationship between sunrise and birth time encodes the correct birth Nakshatra.

### Step-by-Step Procedure

**Step 1:** Note the local 🌅 sunrise time on the date of birth.

**Step 2:** Record the approximate birth time 🕒.

**Step 3:** Calculate the time difference between sunrise and birth in minutes.
```
ΔT_min = Birth_Time - Sunrise_Time  (in minutes)
```

**Step 4:** Multiply the difference by 2.5 to convert to Vighatis.
```
Vighatis = ΔT_min × 2.5
```

**Step 5:** Multiply the result by 4, then divide by 9. Take the remainder.
```
Remainder = (Vighatis × 4) mod 9
```
The remainder ranges from 0 to 8.

**Step 6:** Add this remainder (as steps) to the chosen base Nakshatra index:
- Aswini = 0
- Makham = 9
- Moolam = 18
```
Target_Nakshatra_Index = (Base_Nakshatra_Index + Remainder) mod 27
```

**Step 7:** Check if the resulting Nakshatra matches the actual Moon Nakshatra.

**Step 8:** If not, adjust the birth time by approximately **3.6 minutes per remainder step** and repeat until a match is found.

### Adjustment Rule
Each unit of remainder corresponds to approximately **3.6 minutes** of time adjustment. If the remainder gives a Nakshatra that is N steps away from the actual Moon Nakshatra, adjust the birth time by:
```
Adjustment_min = N × 3.6  (add or subtract as needed)
```

### Example Walkthrough
*(Hypothetical — based on the method described)*
- **DOB:** March 15, 1990
- **Sunrise:** 6:30 AM
- **Recorded Birth Time:** 8:30 AM
- **ΔT_min:** 120 minutes
- **Vighatis:** 120 × 2.5 = 300 Vighatis
- **Step 5:** (300 × 4) ÷ 9 = 1200 ÷ 9 = 133 remainder **3**
- **Base Nakshatra:** Aswini (index 0)
- **Target Index:** 0 + 3 = **3** → Krittika Nakshatra
- **Actual Moon Nakshatra:** Check if Krittika; if not, adjust time by ±N × 3.6 min and repeat.

### Notes
- The three base Nakshatras (Aswini=0, Makham=9, Moolam=18) divide the zodiac into three equal sections of 9 Nakshatras each.
- This method is most reliable when the approximate birth time is within 1–2 hours of sunrise.
- Modern KP software can automate these calculations.

- **Test each version** against ruling planets and life events
- Round numbers are often **close to true time but rarely exact to the minute**

## KP READER — Arudhra Rectification Method (Original Book Text)

**Source:** Astro Secrets & Krishnamurti Padhdhati Part IV (KP Reader), pages 1-21  
**Author:** Prof. K.S. Krishnamurti  
**Section Title:** ARUDHRA RECTIFICATION METHOD

### 1. Definition of Birth Time — First Breath

> **Rule:** "The birth time is that time of the first cry of the child, when the child takes its first breath which means that the child is now an individual and can no longer needs the help of the mother, for its existence. The time of its getting the first oxygen or the pranan is the time of birth of the child. This time can be arrived at only by the application of the ruling planets and not by so many watches worn by father, grand father or the nurse who attended the delivery."

**Application:** The recorded birth time (hospital/clocks) is almost never the true birth time. Rectification via Ruling Planets is mandatory in KP for any serious analysis.

### 2. The Five Ruling Planets (RPs) at Time of Judgment

> **Rule:** The five ruling planets at the time of casting/rectifying the horoscope are:
> 1. **Day Lord** — ruling planet of the weekday
> 2. **Moon Sign Lord** — lord of the zodiac sign occupied by Moon
> 3. **Moon Star Lord** — lord of the nakshatra occupied by Moon
> 4. **Lagna Sign Lord** — lord of the rising sign at that moment
> 5. **Lagna Star Lord** — lord of the nakshatra rising in the East

**Note from the original text:** "These planets should be inserted into the lagna to be cast. If you insert these planets this will give the right time of birth."

### 3. The 5-Step Rectification Procedure (Arudhra Method)

> **Step (a):** Work out the ruling planets at the time of casting the horoscope: Day Lord, Moon sign lord, Moon star lord, Lagna sign lord, Lagna star lord.

> **Step (b):** Calculate the lagna degree for the given birth time and find:
> - (i) Lagna lord (sign lord)
> - (ii) Lagna star lord (nakshatra lord)
> - (iii) Lagna sub lord (sub-division lord)
> - (iv) Lagna sub sub lord (sub-sub-division lord)

> **Step (c):** Fix the nearest planet for the star lord and the commencing point of the sub sub. The exact degree of lagna is thus arrived.
> - The sub lord is fixed (iii)
> - The sub-sub lord is fixed (iv)
> - "The exact degree of lagna is arrived now."

> **Step (d):** Add Ayanamsa to the degree arrived in (c) for the concerned year → the Sayana position is obtained. Find the other proportionate Sayana cusps from the Table of Houses → corresponding Sidereal Time is noted.

> **Step (e):** Take the Sidereal Time of the birth date from the ephemeris. Find the difference of time spent between Sidereal Time of ephemeris and Sidereal Time of birth. Deduct correction at the rate of 10 seconds per hour. Compute:
> - Difference of Time
> - Local Mean Time difference
> - Standard Time of birth
> "The time arrived this way is the right time of birth for the child."

### 4. The Retrograde Rejection Rule

> **Rule:** "If the cusp sub lord is in retrograde, reject it and take the star lord of the 1st cusp. If the star lord is also in retrograde, take the sign lord of the 1st cusp."

**Application:** When the KP-generated cusp sub lord is retrograde at the rectified time, it is automatically disqualified. The star lord (or sign lord as fallback) replaces it as the functional sub lord.

### 5. The Rapt Conjunction Rule

> **Rule:** "If the sub lord of the 1st cusp is rapt-conjoined with another planet, that other planet may serve as the sub lord." Example: 1st cusp sub lord Mars rapt-conjoined with Jupiter → the native may be a 3rd issue (Jupiter = 3rd issue) because Mars gives its power to Jupiter.

### 6. The Rahu/Ketu Dispositor Rule

> **Rule:** "The dispositor of Rahu or Ketu is also not to be overlooked." Example: 1st cusp sub lord is Rahu in Leo → dispositor is Sun → native may be 1st issue (Sun = 1st issue).

**Application:** Nodes (Rahu/Ketu) act through their sign dispositors. When they appear as cusp sub lords, trace back to the sign lord to determine the true nature for birth order matching.

### 7. Birth Order / Issue Status Rule

> **Rule:** The lagna cusp sub lord must match the native's issue number in the family:
> - 1st issue: Ketu, Mars, or Sun
> - 2nd issue: Venus, Moon, Mercury
> - 3rd issue: Jupiter
> - 4th issue: Rahu
> - 5th issue: Mercury
> - 6th issue: Venus
> - 7th issue: Ketu
> - 8th issue: Saturn
> - 9th issue: Mars

**Application:** "After we fix up the sub lord of the first cusp, we can easily adjust the birth time. Accordingly we can predict the issue status of your spouse also, from the 7th cusp sub lord."

### 8. Verification Sequence — Past Events Must Tally

> **Rule:** "It is not enough if we rest content with mere rectification alone. The past events in life should fully tally along with transit also."

**KP Reader Verification Protocol:**
1. **Ruling Planets match** — birth lagna's sign/star/sub lords must be among the RPs at judgment time
2. **Dasha verification** — Dasa-Bhukti-Antara at each major event must have lords that signify the event's houses
3. **Transit verification** — At the event date, Moon must transit in the star of a significator and sub of another significator of that event
4. **All major events must pass** — marriage, childbirth, job changes, parent deaths, etc.

### 9. Sub-Sub Lord Enforcement (Precision Rule)

> **Rule:** "The sub-sub lord of the ascendant at the time of judgment (Ruling Planets) must be the sub-sub lord of the ascendant at birth."

**Application:** If they don't match, adjust the birth time until the birth Ascendant's sub-sub lord matches the RP sub-sub lord. This is the tightest constraint in BTR — it forces the Ascendant to a ~15' arc precision.

### 10. Planet in Ascendant as Sub-Sub Lord

> **Rule:** "If any planet is posited in the ascendant according to the restricted Ascendant point, that planet should be taken as the sub-sub lord of the Ascendant."

### 11. Lagna Lord at Judgment → Sub Lord at Birth

> **Rule:** "The lord of the lagna at the time of judgment, if strong, will generally be the sub lord of the lagna at birth. The planet occupying the lagna or strongly aspecting the lagna at judgment must also be taken into account."

### 12. War Time / Clock Adjustment Rule

> **Rule:** Historical clock adjustments (WWII: 1-9-1942 to 14-10-1945 clocks ahead by 1 hour) must be factored into BTR. The given birth time may be "war time," not real time.

**Application:** If the recorded birth time falls in a period with war time/daylight saving adjustments, first convert to real time before doing BTR rectification.

### 13. The Number Method (Tie-Breaker)

> **Rule:** When RPs alone cannot narrow down the sub lord, ask the querent for a random number (1-249 for standard, or 1-108 for the Ascendant method). The number corresponds to a specific sign-star-sub combination through planetary progression. The resulting planetary rulers should match the RPs for confirmation.

## Rule: Moon Signification Check — Negative Time Detection (UNIQUE TO THIS SOURCE)

**Source:** Sushmajee KP BTR  
**URL:** http://www.sushmajee.com/astrology/kp-system/birth-time.htm  

**Rule:** Before commencing BTR, check the Moon's position at the rectification moment. If Moon signifies any TWO of the 6th, 8th, or 12th houses (up to Sign and Star level), do NOT proceed with BTR at that time. The time is astrologically negative for analytical work — any rectification performed will produce incorrect or misleading results. Wait for a different time or different day.

**Application — Signification Level:** The negative time check is based on Sign and Star level only (NOT sub, sub-sub, or sub-sub-sub). Evaluate:
1. **Moon's Sign Lord** → does it own or occupy 6th, 8th, or 12th houses?
2. **Moon's Star Lord** → does it own or occupy 6th, 8th, or 12th houses?
3. **Moon's Sub / Sub-Sub / Sub-Sub-Sub Lords** → NOT considered for this filter — only Sign and Star level

**Threshold:** Any TWO of the three negative houses (6th, 8th, 12th) must be signified by the Moon's Sign or Star Lord — not all three, not just one.

**Procedure:**
1. At the moment you sit to perform BTR, note the Moon's zodiac sign and nakshatra
2. Determine the Moon's Sign Lord (planet ruling the sign Moon occupies)
3. Determine the Moon's Star Lord (planet ruling the nakshatra Moon occupies)
4. Check whether the Moon's Sign Lord owns or occupies either the 6th, 8th, or 12th house in the natal chart (or in the judgment chart)
5. Do the same for the Moon's Star Lord
6. If between the two (Sign Lord + Star Lord), any TWO of 6/8/12 are signified → STOP. Do not proceed with BTR

## Rule: Outer Planet Aspect Trigger for Negative Time

**Source:** Sushmajee KP BTR  
**URL:** http://www.sushmajee.com/astrology/kp-system/birth-time.htm  

**Rule:** Even if Moon itself does not directly signify 6th, 8th, or 12th houses, check whether any outer planet (typically Saturn, Rahu, Ketu, or any slow-moving planet) aspects Moon AND that same outer planet signifies any two of 6th, 8th, or 12th houses. If both conditions are met, the time is also negative — do not proceed with BTR.

**Application:** The outer planet's influence via aspect can "infect" the Moon with negative house significations even if the Moon's own sign/star lords are clean. Always check aspects to Moon from planets that carry 6th/8th/12th significations before beginning BTR.

**Procedure:**
1. Identify all planets that aspect the Moon within 3.33° orb
2. For each such planet, check what houses it signifies (occupation + ownership)
3. If any aspecting planet signifies any two of 6th, 8th, or 12th → negative time
4. This applies to BOTH conjunction and aspect relationships

## Step A: Ascendant Sub Lords ↔ Moon Star Lord

**Source:** Sanjeev Gadhok, CIL BTR — Step A
**Rule:** The **Sub Lord and Sub-Sub Lord of the Ascendant** must be linked with the **Star Lord of the Moon** at the time of birth. This is the first and foundational linkage — all subsequent steps depend on this connection being established.

2. Verify that both the Ascendant Sub Lord and Sub-Sub Lord have a meaningful connection to the Moon's Star Lord
