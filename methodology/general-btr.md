## 3. Phase 1: Preparation (Before You Open Jagannatha Hora)

**Source section**: Phase 1: Preparation

> "Rushing into JHora without preparation is the most common mistake. You'll waste hours clicking around, generating random charts, and getting more confused than when you started."

> "Spend 30-60 minutes on preparation. It pays off tenfold."

### Data Collection Checklist

**Approximate Birth Time:**
> "Gather every piece of evidence:
> - Birth certificate (most common, but least accurate)
> - Hospital records (sometimes more detailed than the certificate)
> - Your mother's memory ('It was just after the morning news started')
> - Father's recollection
> - Any notes or diaries from the time"
>
> "Note the source reliability. A time from your mother's clear memory ('I remember the clock showing 10:22 when they handed you to me') is more reliable than a birth certificate that says '10:30.'"
>
> "Establish your uncertainty range. If the certificate says 10:30 and your mother vaguely remembers 'mid-morning,' your range might be 9:45 AM to 11:00 AM."

**Exact Place of Birth:**
> "City/town name, Country, Ideally: The specific hospital or location"
>
> "JHora has an extensive atlas, but verify the coordinates are correct. A wrong longitude by even 0.5 degrees will throw off your cusps."
>
> "For Indian births: Verify the coordinates JHora shows against Google Maps. Indian towns with similar names exist in different states. 'Fatehpur' in UP is not 'Fatehpur' in Rajasthan."

### The Life Event Audit
> "Create a simple document (Excel, Notepad, whatever works) with three columns: Date, Event Type, Nature (Positive/Negative)"

> - Major illnesses or surgeries (yours)
> - Deaths in immediate family
> - Significant travel or relocation
> - Major financial events (inheritance, bankruptcy, lottery)
> - Education milestones (graduations, failing exams)
> - Legal matters (lawsuits, settlements)
> - Property transactions

> "Aim for at least 5 major events. More is better. 8-10 events gives you excellent cross-verification."
>
> "Be honest about dates. 'I think I got married in June 2012' is less useful than your marriage certificate's exact date."

**House Correspondence:**
> - Marriage/Partnership: 7th house
> - Career/Profession: 10th house
> - Children: 5th house
> - Father: 9th house (some say 10th)
> - Mother: 4th house
> - Health crises: 6th house
> - Sudden events/accidents: 8th house
> - Travel abroad: 9th or 12th house
> - Property: 4th house
> - Education: 4th and 9th houses

## 6. Common Mistakes and Troubleshooting

**Source section**: Common Mistakes and Troubleshooting

### Mistake 1: Forcing Events into Wrong Houses
> "A 'job change' isn't always a 10th house matter."
> - Job change due to relocation: 3rd or 12th
> - Job change due to marriage: 7th
> - Job change due to higher education: 9th
> - Business start: 7th (self-employment)

### Mistake 2: Day Start Confusion
> "In Vedic astrology, the day traditionally starts at sunrise, not midnight."
> For births near midnight, the weekday for Day Lord purposes may be the previous day.

### Mistake 3: Over-Rectifying
> "Target accuracy: ±2 minutes is excellent. ±1 minute is the practical limit."
> KP sub-divisions span ~1°28' — you have a "confidence zone" of about 4-6 minutes.

### Mistake 4: Using Too Few Events
> "With 5+ events, wrong times get eliminated more reliably."
> Use additional events as tiebreakers if stuck between two candidate times.

### Mistake 5: Wrong Coordinates
> "Always verify: Google your birth city + 'coordinates' and compare with JHora."
> Ensure timezone is correct.

### Mistake 6: Ignoring Retrograde RPs
> "If a planet is retrograde, it's often considered 'weak' or 'rejected' as an RP."
> Try the RP filter analysis without retrograde planets.

### Mistake 7: Confusing Cuspal Sub-Lord Significations
> "The 7th Cusp Sub-Lord doesn't need to BE Venus or Jupiter. It needs to SIGNIFY houses 2, 7, or 11."
> **"It's about house signification, not planetary nature."**

### What If No Candidate Time Works?
> 1. Widen your range dramatically
> 2. Re-examine event classifications
> 3. Check for recording errors in events
> 4. Verify JHora settings again (especially Ayanamsa)
> 5. Consider memory imperfections

## 5 BTR Methods — Quick Reference

### Parashari — Tattwa Shodhana
**~12-second precision** — Uses elemental (tattwa) cycles to refine birth time. Each tattwa (Earth, Water, Fire, Air, Ether) has specific durations. By checking which tattwa was active at the recorded birth time and cross-referencing with the native's constitution, birth time is refined to ~12-second accuracy.

### Parashari — Varga-Based Rectification
Uses divisional charts (D9 Navamsa, D10 Dashamsa, D60 Shashtiamsa) to verify birth time. Each divisional chart has a smaller time window than the Rasi chart. If a divisional chart changes significantly within a few minutes, the correct time is the one where the relevant divisional chart matches the native's life.

### KP — Ruling Planets (RP) Method
At the moment of judgment, note 5 ruling planets: Day Lord, Moon Sign Lord, Moon Nakshatra Lord, Ascendant Sign Lord, Ascendant Nakshatra Lord. The correct birth time produces an Ascendant whose Sign-Star-Sub lords match these RPs. The more RPs match, the more likely the time is correct.

### KP — Cuspal Sub-Lord Method
Each house cusp has a Sign Lord, Star Lord, and Sub Lord. The Sub Lord is the most sensitive — changing every 5-12 minutes. For an event to occur, the relevant Cuspal Sub-Lord must signify (by ownership, occupation, star, or sub) the houses that permit that event.

### Nadi — Nakshatra Nadi Method
Uses Moon's precise Nakshatra position (Pada level) to determine birth time. Each Nakshatra is 13°20' and is divided into 4 Padas of 3°20' each. The Moon traverses one Pada in approximately 12-24 minutes. By determining the correct Pada, birth time is refined to that window.

### Nadi — Nadi Amsha Method
Further divides each Nakshatra Pada into smaller units called Nadi Amshas using ancient mathematical formulations. This can theoretically achieve precision down to the second level when combined with other methods.

## Overview: 3-Stage Algorithmic BTR

The 3-stage algorithm progressively narrows the search space while increasing scoring precision. It treats rectification as systematic hypothesis testing — generating candidate birth times, scoring them against known life events using transits, progressions, and directions, and converging on the time that consistently aligns planetary timing with biographical reality.

---

## Stage 1 — Coarse Search (5-Minute Intervals)

**Source**: How the App Implements Rectification — Stage 1

**Process:**
1. Generate a candidate every 5 minutes across the search window — 288 candidates for a full 24-hour search
2. For each candidate, calculate the complete natal chart (planet positions, house cusps, Ascendant sign)
3. Compute a **static fitness score** evaluating four factors:

| Factor | Description | Weight |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Ascendant Ruler's Essential Dignity | Chart ruler's condition (dignity/debility) | 2x |
| Ascendant Ruler Angularity | Whether Asc ruler is within 10° of an angle | 1x |
| Angular Emphasis | Planets near ASC, MC, DSC, or IC — Sun, Moon, Jupiter get 2x planet weight | 1x |
| Moon's Sign Dignity & Angular Proximity | Moon's essential dignity and nearness to angles | 1x |

4. Each candidate receives a **preliminary event fitness score** based on transit-to-cusp analysis
5. Candidates sorted by combined score and grouped by rising sign
6. **Top 2–3 rising-sign clusters retained**; rest eliminated
7. This reduces 288 candidates to approximately **60–80 survivors**

---

## Stage 2 — Fine Search (1-Minute Intervals, ±20 Minutes)

**Source**: How the App Implements Rectification — Stage 2

**Process:**
1. Each surviving cluster expanded to **1-minute resolution** within a ±20-minute margin around the cluster center
2. Produces approximately **40 candidates per cluster**
3. Each candidate receives a **full event fitness score**:
   - Every transit planet's angular relationship to every event-relevant house cusp is measured
   - **Weighted by planet's speed** (slower = higher weight)
   - **Weighted by aspect tightness** (tighter = higher score)
   - Transit planets scored against the **natal Ascendant ruler** and the **four angles** (ASC, MC, DSC, IC)
   - Each event's contribution **weighted by its importance score** (6–10, normalized to 0.6–1.0)
4. Candidates sorted by total score (static + event)
5. **Top 15 advance to Stage 3**

## Stage 3 — Fine Tuning (Progressions, Solar Arcs, Primary Directions)

**Source**: How the App Implements Rectification — Stage 3

### 3a. Secondary Progressions

Five progressed planets are scored, each with a specific weight:

| Progressed Planet | Weight | Rationale |
|-------------------|--------|-----------|
| Sun | 2.5 | Most consistent motion, ~1°/year |
| Moon | 2.0 | ~1°/month in progressed time |
| Venus | 1.5 | Irregular but meaningful motion |
| Mars | 1.5 | Slow enough for meaningful aspects |
| Mercury | 1.0 | Stays close to progressed Sun |

**Key measurement**: Whether a progressed planet forms an aspect to a **natal angle** (ASC, MC, DSC, or IC) at the time of a major life event. Angles receive a **1.5x scoring bonus** because they are the most birth-time-sensitive points in the chart. Progressed aspects to house cusps also score but at lower weight.

### 3b. Solar Arc Directions

**Orb**: 1.5 degrees
**Mechanism**: Solar arc (distance progressed Sun traveled from natal position) is applied uniformly to every planet and point in the natal chart. Directed planets checked against natal angles and event-relevant house cusps. Directed MC also tested against natal planets.

### 3c. Primary Directions

**Orb**: 1.0 degree (tightest of any technique)
**Mechanism**: Directed MC (advancing ~1° per year) checked against natal planets
**Weighting**:
| Planet Group | Weight |
|-------------|--------|
| Sun, Moon, Saturn, Jupiter | 3x |
| Mercury, Venus, Mars | 1.5x |

### Contradiction Penalty

A penalty is applied if events of opposite nature (e.g., marriage and breakup within the same year) both score high for the same houses.

```
Final Score = Static Score + Event Score + Fine Tuning Score − Contradiction Penalty
```

# Kerykeion Practical Rectification Guide — 6-Step Protocol

## Step 1: Assess the Starting Point

**Rule:** The process begins by determining the reliability of the available birth time data. Birth times fall into categories with decreasing reliability:

| Category | Description | Typical Window |
|----------|-------------|----------------|
| **Precise recorded time** | Birth certificate or hospital record | ±5-10 min (may be rounded) |
| **Approximate time** | Family memory | ±30 min or more |
| **General period** | "Morning," "afternoon," "evening" | 4-6 hour window |
| **Completely unknown** | No data available | All 12 rising signs |

**Key Principle:** Even precise recorded times benefit from minor rectification to account for rounding. Family memories are often shaped by narrative ("you were born at dawn") rather than precise observation. Completely unknown times require considering all 12 rising signs and typically professional assistance.

**Application:** Document the data source reliability before proceeding to Step 2.

## Step 2: Establish the Probable Rising Sign

**Rule:** Narrow the probable rising sign using observable traits, personality characteristics, and general life themes. This establishes an approximate two-hour window for the birth time.

**Per-Event Rule — Rising Sign Personality Assessment:**
- Assess physical presentation, temperament, and instinctive behavioral patterns
- Compare overall pattern of characteristics against rising sign profiles
- When two rising signs are plausible, carry both forward into the testing phase

**Per-Event Rule — House Structure Life Theme Alignment:**
- The rising sign creates the house structure (e.g., Aries Ascendant → Libra on 7th cusp → partnerships carry particular weight)
- Check whether the person's major life themes align with their proposed house structure
- Example: Scorpio Ascendant → Taurus on 7th → stability/sensory connection in relationships

**Application:** Use this as an early filter for candidate birth times. If a proposed Ascendant's house structure contradicts the native's known life themes, the Ascendant may be wrong.

**Rule:** Gather at least **five significant life events** with precise dates. Each event determines which angle or house cusp it tests.

**Per-Event Rule — Event Classification:**
- **Marriages** → 7th house / partnership angle
- **Career milestones** (promotions, job changes, terminations) → 10th house / MC
- **Relocations** (moves, immigration) → 4th house / IC or 12th house
- **Births of children** → 5th house
- **Losses** (deaths, major financial loss) → 8th or 12th house
- **Health crises** → 6th house

**Format:** Organize chronologically with dates and brief descriptions. Each event must be annotated with the **area of life it affects**, as this determines the testing angle.

**Minimum Events:** 5 events minimum. More events produce stronger verification.

## Step 4: Test Proposed Times

**Rule:** Generate charts for several times within the estimated window (typically **15-minute intervals**). For each proposed time, apply at least **2 predictive techniques** to check correspondence with life events.

**Per-Event Rule — Testing Techniques:**
- **Transits to angles:** Check transiting planets to the Ascendant, MC, Descendant, IC at event dates
- **Solar arc directions:** Direct the angles by solar arc and check contacts to event significators
- **Secondary progressions:** Progress the chart to event dates and check progressed angles

**Per-Event Rule — Minimum Technique Requirements:**
- Each candidate time must be tested with at least **2 independent predictive techniques**
- Converging evidence from multiple techniques is stronger than any single technique
- If techniques contradict each other for a given candidate, that time is weakened

**Practical Note:** Modern software significantly accelerates this step, allowing the practitioner to adjust the birth time and immediately see resulting changes in directed and progressed contacts.

## Step 5: Narrow and Refine

**Rule:** As analysis identifies the best-fitting times, test at progressively finer intervals:
1. **15-minute intervals** — initial broad screening
2. **5-minute intervals** — secondary refinement
3. **1-minute intervals** — final precision

**Per-Event Rule — Consistency Scoring:**
- A time that accurately describes **6 out of 7 events** is more reliable than one that perfectly describes **3 but fails on 4**
- Do not over-weight a single perfect match while ignoring failures on other events
- Look for the time that **consistently** produces the best fit across **multiple events and multiple techniques**

**Application:** This is an iterative elimination process. Times are narrowed to smaller windows, then retested at finer resolution.

---

## Step 6: Verify

**Rule:** Once a proposed time is established, verify it against **additional events that were NOT used during the initial rectification**.

**Per-Event Rule — Holdout Validation:**
- Reserve at least 1-2 known events as "holdout" test cases
- The proposed time should accurately describe the holdout events as well
- When a chart passes this final test, confidence increases significantly

**Per-Event Rule — Rising Sign Consistency Check:**
- The proposed rising sign should **consistently match** the person's observable presentation and habitual approach to life
- If the technical analysis produces a rising sign that does **not** fit the person's known characteristics, reconsider the result

**Per-Event Rule — Convergence Requirement:**
- **Multiple techniques** must converge on the same time
- The correct time should produce accurate results across **several predictive systems**
- The convergence of **independent methods** is the strongest evidence for a proposed time

## 7. Bhava vs. Bhava Cusp Distinction

**Source:** Vidyavaridhi — Parameters that complicate astrology (Part 1)
**URL:** https://vidyavaridhi.org/index.php/2015/08/09/788/

**Rule:** There is a critical distinction between a **Bhava** (house) and a **Bhava Cusp**:

- **Bhava (House):** Represents the **qualities** of that particular manifestation in life
- **Bhava Cusp:** Represents the **tangible manifestation** of that particular indication

> "The keyword to keep in mind is 'qualities' for Bhavas and 'tangible manifestation' for Bhava Cusp. There is a subtle difference."

**Example — 7th House:**
- The 7th bhava represents the spouse and his/her characteristics
- The 7th bhava cusp is the **actual physical manifestation** of the spouse

Varga Chatushtaya ("four multiples") is an ancient BTR method traceable to **Skanda Hora** and **Saunaka Hora** — sage-level sources predating even classical texts like Brihat Jataka. It uses 4 key multiples of the Lagna longitude to verify birth time against the Moon's Nakshatra.

```
## Rule: Varga Chatushtaya — The Four Multiples

| # | Name | Multiplier | Sanskrit Term |

| 4 | Navamsa-Dwadasamsa | ×108 | Navamsa-Dwadasamsa |

**Decision Rule (Saunaka Hora):** "If 2 or more of these multiples indicate a
```

**Source:** Aquamoonlight Rectification
**URL:** https://www.aquamoonlight.co.uk/rectification.html

**Source:** Aquamoonlight Rectification
**URL:** https://www.aquamoonlight.co.uk/rectification.html
**Rule:** Major life events are most often shown by a **major transit over the Ascendant and/or MC axis** or in aspect to it. It is valid to erect **event charts** rather than just basing analysis on the ephemeris. By erecting event charts, the correlation between that chart and the birth chart becomes much plainer.

**Graph Plotting Method:** Construct a graph plotting the planetary positions for each event. This enables easy identification of degrees that occur most prominently — they will peak in the graph. The object is to identify **sensitive points** which are likely to be representative of the angles of the true natal chart:

**Source:** Aquamoonlight Rectification
**URL:** https://www.aquamoonlight.co.uk/rectification.html
**Rule:** When particular degrees are most prominent in event charts, and in particular **qualities (quadruplicities)** — cardinal, fixed or mutable signs — then the chances are that the most likely Ascendant and Midheaven have been found.

**Ascendant-MC Relationship:** At any given latitude, the Ascendant degree is always **relative to the degree of the Midheaven**. By adjusting the birth chart in terms of the ascending degree and sign, it automatically adjusts the sign and degree of the Midheaven. If the two most prominent degrees shown by life events are set so that one is the Ascendant and the other is the MC, and it works at that latitude, then this should show the natal positions of these angles.

**Complication:** If these sensitive points coincide with a **natal planet** position — especially if the planet is conjunct or in a 4th harmonic aspect (conjunction, square, opposition) to the true natal angle — it can overshadow the angle itself and obscure it.

## The Three Definitions of Birth Time (Satya Jatakam)

Sage Satyacharya, in *Satya Jatakam* (Basis of Dhruva Nadi), Dhruv Matham, identifies **three different moments** that can be taken as the Time of Birth:

### 1. Adhana Lagna (आधान लग्न) — Moment of Conception
The fusion of a spermatozoon with an ovum resulting in the formation of a zygote.

### 2. Siro Darshana Lagna (सिरो–दर्शन लग्न) — Moment the Head is First Sighted
The moment at which the head of the child is first sighted during delivery.

### 3. Bhupatana Lagna (भूपतन लग्न) — Moment the Child Touches the Ground
The moment at which the child leaves the body of the mother (umbilical cord is cut) and touches the ground/earth.

> **Recommendation**: "As it is difficult to determine the first two moments accurately, the third one, Bhupatana Lagna, should be taken for preparing a horoscope."

## Method: Transit Method for BTR
**Source:** Perfecting the Moment by Vijay Dubey
**Rule:** Use planetary transits (Gochara) to verify and rectify birth time. The premise: for a correctly timed birth chart, the transits of slow-moving planets (Saturn, Jupiter, Rahu/Ketu) over sensitive points in the natal chart should correlate with major life events.

**Key Principle:** If the recorded birth time is correct, transits at the time of known life events should activate houses and significators appropriate to those events. A transit that activates a wrong house indicates the birth time needs adjustment.

**Application:** For each major life event, check the transiting positions of Saturn, Jupiter, Rahu, and Ketu relative to the natal chart. ## Method: Solar and Lunar Returns for BTR Verification
**Source:** Perfecting the Moment by Vijay Dubey
