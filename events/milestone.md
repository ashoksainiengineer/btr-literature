### Rule 1: Nakshatra Verification (Birth Constellation Check)

**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology, Ch.11 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-11-Birth-Verification-and-Rectification.html
**Rule:** "Multiply the time of birth in vighatis by 4 and divide the product by 9. The remainder must give the ruling constellation when counted from Aswini, Makha or Moola." — B.V. Raman

**Formula:** (Vighatis × 4) ÷ 9 → remainder → count from Aswini/Makha/Moola = birth nakshatra
**Purpose:** Confirm birth time is consistent with the Moon's birth star
**Worked example:** 2033 vighatis × 4 = 8132 ÷ 9 = remainder 5 → 5th from Aswini = Mrigasira ✓

### Three Nakshatra Groups

| Remainder | From Aswini (G1) | From Makha (G2) | From Moola (G3) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Aswini | Makha | Moola |
| 2 | Bharani | Pubba | Poorvashadha |
| 3 | Krittika | Uttara | Uttarashadha |
| 4 | Rohini | Hasta | Sravana |
| 5 | Mrigasira | Chitta | Dhanishta |
| 6 | Aridra | Swati | Satabhisha |
| 7 | Punarvasu | Visakha | Poorvabhadra |
| 8 | Pushyami | Anuradha | Uttarabhadra |
| 9 (or 0) | Aslesha | Jyeshta | Revati |

### Rule 2: Lagna Verification (Ascendant Check)

**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology, Ch.11 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-11-Birth-Verification-and-Rectification.html
**Rule:** "Multiply the number of ghatis from birth by 6 and add the longitude of the Sun (the number of degrees passed in the sign). Divide the sum by 30. The quotient plus 1 counted from the Sun's sign will give the rising sign." — B.V. Raman

**Formula (ghatis):** (Ghatis × 6 + Sun° in sign) ÷ 30 → quotient + 1 from Sun's sign = Ascendant
**Formula (hours):** (Hours × 15 + Sun° in sign) ÷ 30 → quotient + 1 from Sun's sign = Ascendant
**Worked example:** 33.88 ghatis × 6 = 203.28 + 24° (Sun in Cancer) = 227.28 ÷ 30 = q=7 → 8 from Cancer = Aquarius ✓
**Caveat:** Approximate check to verify Ascendant **sign**, not exact degrees.

### Rule 3: Trinal Relationship Check

**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology, Ch.11 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-11-Birth-Verification-and-Rectification.html

The 7th from the sign occupied by the lord of the Moon's sign or the 5th or the 9th from the similar sign and in some cases the sign where the Moon is at radix itself becomes the ascendant." — B.V. **Check:** Asc should be 5th, 9th, or 7th from the Moon's sign lord's position
**Rationale:** Houses 1, 5, 9 form the Dharma Trikona — the Ascendant lord and Moon's sign lord should be harmoniously related.

### Cascade Effect of Time Error

| Time Error | Ascendant Effect | Dasa Balance Effect | Divisional Chart Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1-2 min | Degree shifts slightly | Days change | Usually unchanged |
| 5-10 min | Degree shifts noticeably | Weeks to months change | Navamsa (D-9) may change |
| 15-30 min | May cross Navamsa boundary | Months change significantly | Multiple divisional charts shift |
| 1-2 hrs | Likely changes sign entirely | Entire Dasa may change | Nearly all charts affected |
| 4+ hrs | Completely different Lagna | Different Dasa lord | Entirely different horoscope |

# BPHS Classical Raja Yoga Rules — Milestone BTR

## Key Milestone-Indicating Yogas from BPHS

### 1. Raja Yogas as Life Milestones (Ch.39)

Major life achievements (milestones) in BPHS are primarily described through Raja yoga combinations. The presence and activation of these yogas denote significant turning points in the native's life trajectory.

#### Maha Raja Yoga (Slokas 6-7)
> Should the ascendant lord and the 5th lord exchange signs OR Atma Karaka + Putra Karaka in ascendant/5th/exaltation/own sign/own Navamsa with benefic aspect → Maha Raja yoga.

**Milestone significance:** This yoga places the native at Himalayan heights. Its activation through Dasa periods marks major life milestones.

#### Arudha Lagna + Darapada Relationship (Sloka 23)
> If AL and Dara Pada are in mutual angles/trines/3-11 → king. If in 6th/8th or 2nd/12th → poverty despite other yogas.

**Milestone significance:** This is a prerequisite condition — without proper AL-DP relationship, other Raja yogas don't mature into milestones.

#### Dinardha/Nisardha Birth (Sloka 40)
> Birth within ~60 minutes of Dinardha (half-day) or Nisardha (half-night) → kingly birth.

**Milestone significance:** Koteeswara yoga or Lakshadhikari yoga — the very timing of birth itself is a milestone indicator.

### 2. Wealth Milestones (Ch.41)

#### 5th & 9th Lords as Dasa Triggers (Sloka 16)
> The 9th lord and 5th lord are capable of bestowing wealth. Planets conjunct such a lord also give wealth during their Dasa periods.

**Milestone significance:** Major financial milestones occur in the Dasa of 5th lord, 9th lord, or planets conjunct them.

#### Great Affluence Combinations (Slokas 2-8)
The specific combinations where 5th lord in 5th and 11th lord in 11th (per ascendant) indicate major wealth milestones at the birth chart level.

### 3. Angular-Trinal Lord Relationships (Ch.39, Slokas 35-38 & Ch.41, Sloka 28)

> Angles = Vishnusthaanas, Trines = Lakshmisthaanas. Angular lord + trinal lord relationship → Rajayoga.

**Milestone significance:** The Dasa activation of an angular lord related to a trinal lord marks a major life milestone — the "Vishnu-Lakshmi" blessing period.

### 4. Divisional Dignity Milestones (Ch.41, Slokas 29-34)

When an angular lord and trinal lord (with relationship) are in specific Amsas, the milestone corresponds to the dignity:

| Amsa | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| Simhasanamsa | Emperor over entire earth |
| Gopuramsa | Honoured by other kings |
| Devalokamsa | Incarnations of Lord Vishnu |

### 5. Milestone Activation Through Dasa

The yogas described above provide the POTENTIAL for milestones. Their ACTIVATION occurs during:
- Dasa of 5th lord → wealth milestones
- Dasa of 9th lord → fortune/status milestones
- Dasa of angular lords related to trinal lords → Raja yoga milestones
- Dasa of planets in their exalted Amsas → dignity-related milestones

- [ ] For status milestones: check angular-trin lord relationship

Success/acquisition of any Bhava occurs when:
1. Lord of Lagna transits Trikona to Bhava lord's Rasi/Amsa
2. Lagna lord transits the Bhava itself
3. Bhava lord transits triangle to Lagna lord's Rasi/Amsa
4. Bhava lord transits Lagna
5. Two lords conjoin or aspect each other
6. Karaka of Bhava conjoins lord of Rasi containing Lagna/Moon
7. Jupiter transits trine to Bhava lord's Rasi/Amsa

Each Bhava lord's Dasa produces specific effects — strong = positive, weak = negative.

- Jupiter + Moon's lord + Lagna lord in Kendras at birth = middle portion of life is good
- Planets in Prishtodaya signs = good results at end
- Planets in Ubhayodaya signs = good in middle
- Planets in Sirshodaya signs = good in beginning

## 4. BTR Application

1. **Life milestones → Match event to specific Bhava lord Dasa**
2. **Cross-verify via Jupiter transit:** Over Bhava lord's position
3. **Lagna lord transit:** Activates effects of whichever Bhava it aspects
4. **Use XVI.31-32 for timing precision of any life event**

# Milestone Rules from Brihat Jataka of Varahamihira (1905 Ed.)

**Source**: Full text of "The Brihat Jataka" — https://archive.org/details/brihatjataka00varaiala
**Chapters**: VII (Ayurdaya), VIII (Dasas and Antardasas)

---

## Chapter VIII: Dasa System — The Timing Framework

### Dasa Basis
The lord of the Dasa period at the time of birth is determined by the Nakshatra occupied by the Moon. The length of each Dasa and the order are described.

### Dasa Calculation
The Moon's longitude at birth determines the balance of Dasa. The elapsed portion and remaining portion at birth must be calculated.

### Life Event Timing
"Which of the several Yogas described in this Chapter are applicable to the time of birth also, shall be determined on the nature of such Yogas."

---

## Chapter VII: Ayurdaya — Life Span Determination

The length of life is determined from:
1. **Graha Ayurdaya** (Planetary method): Based on planetary positions at birth
2. **Nakshatra Ayurdaya** (Stellar method): Based on Moon's Nakshatra
3. **Pindayu** and **Nisargayu** methods

### Key Principle
The 8th house and its lord, 8th from the Moon, and the lord of the Drekkana of the Lagna all contribute to determining the life span. This directly cross-verifies birth time.

---

## Application for BTR

1. **Dasa balance at birth**: Must be correctly computed from Moon's Nakshatra — a key BTR anchor
2. **Life events must fall in appropriate Dasas**: The Dasa of planets signifying the relevant house must be running at the time of each life event
3. **Ayurdaya (longevity)**: If person lived past a certain age, the chart must show corresponding longevity indicators
4. **Dasa sequence**: The order of major life events must follow the Dasa sequence logically

## G1. The 12-House Life Audit — Client Interview Method

**Rule (Brennan):** The primary data for rectification comes from known life events and personal characteristics. The astrologer's interview with the client is a systematic process:

**Step 1 — Ask about major life events:**
What are the major events that have already occurred in the client's life? What makes their life distinct from others? Note: people tend to normalize their unique experiences, so the astrologer must probe for what stands out.

**Step 2 — Focus on benefics and malefics:**
Identify the areas of life that have been most positive and most challenging. These correspond to the house placements of benefics and malefics in the correct chart.

**Step 3 — Systematic house-by-house audit:**
Go through each of the 12 houses and ask targeted questions:

| House | Domain | Questions |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| 1st | Character, body, appearance | Self-description, physical traits |
| 2nd | Finances, possessions | Financial stability, resources |
| 3rd | Siblings, travel, early education | Sibling relationships, short trips |
| 4th | Parents, home, living situation | Family background, home life |
| 5th | Children, creativity | Children, creative pursuits |
| 6th | Illness, injury, work, subordinates | Health issues, work environment |
| 7th | Relationships, partners | Marriage/partnership history |
| 8th | Death, other people's money | Inheritances, losses |
| 9th | Religion, travel, foreign, higher education | Beliefs, long journeys, advanced study |
| 10th | Career, reputation | Professional life, public standing |
| 11th | Friends, groups | Social circles, networks |
| 12th | Enemies, loss, isolation | Hidden challenges, solitude |

**Step 4 — Listen for subtle clues:**
The client's perception may not always be accurate. Sometimes the discrepancy between what they say and what the chart indicates is itself an important clue.

**Step 5 — Cross-reference by house topics:**
A candidate time that places known life strengths in houses ruled by benefics and challenges in houses ruled by malefics is more likely correct.

**BTR Application:** This 12-house audit structure should inform the AI-Pandit event data collection interface. The system should prompt for events in each house domain to ensure comprehensive coverage, and use the house assignments to score candidate times.
