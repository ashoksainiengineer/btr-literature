### Rule 1: Nakshatra Verification (Birth Constellation Check)

**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology, Ch.11 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-11-Birth-Verification-and-Rectification.html
**Rule:** "Multiply the time of birth in vighatis by 4 and divide the product by 9. The remainder must give the ruling constellation when counted from Aswini, Makha or Moola." — B.V. Raman

**Formula:** (Vighatis × 4) ÷ 9 → remainder → count from Aswini/Makha/Moola = birth nakshatra
**Purpose:** Confirm birth time is consistent with the Moon's birth star
**Worked example:** 2033 vighatis × 4 = 8132 ÷ 9 = remainder 5 → 5th from Aswini = Mrigasira ✓

### Rule 2: Lagna Verification (Ascendant Check)

**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology, Ch.11 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-11-Birth-Verification-and-Rectification.html
**Rule:** "Multiply the number of ghatis from birth by 6 and add the longitude of the Sun (the number of degrees passed in the sign). Divide the sum by 30. The quotient plus 1 counted from the Sun's sign will give the rising sign." — B.V. Raman

**Formula (ghatis):** (Ghatis × 6 + Sun° in sign) ÷ 30 → quotient + 1 from Sun's sign = Ascendant
**Formula (hours):** (Hours × 15 + Sun° in sign) ÷ 30 → quotient + 1 from Sun's sign = Ascendant
**Worked example:** 33.88 ghatis × 6 = 203.28 + 24° (Sun in Cancer) = 227.28 ÷ 30 = q=7 → 8 from Cancer = Aquarius ✓
**Caveat:** Approximate check to verify Ascendant **sign**, not exact degrees.

**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology, Ch.11 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-11-Birth-Verification-and-Rectification.html
**Rule:** "The 5th or the 9th sign from the house occupied by the lord of the sign in which the Moon is placed becomes the Janma Lagna or the ascendant. The 7th from the sign occupied by the lord of the Moon's sign or the 5th or the 9th from the similar sign and in some cases the sign where the Moon is at radix itself becomes the ascendant." — B.V. Raman

**Check:** Asc should be 5th, 9th, or 7th from the Moon's sign lord's position
**Rationale:** Houses 1, 5, 9 form the Dharma Trikona — the Ascendant lord and Moon's sign lord should be harmoniously related.

| Time Error | Ascendant Effect | Dasa Balance Effect | Divisional Chart Effect |
|---|---|---|---|

## Rule: Dual Planet 7th Cusp Sublord + 6th House Signification → Divorce

**Source:** Astrogle KP BTR Article
**URL:** https://www.astrogle.com/astrology/birth-time-rectification-through-k-p-astrology.html
**Rule:** According to late Prof. K. S. Krishnamurthy, if the 7th cusp sublord is a dual planet (Mercury or Jupiter) AND is posited in the star or sub of a planet signifying the 6th house, there is negation of marriage leading to divorce.

**Mechanism:**
- Dual planets (Mercury, Jupiter) represent duality/multiplicity in relationships
- When such a planet is the sublord of the 7th cusp (marriage), it indicates the potential for more than one marital-like relationship
- The star/sub lord connection to 6th house significators (enmity, debt, disease, service) poisons the partnership
- Result: The marriage is negated → separation/divorce

**Key Conditions:**
1. 7th cusp sublord = Mercury OR Jupiter (dual planet)
2. The star lord of the 7th CSL must signify 6th house
3. OR the sub lord of the 7th CSL must signify 6th house
4. OR both star and sub lords signify 6th/12th houses

**Houses/Planets:** 7th cusp sublord, 6th house, Mercury, Jupiter, Rahu, Ketu
**Dasha Pattern:** N/A — structural principle

**Classical Reference:** `/data/btr-knowledge/classical/phaladeepika.md` Sections 3, 10

---

## 1. Divorce/Separation Yogas (X.1-5)

Conditions leading to loss/separation from wife:
- **Malefics in 12th, 4th, 8th from Venus** or Venus hemmed by malefics
- **Venus aspected by or conjoined with malefics**
- **7th house/lord with malefics, in depression/inimical/eclipsed**
- **7th lord in 5th:** Lose wife or sonless
- **5th lord or 8th lord in 7th:** Certain loss of wife
- **Weak Moon in 5th + malefics in 12th/7th/1st:** Bereft of wife and children
- **Sun + Rahu in 7th:** Loss of wealth through women

## 2. Liaison with Others' Wives (X.4)

- Venus in Varga of Mars or Saturn
- Venus with aspect of Mars or Saturn
→ Indicates affairs leading to divorce

## 3. Weak 7th Lord Dasa (XX.18)

- Distress to son-in-law
- Separation from beloved
- Trouble through opposite sex
- Intrigues with wicked women
- Diseases in private parts
- Wandering

## 4. BTR Application

1. **Divorce event → Dasa of weak 7th lord or malefic afflicting 7th**
2. **Venus + malefic connection:** Key divorce indicator
3. **Saturn/Mars in 7th:** May indicate divorce if other factors align
4. **Weak 7th lord Dasa = period of marital breakdown**

## Rule: Divorce as BTR Event

Divorce is listed as one of the **highly reliable** BTR events because:
- It has a **specific, verifiable date** (court decree, legal separation date)
- It has a **strong house signature**
- It is a **major life turning point** with legal documentation

---

## Rule: Separation / Loss House Correspondence

Separation and loss are judged through **adverse combinations** of houses. The source states:

> "Separation or loss through the relevant adverse combinations"

**Applicable houses for separation/divorce**:
- **7th house** — Partnership dissolution (the primary relationship house)
- **8th house** — Break, transformation, separation
- **12th house** — Loss, separation, endings
- **6th house** — Disputes, conflict, litigation

The relevant Cusp Sub-Lord should signify houses associated with separation/breakage rather than partnership/union houses.

---

## Rule: Divorce Verification Logic

> "Separation or loss through the relevant adverse combinations."

This confirms that separation events test **adverse house combinations** — the opposite of the union houses (2, 7, 11) that govern marriage. A correct birth time must explain known divorce/separation events through CSLs signifying loss/breakage houses (6, 8, 12) during the relevant Dasha periods.

---

## Rule: Separation vs. Divorce Classification

The source lists both "separation" and "divorce" as usable BTR events:
- **Separation**: May involve physical separation without legal dissolution
- **Divorce**: Legal dissolution of marriage

Both are 7th house matters, but divorce has stronger legal documentation and is therefore a more reliable BTR datapoint.

## PVR Part 2: Classical Divorce Indicators (Upapada-based)

**Source:** *Vedic Astrology: An Integrated Approach* by P.V.R. Narasimha Rao (2000), Part 2 (Pages 101-200)

---

### 1. Divorce Indications from Upapada Lagna (UL)

> **Page 103:**
> "The 8th house from UL shows the longevity of marriage and the 2nd and 7th houses from UL show the end of marriage. Malefics like Mars, Saturn and nodes in these houses from UL can result in troubles for the marriage and even a divorce."

**Rule:** Divorce is indicated when:
- **Malefics (Mars, Saturn, Rahu, Ketu)** occupy the **2nd, 7th, or 8th from UL**
- The **2nd and 7th from UL** specifically show the **end of marriage**
- Strong malefic influence in these positions → high probability of divorce

### 2. Distinction Between Spouse Quality and Marriage Longevity

> **Page 104:**
> "Nature of spouse and the length of the marriage are two different issues. If Mars and Saturn are in UL and Jupiter is in the 8th from UL, it may show a long marriage to an argumentative and mean spouse. If Jupiter is in UL and Mars and Saturn in the 8th from it, one may have a noble spouse but the marital life will be very rough and there can even be a divorce."

**Rule:** A chart may have:
- **Good spouse + stormy marriage:** Benefic in UL, malefics in 8th from UL
- **Difficult spouse + long marriage:** Malefics in UL, benefic in 8th from UL

**BTR Application:** When verifying a known divorce event, check that the 2nd and 7th houses from UL are afflicted by functional malefics AND that the Dasa running at divorce time involves a maraka or dusthana lord connected to these houses.

## Rule PVR-DIV-1: Dasas of 2nd and 7th from UL Bring Marital Troubles/Divorce
**Source:** PVR, pp. 259-260, 269 (Examples 72, 29)
**Rule:** In Navamsa Narayana dasa, dasas of the 2nd and 7th houses from UL (upapada) can bring troubles in marriage and even a divorce when the chart has such indications. The 1st, 3rd, and 8th houses from UL show its birth, vitality, and life (longevity), while the 2nd and 7th houses from it show its end.

**Specific divorce indicators:**
- Dasa of a rasi that is the 2nd from UL = shows the end of marriage
- Ketu in the 2nd from UL + malefic aspects (Sun, Mars, Rahu) = divorce
- Sun's aspect on 2nd from UL can denote father's influence in the break-up
- The 2nd from UL afflicted by malefics = strong divorce indicator

**BTR Application:** For divorce/separation events, compute Navamsa Narayana dasa. The divorce should occur in the dasa of a rasi that is the 2nd (end of marriage) or 7th (partnership dissolution) from UL. Verify that the rasi is afflicted by malefics or contains Ketu.
**Examples:** Example 72 (principle stated), Exercise 29/Chart 30 (Vi dasa = 2nd from UL in Navamsa ND, with Ketu + Sun/Mars/Rahu aspects → divorce in June 2000)

---

## Rule PVR-DIV-2: UL Rules in Narayana Dasa for Marital State
**Source:** PVR, pp. 251, 260 (Sections 18.4, Example 72)
**Rule:** In rasi chart Narayana dasa, dasa of upapada (UL) may bring marriage. Dasas of the 2nd house or the 7th house from upapada may bring troubles in marriage. This is a general Narayana dasa principle applicable to both marriage events and marital breakdowns.
**BTR Application:** For a divorce event, check the rasi chart Narayana dasa as well. If the running dasa is the 2nd or 7th from UL, and the chart has divorce indications (malefics in 7th, 8th lord in 7th, etc.), this supports divorce timing.

---

## Rule PVR-DIV-3: 7th from Dasa Lagna Affliction = Marital Problems
**Source:** PVR, p. 251 (Section 18.4)
**Rule:** If the 7th house from dasa lagna and paaka rasi is afflicted by malefics, there may be troubles in marriage.
**BTR Application:** During the period when marital troubles led to divorce, verify that in the rasi chart Narayana dasa, the 7th house from the running dasa lagna and the paaka rasi were afflicted by malefics (Saturn, Mars, Rahu, Ketu). This provides supporting evidence for the divorce timing.

## 1. Divorce Antardasa in Kalachakra — 8th House from Lagna + 7th from UL

**Source**: Ch.24, Exercise 36, pages 317-318, answer page 322
**Pages**: 317-318, 322

**Rule**: In Kalachakra dasa, divorce occurs in antardasa of a rasi that is:
- The 8th house from lagna (marital troubles, quarrels)
- AND/OR the 7th house from upapada (UL) (end of marriage)
- The rasi should be strong in SAV (evil houses when strong bring evil results)

**Example**: A lady married in Ge antardasa (Feb 1992) and divorced in Li antardasa (late 1995), both within the same Sg dasa.
- Li is the 8th house from lagna → marital troubles
- Li is the 7th house from upapada → end of marriage
- In navamsa, Li had 30 rekhas in SAV → strong evil house

**Supporting analysis**:
- Navamsa 7th house (Vi) had only 22 rekhas (weak), while 8th house (Li) had 30 rekhas (strong)
- The 8th house being stronger than the 7th confirms divorce over continued marriage

**BTR Application**: For divorce event verification with Kalachakra dasa:
1. Identify the antardasa running at divorce time
2. Check if the antardasa rasi = 8th house from lagna OR 7th from UL
3. Check SAV strength of the antardasa rasi in navamsa — 30+ rekhas in an evil house confirms evil results
4. UL and A7 in navamsa are key reference points for marriage/divorce analysis

## 2. Separation Indicators via Muntha in Annual Chart

**Source**: Ch.28, pages 386-387
**Pages**: 386-387

**Rule**: Muntha in the 7th house of the annual chart gives troubles in marriage and many hardships. Muntha in the 4th house gives disputes and loss of position.

**BTR Application**: In the Tajaka annual chart for the year of separation/divorce, check that Muntha fell in the 7th house (marital troubles) or 4th house (domestic disputes).
