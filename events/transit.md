# Aquamoonlight Transit-Based Event Rectification Rules

**Source:** Aquamoonlight Astrology — Chart Rectification
**URL:** https://www.aquamoonlight.co.uk/rectification.html
**Retrieved:** 2026-05-24

---

## Rule: Transit Analysis Requires ≥10 Events
**Source:** Aquamoonlight Rectification
**URL:** https://www.aquamoonlight.co.uk/rectification.html
**Rule:** At least **ten life events** should be analysed for transit-based rectification to be definable with regards to most likely timing. Exact dates and times are best due to the accuracy of planetary positions. Just knowing a year is not of much use.

---

## Rule: Best Event Types for Transit Rectification
**Source:** Aquamoonlight Rectification
**URL:** https://www.aquamoonlight.co.uk/rectification.html
**Rule:** Accidents, traumatic incidents, major illnesses and the like are the **best kind** of events to use because they are peculiar to that person rather than general to a large number. Include:
- Marriage, divorce
- Childbirth
- Death of a parent or other close relative
- Change of employment, promotion
- Any other major change in circumstances affecting the native

Events should be **spaced out as much as possible** over the years — this becomes even more important if using Progressions or Solar Arcs for rectification.

---

## Rule: Major Transit Over Ascendant/MC Axis
**Source:** Aquamoonlight Rectification
**URL:** https://www.aquamoonlight.co.uk/rectification.html
**Rule:** Major life events are most often shown by a **major transit over the Ascendant and/or MC axis** or in aspect to it, though often will be found to have correlation to the natal planets too. The angles (Ascendant/Descendant axis and MC/IC axis) change by about **one degree every four minutes** and are very sensitive to major transits.

**Event Check:** When a major life event occurred, check whether a slow planet (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, Pluto) was transiting the natal Ascendant, Descendant, MC, or IC — or in hard aspect (conjunction, square, opposition) to these angles.

---

## Rule: Transit Event Graph Plotting
**Source:** Aquamoonlight Rectification
**URL:** https://www.aquamoonlight.co.uk/rectification.html
**Rule:** Erect **event charts** and construct a graph plotting the planetary positions for each event. This enables easy identification of **degrees that occur most prominently** — they will peak in the graph. The object is to identify **sensitive points** which are likely to be representative of the angles of the true natal chart.

**Procedure:** For each life event, note the position of each planet. Plot these on a degree-by-degree graph. Degrees that appear repeatedly across multiple events are candidate Ascendant/MC degrees.

---

## Rule: Quadruplicity (Quality) Detection
**Source:** Aquamoonlight Rectification
**URL:** https://www.aquamoonlight.co.uk/rectification.html
**Rule:** When it is noted that particular degrees are most prominent in event charts, and in particular **qualities (quadruplicities)** — i.e. cardinal, fixed or mutable signs — then the chances are that the most likely Ascendant and Midheaven have been found. At any given latitude, the Ascendant degree is always **relative to the degree of the Midheaven**. By adjusting the birth chart in terms of the ascending degree and sign, it will automatically adjust the sign and degree of the Midheaven.

**Application:** If the two most prominent degrees shown by life events are set so that one is the Ascendant and the other is the MC, and it works at that latitude, then this should show the natal positions of these angles.

---

## Rule: Natal Planet Conjunction Complication
**Source:** Aquamoonlight Rectification
**URL:** https://www.aquamoonlight.co.uk/rectification.html
**Rule:** If the sensitive points found through event analysis happen to be in the same position as a **natal planet**, this complicates the rectification. If the planet is conjunct or in a **fourth harmonic aspect** (conjunction, square, opposition) to the true natal angle, it can overshadow the angle itself and obscure it from being identified as the Ascendant/MC. If no other sensitive points are located, then this is most likely the case.

---

## Rule: Inner Planets on Angles in Event Charts
**Source:** Aquamoonlight Rectification
**URL:** https://www.aquamoonlight.co.uk/rectification.html
**Rule:** The main planets emphasised in event charts are the **inner planets (Sun through Mars)** — these are most often on the angles or in major aspect to them at event times, even if they do not appear to make any major aspects to the natal planets.

**Timing Precision:** If the exact date of an event is known, the Sun, Mercury, Venus and Mars can be used with good effect. If the exact date is **not** known, disregard inner planets and rely on outer planets (Jupiter through Pluto) and Chiron.

---

## Rule: Hard Aspects (4th Harmonic) in Transit Events
**Source:** Aquamoonlight Rectification
**URL:** https://www.aquamoonlight.co.uk/rectification.html
**Rule:** In any event, the **harder aspects** (square, opposition) usually have a much more powerful impact and are more likely to produce events best remembered. These are the aspects of the **fourth harmonic** — conjunction, square, opposition — replicating the possible angles of the chart.

**Event Check:** When testing a candidate birth time against events, prioritise transits forming 0°, 90°, or 180° aspects to natal angles and planets. Soft aspects (trine, sextile) are less reliable for event-based rectification.

---

## Rule: Orb Size by Planet Speed
**Source:** Aquamoonlight Rectification
**URL:** https://www.aquamoonlight.co.uk/rectification.html
**Rule:** The orbs used should be **relative to the speed of the planets involved**. Transits of the heavy, slower-moving, outer planets (Jupiter to Pluto) will have an allowance **greater** than that of the faster-moving inner planets (Sun through Mars). Faster planets move through degrees quicker, so their influence is more tightly confined to exact aspect.

---

## Rule: Default Chart — Sun on Ascendant (No Birth Time)
**Source:** Aquamoonlight Rectification
**URL:** https://www.aquamoonlight.co.uk/rectification.html
**Rule:** When birth time is completely unknown, it is usual to erect a chart placing the **Sun on the Ascendant** (a dawn chart). Alternatively, the chart can be set for **midday** (12 hours either side). Neither is acceptable for Progressions, Astrocartography, or Arabic Parts — these require rectified birth times.

This can be a prime indicator of the ascending sign.

- **Conjunct the MC/IC axis**, OR
- **Conjunct its natal position** (the Lunar Return)

**Application:** If the natal Moon position can be determined to the most likely degree through event analysis, this narrows down the possibilities of the Ascendant and Midheaven considerably. Find which Moon degree is hit repeatedly by transiting Moon during major events — that degree is likely the natal Moon position.

# Jataka Parijata Vol II — Transit (Gochara) Rules

**Source:** Jataka Parijata Vol II, Adhyaya X (Ashtakavarga Gochara)
**Author:** Vaidyanatha Dikshita (Tr. V. Subrahmanya Sastri, 1932)
**Extracted:** 2026-05-24

---

## 1. Gochara Effect via Ashtakavarga (Sloka 3-9)

The effect of a planet's arrival in any bhava in its progress through the orbit (Gochara) is:
- **Benefic** → if benefic dots in that Rasi's Bhinnashtakavarga > 4
- **Mixed** → if benefic dots = 4
- **Malefic** → if benefic dots < 4

---

## 2. Upachaya Enhancement (Sloka 10)
If a planet has more benefic dots AND is posited in an Upachaya house (3, 6, 10, 11 from Lagna or Moon):
→ The benefic influence is even greater and more marked

---

## 3. Apachaya Reduction (Sloka 11)
If a planet has more benefic dots BUT is in an Apachaya house (depression, inimical, or the remaining houses):
→ The good effect is considerably reduced

If there are more malefic strokes instead of benefic dots → worsened evil effect.

---

## 4. Reference Point (Notes)
Upachaya/Apachaya places are reckoned with respect to the Lagna or Moon at birth, NOT by the planet's transit position. Good and bad places are determined at birth-time only.

---

## 5. Dasa Lord Transit (Adhyaya XVIII, Sloka 6-8)
The Lagna at commencement of a Dasa determines quality. The Moon's transit position relative to the Dasa lord determines whether the Dasa brings prosperity or damage to specific bhavas.

---

## 6. BTR Application for Transits

Transits provide precise timing confirmation for BTR:
1. **At the time of any life event**, check the transit position of Jupiter (for benefic events) and Saturn (for challenging events)
2. Compute Ashtakavarga for the candidate birth time — verify that transits at event date had dots > 4 in relevant bhavas for positive events
3. Saturn's transit through Rasis with 0 dots in a planet's Ashtakavarga indicates death of the relation signified by that planet (Sloka 51)
4. Moon's transit position relative to Dasa lord determines event quality

## 1. Transits from Moon (Janma Rasi) — Tables 53-59

**Source**: Ch.25, pages 327-330 (Tables 53-59)
**Pages**: 327-330

**Rule**: The rasi occupied by Moon in the natal chart is called janma rasi. Planets give different results when transiting in different houses from janma rasi. The standard classical results are tabulated for each planet.

**Key transit principles**:
- Results depend on what the planet stands for in the natal chart, not just the generic table
- Example: If Mars is 5th lord, worries during Mars transit in 8th from janma rasi pertain to children
- Transit of Vimsottari dasa lord is particularly important for mental state
- Results in literature are reference points — adapt intelligently to the chart

**BTR Application**: When verifying events by transit, consider:
1. Which house from janma rasi each planet transited
2. Whether that transit is favorable or unfavorable per tables
3. What the transit planet signifies in the natal chart
4. The interaction determines the event's nature

## 2. Transits from Lagna and Paaka Lagna

**Source**: Ch.25, Section 25.3, pages 331-332
**Pages**: 331-332

**Rule**: Transits can be analyzed from lagna (vitality/personality) and paaka lagna (physical self):
- A malefic transiting in janma rasi → mental worries
- A malefic transiting in natal lagna → affects vitality, creates obstacles
- A malefic transiting in paaka lagna → bodily complaints
- Transit Jupiter in the 7th from lagna can give marriage
- Transit Saturn in the 7th from lagna can give relationship/marital problems

**Transit aspects**: Also consider aspects cast by transiting planets:
- Jupiter in Vi aspects Ta (7th from Sc lagna) → can give marriage during Vi transit
- A planet occupying or aspecting a rasi influences matters signified by houses/planets in that rasi in natal chart

**BTR Application**: For transit verification of any event:
1. Check transits from both janma rasi (mental) and lagna (physical)
2. Use aspects by transit planets to natal houses
3. Cross-reference with paaka lagna for physical manifestations

## 3. Transit-Divisional Chart Interaction

**Source**: Ch.25, Section 25.4, pages 334-341
**Pages**: 334-341

**Rule**: Two critical interactions for event timing:

**(A) Natal divisional chart + Transit rasi chart** → Coarse timing of events
- Shows how innate potential in a specific life area is influenced at a given time
- Example: Natal D-9 + transit rasi for marriage timing

**(B) Natal rasi chart + Transit divisional chart** → Fine timing
- Shows how physical-level potential transforms into an event in a specific area
- Example: Natal rasi + transit D-11 for death timing

**BTR Application**:
- For coarse timing: check transit rasi planets activating natal divisional chart houses
- For fine timing: check transit divisional charts activating natal rasi positions
- Use multiple divisional charts for different event types (D-9 for marriage, D-10 for career, D-7 for children, D-11 for death)

## 4. Transit Ashtakavarga for Event Timing

**Source**: Ch.25, Section 25.5, pages 342-349
**Pages**: 342-349

**Rule**: The most important purpose of Ashtakavarga is transit interpretation.

**BAV method**:
- If a planet has 5+ rekhas in its transit rasi in its BAV (out of 8 references) → good results
- 3 or less rekhas → bad results
- 6-7 rekhas → excellent results
- 1-0 rekhas → very poor results

**SAV method**:
- Rasis with 30+ rekhas in SAV → favorable transit results
- Rasis with less than 25 rekhas → unfavorable transit results
- SAV dominates over individual BAV when it has too many or too few rekhas

**D-10 example (pages 343-345)**: Vajpayee becoming PM — 6/7 planets in rasis with 30+ SAV rekhas in D-10.

**Kakshyas (pages 345-348)**: Each rasi divided into 8 kakshyas of 3°45'. PAV shows which kakshya lord each planet is benefic with respect to. Used for fine-tuning predictions to specific days.

**BTR Application**: For any event:
1. Check each important planet's BAV rekhas at its transit rasi
2. Check SAV strength of transit rasis
3. For day-specific events, check Kakshya/PAV activation
4. Use divisional chart SAV for area-specific events (D-10 for career, D-24 for education)

## 5. Murthis (Forms) — Transit Quality

**Source**: Ch.26, Section 26.2, Table 62, pages 358-359
**Pages**: 358-359

**Rule**: At the moment a planet enters a rasi, the house transit Moon occupies from natal Moon determines the planet's murthi (form) during its entire transit of that rasi:

| House from Natal Moon | Murthi | Results |
|----------------------|--------|---------|
| 1st, 6th, 11th | Swarna (Golden) | Highly favorable |
| 2nd, 5th, 9th | Rajata (Silver) | Favorable |
| 3rd, 7th, 10th | Taamra (Copper) | Unfavorable |
| 4th, 8th, 12th | Loha (Iron) | Highly unfavorable |

**BTR Application**: When a planet is expected to give good results but gives marginal ones, or vice versa, check its murthi. A golden form planet gives full results even in a moderately favorable transit. An iron form planet gives poor results even in a favorable transit.

## 6. Rasi Gochara Vedha — Transit Obstruction

**Source**: Ch.26, Section 26.3, Table 63, pages 359-360
**Pages**: 359-360

**Rule**: Even when a planet transits a favorable house from natal Moon, it may be "obstructed" by another planet transiting the vedha sthana (house of obstruction).

**Key vedha pairs**:
- Sun's good transits (3, 6, 10, 11) → vedha from (9, 12, 4, 5)
- Moon's good transits → vedha from (5, 9, 12, 2, 4, 8)
- Jupiter's good transits (2, 5, 7, 9, 11) → vedha from (12, 4, 3, 10, 8)

**Exceptions**: Sun and Saturn do not cause vedha on each other. Moon and Mercury do not cause vedha on each other.

**BTR Application**: When an event was expected but did not materialize, check if the activating planet had vedha from another planet at the critical transit time.

## 10. Latta (Kick) — Transit Kicks on Nakshatras

**Source**: Ch.26, Section 26.7, pages 366-368
**Pages**: 366-368

**Rule**: Each planet has latta (kick) on specific constellations. If a planet has latta on janma nakshatra or lagna nakshatra, unfavorable results related to its natal significations occur.

**Forward latta**:
- Sun: 12th nakshatra forward
- Mars: 3rd nakshatra forward
- Jupiter: 6th nakshatra forward
- Saturn: 8th nakshatra forward

**Backward latta**:
- Moon: 22nd nakshatra backward
- Mercury: 7th nakshatra backward
- Venus: 5th nakshatra backward
- Rahu: 9th nakshatra backward

**If the 6th lord has latta on janma/lagna nakshatra** → litigation/enemy troubles
**If the 7th lord has latta on janma/lagna nakshatra** → marital/spouse troubles
**If an important 10th house planet has latta on janma/lagna nakshatra** → career troubles

**BTR Application**: At any significant event time, check which planets have latta on janma/lagna nakshatra. The common house of those planets in the natal chart indicates the affected life area.

## 1. Saturn Transit Effects Based on Reference Point (Page 84)

PVR provides specific transit rules based on which reference point Saturn is transiting:

| Transit Over | Effect |
|-------------|--------|
| **Lagna** | Throws obstructions, hampers activities |
| **Chandra Lagna (Moon)** | Creates frustration and mental depression |
| **Paaka Lagna (Lagna Lord)** | Leaves one feeling sick, attacks physical vitality |

> **Page 84**: "Saturn's transit over the rasi containing one's lagna may throw obstructions and hamper one's activities. Saturn's transit over the rasi containing one's Chandra lagna may create frustration and mental depression. Saturn's transit over the rasi containing one's paaka lagna may leave one feeling sick all the time and attack the physical vitality."

### BTR Application
When verifying a known challenging event against a candidate birthtime:
1. Check which reference Saturn was transiting at the time
2. The event's nature should match the reference it activates:
   - Life obstruction → Lagna transit
   - Mental health crisis → Chandra lagna transit
   - Physical health crisis → Paaka lagna transit
3. This multi-reference transit principle helps disambiguate between candidate birthtimes

- Transits through the **visible half** produce events that are externally visible and publicly known
- Transits through the **invisible half** produce events that are personal and private
- An event that is highly public should involve transits through the visible half

**Transit-Dasa Integration Principle**:
1. A dasa period shows the **potential** for an event

3. For BTR: both dasa lord and transit lord at event time should connect to the same house/chart

## E1. Transit-Based Rectification — Saturn and Jupiter House Transits

**Rule (Brennan):** After narrowing down candidate rising signs, transit-based timing is one of the most effective verification methods. Saturn and Jupiter transits are particularly valuable because their natures are so distinct and their effects are consistently noticeable:

- Saturn's nature = restriction, structure, consolidation, challenges
- When Saturn transits a house, events in that house's domain become significant
- Example: Saturn transiting the 10th house → career restructuring; Saturn transiting the 7th house → relationship challenges/testing
- Cross-reference the dates Saturn was in each house with the client's known events

**Jupiter transit method:**
- Jupiter spends ~1 year in each whole sign house
- Jupiter's nature = expansion, growth, opportunity, benefit
- When Jupiter transits a house, positive developments in that domain occur
- Example: Jupiter transiting the 5th house → childbirth; Jupiter transiting the 2nd house → financial growth

**Degree-level fine-tuning:**
- Once the rising sign is confirmed, use the exact transit degree to fine-tune the Ascendant
- Major life events should coincide with transiting planets making exact aspects (0°, 90°, 180°) to natal points
- The tighter the orb, the more precise the verification

**BTR Application:** For each candidate time, compute the transit dates of Saturn and Jupiter through each house, then cross-reference against known events. The candidate time with the best house-event alignment is preferred.
