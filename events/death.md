## PVR Part 2: Classical Longevity & Death Timing Rules

**Source:** *Vedic Astrology: An Integrated Approach* by P.V.R. Narasimha Rao (2000), Part 2 (Pages 192-197)

### 1. Marakas (Killers) — Houses and Planets of Death

> **Page 192-193:**
> "The 3rd and 8th houses are the houses of life. The 3rd house shows the vitality of one's existence and the 8th house shows the longevity. The 12th house from any house shows losses related to the matters signified by that house. So the 12th house from these two houses shows death. So the 2nd and 7th houses are the houses of death."

**Key Principle:** Death is indicated by the 2nd and 7th houses (being 12th from 3rd and 12th from 8th respectively).

**Maraka Sthanas (Killer Stations):**
> "The rasis containing the 2nd and 7th houses are called maraka sthanas (killer stations). When we use rasi-ruled dasas that can show death, dasas of these rasis can bring death."

**Maraka Grahas (Killer Planets):**
> "Lords of the 2nd and 7th houses are called maraka grahas (killer planets). When we use planet-ruled dasas that can show death, dasas of these planets can bring death."

**Additional Marakas (Page 193):**
> "If a malefic planet powerfully conjoins or aspects, using graha drishti, the 2nd and 7th houses or their lords, then it qualifies as a maraka graha."

**Examples of conditional marakas (Page 193):**
- Lagna in Le, Saturn in Sg, Mars in Ge: Saturn is maraka (owning 7th). Mars aspects 2nd house and 7th lord → also a maraka
- Lagna in Pi, Mars in Ge, Mercury in Cp, Saturn in Ar: Saturn in 2nd aspects 2nd lord and 7th lord → stronger maraka than Mars/Mercury

**BTR Application:** For death events, verify the running Dasa lord is a maraka (2nd lord, 7th lord, or a malefic strongly connected to them). Transits activating maraka sthanas also confirm.

### 2. Rudra, Trishoola and Maheswara

> **Page 193-194:**
> "For every person, there are eleven rasis that represent the eleven Rudras. These rasis bring suffering related to various areas of life."

#### Rudra Calculation

> **Page 194:**
> "Consider the lord of the 8th house from (i) lagna and (ii) the 7th house. Find the 8th house using Table 32 and not in the normal way. The stronger of the two planets becomes Rudra."

**Table 32 — 8th House for Rudra Calculation (Page 194):**

|--------|-----------|
| Ar     | Sc        |
| Ta     | Ge        |
| Ge     | Cp        |
| Cn     | Sg        |
| Le     | Cn        |
| Vi     | Aq        |
| Li     | Ta        |

| Sg     | Cn        |

> **Note (Page 194, footnote):** For odd rasis, count houses zodiacally. For even rasis, count anti-zodiacally. For Brahma and Vishnu rasis, use regular motion. For Shiva rasis, use Shiva's motion.

**Strength comparison (Page 194):**
> "We say that a planet is stronger if it conjoins more planets. If both planets conjoin the same number of planets, a planet in exaltation or own rasi is stronger. A planet joining exalted planets is stronger. A planet aspected by many planets (rasi aspect) is stronger. Finally, a planet which is more advanced in its rasi is stronger."

**Exception (Page 194):**
> "If the weaker planet is debilitated or in an inimical sign and conjoined/aspected by malefics like Mars, Saturn, Rahu and Ketu, then it becomes Rudra."

#### Trishoola Rasis

> **Page 194:**
> "The three trines from the rasi occupied by Rudra in rasi chart represent the three spikes of Shiva's Trishoola/trident. They are called Trishoola rasis."

> "Depending on whether a native has short life or middle life or long life, one of the three Trishoola rasis kills the native during its Shoola dasa."

#### Maheswara

> **Page 194-195:**
> "The lord of the 8th house from AK (chara atma karaka) is called Maheswara. AK stands for the soul and the 8th lord from him stands for the liberation of soul."

**Exceptions (Page 195):**
1. If 8th lord from AK is in own/exaltation, take stronger of 8th/12th lords from him
2. If Rahu/Ketu joins AK or 8th from him, take 6th lord from AK instead of 8th lord
3. If Rahu becomes Maheswara, take Mercury instead. If Ketu becomes Maheswara, take Jupiter instead.

**BTR Application:** The Dasa of Maheswara or a Trishoola rasi can trigger death/liberation. Cross-reference with maraka periods.

### 3. The Method of Three Pairs — Longevity Estimation

> **Page 195-196:**

**Three Pairs:**
1. Lagna lord and 8th lord (using Table 32 for 8th house)
2. Moon and Saturn
3. Lagna and Hora Lagna (HL)

**Table 33 — Longevity Category (Page 195):**
| Combination 1     | Combination 2     | Result      |
|-------------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Fixed + Dual      | Movable + Movable | Long life   |
| Movable + Fixed   | Dual + Dual       | Middle life |
| Movable + Dual    | Fixed + Fixed     | Short life  |

**Longevity Ranges (Page 196):**
- **Long life:** 72-108 years
- **Middle life:** 36-72 years
- **Short life:** 0-36 years

**Combining three pairs (Page 196-197):**
> "If all the three pairs result in the same longevity category, that will be the combined classification. If two pairs give one result and the third pair gives a different result, then the result given by two pairs dominates."

**Table 34 — Paramaayush Reckoner (Page 196):**

**If all three give different results (Page 197):**
> "Give preference to the third pair of lagna and horalagna. However, if Moon is in lagna or the 7th house, then the second pair of Moon and Saturn should be given preference."

### 4. The Eighth Lord Method

> **Page 197:**
> "Find the stronger of lagna and 7th house and take it as the reference. Find the 8th lord from it and see where he is placed from it."

- If 8th lord is in a **quadrant** from reference → **Long life**
- If 8th lord is in a **panaphara** from reference → **Middle life**
- If 8th lord is in an **apoklima** from reference → **Short life**

**Caution (Page 197):**
> "The two methods outlined here for determining the longevity category are not infallible. There are some exceptions not covered in this book."

# Rules from Astro Secrets & Krishnamurti Padhdhati Part IV — Chunk 4

## Rule: K.S.K. Life/Death Determination via Sub-Lord of Relative's House Cusp
**Source:** Astro Secrets & KP Part IV, Chunk 4 (final), page 189 (K.P. Reader VI page 160)
**Rule:** To determine if a missing person is alive or dead: Take the house representing the relationship. If the sublord of the cusp is in the constellation of a planet who is the significator of badhak and marak sthanas, boldly declare that he is dead. If the sublord of the cusp of the house representing the relative is in the constellation of the lord of that house or the significators of the house representing that relation, he is alive. This rule applies mutatis mutandis to the sub-sublord also.

## Rule: Cause of Death Determination by Element of Significator's Sign
**Source:** Astro Secrets & KP Part IV, Chunk 4 (final), page 190 (K.P. Reader VI page 106)
**Rule:** The significator's position determines cause of death: (1) Watery sign → drowning; (2) Fiery sign → injuries; (3) Earthy sign → natural end; (4) Airy sign → inferred as firing/gun shots (since Sun denotes fire and airy sign denotes communication of fire). In the worked example, Budha in Uttarashada whose lord Sun is in Aquarius (airy sign) led to the conclusion of death by gun shots.

### Rule 1: Nakshatra Verification (Birth Constellation Check)

**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology, Ch.11 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-11-Birth-Verification-and-Rectification.html
**Rule:** "Multiply the time of birth in vighatis by 4 and divide the product by 9. The remainder must give the ruling constellation when counted from Aswini, Makha or Moola." — B.V. Raman

**Formula:** (Vighatis × 4) ÷ 9 → remainder → count from Aswini/Makha/Moola = birth nakshatra
**Purpose:** Confirm birth time is consistent with the Moon's birth star
**Worked example:** 2033 vighatis × 4 = 8132 ÷ 9 = remainder 5 → 5th from Aswini = Mrigasira ✓

### Three Nakshatra Groups

| Remainder | From Aswini (G1) | From Makha (G2) | From Moola (G3) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Aswini | Makha | Moola |

### Rule 2: Lagna Verification (Ascendant Check)

**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology, Ch.11 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-11-Birth-Verification-and-Rectification.html
**Rule:** "Multiply the number of ghatis from birth by 6 and add the longitude of the Sun (the number of degrees passed in the sign). Divide the sum by 30. The quotient plus 1 counted from the Sun's sign will give the rising sign." — B.V. Raman

**Formula (ghatis):** (Ghatis × 6 + Sun° in sign) ÷ 30 → quotient + 1 from Sun's sign = Ascendant
**Formula (hours):** (Hours × 15 + Sun° in sign) ÷ 30 → quotient + 1 from Sun's sign = Ascendant
**Worked example:** 33.88 ghatis × 6 = 203.28 + 24° (Sun in Cancer) = 227.28 ÷ 30 = q=7 → 8 from Cancer = Aquarius ✓
**Caveat:** Approximate check to verify Ascendant **sign**, not exact degrees.

### Rule 3: Trinal Relationship Check

**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology, Ch.11 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-11-Birth-Verification-and-Rectification.html

The 7th from the sign occupied by the lord of the Moon's sign or the 5th or the 9th from the similar sign and in some cases the sign where the Moon is at radix itself becomes the ascendant." — B.V. Raman

**Check:** Asc should be 5th, 9th, or 7th from the Moon's sign lord's position
**Rationale:** Houses 1, 5, 9 form the Dharma Trikona — the Ascendant lord and Moon's sign lord should be harmoniously related.

### Cascade Effect of Time Error

| Time Error | Ascendant Effect | Dasa Balance Effect | Divisional Chart Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1-2 min | Degree shifts slightly | Days change | Usually unchanged |
| 5-10 min | Degree shifts noticeably | Weeks to months change | Navamsa (D-9) may change |
| 15-30 min | May cross Navamsa boundary | Months change significantly | Multiple divisional charts shift |
| 1-2 hrs | Likely changes sign entirely | Entire Dasa may change | Nearly all charts affected |
| 4+ hrs | Completely different Lagna | Different Dasa lord | Entirely different horoscope |

**Formula:** See bphs-rules.md for detailed calculation. Key point: 4 reductions apply (combustion, enemy sign, visible half, malefic in ascend), but only the highest reduction is applied.

**BTR Application:** Pindayu calculation provides a longevity estimate that can be cross-checked against the native's actual lifespan at death.

### 2. Nisargayu System (Slokas 16-17)
**Base years:** Moon=1, Mars=2, Mercury=9, Venus=20, Jupiter=18, Sun=20, Saturn=50. Same rectifications as Pindayu.

### 3. Amsayu System (Slokas 18-22)
**Calculation:** Multiply longitude by 108, expunge 12s, remainder = years. Treble if exalted/own sign, double if own Navamsa/decanate.

## Rule: Son's Death Analysis via Fifth House as Lagna
**Source:** KP Reader Part 3, Chapter 1 (lines 3115-3127)
**Rule:** "Take the 5th house of this chart as the first house representing his son. Then calculate. Ascertain the date of death taking Bhadka and Marakasthanams."

**Application:** For death of a child, rotate the chart so the 5th house becomes the 1st house (lagna of the child). Then apply Bhadaka and Maraka rules from that new lagna. The Dasa period running at death must be of a planet connected to these derived death houses.

- If the lord of the 8th or 12th house is very weak → death expected during the Bhukti of either

## 2. Fatal Planet Identification (XX.31)

Among the group: Saturn, Mandi, Rahu, lord of 22nd decanate, lord of 8th house, and lords of Navamsa Rasis occupied by these planets:
- Find the **weakest** planet
- Only the Dasa of that planet will prove fatal
- Death occurs when Saturn transits an untoward house (6th, 8th, or 12th)

### Alternative Fatal Pair Method (XX.32)
From two sets:
1. Lord of house occupied by 8th lord + Lord of Navamsa occupied by 8th lord
2. Lord of 22nd decanate + Lord of rising decanate
→ Find stronger in each pair. When Dasa of stronger is in progress AND Jupiter transits Rasi/Navamsa of 8th lord or its trines → death.

## 3. Death Timing via Transit (XVII.1-28)

### Saturn Transit Death Indicators (XVII.1-2, 10, 13, 15, 17-18, 21, 23-26)
Death when Saturn transits:
- Rasi and Navamsa occupied by lord of 8th or 12th house from any Bhava → total destruction of that Bhava

- Rasi indicated by sum of 6th + 12th + 8th lords
- Rasi triangular to Gulika (night-birth) or 7th from Gulika (day-birth)
- Rasi/Amsa occupied by Saturn at birth or its trines
- Rasi/Amsa occupied by Moon (night-birth) or 7th/5th/9th from Sun (day-birth)
- Rasi so far removed from Mandi as Mandi is removed from 8th lord

Death when Jupiter transits:
- Rasi/Amsa occupied by rising decanate lord, 8th lord, or 22nd decanate lord
- Rasi indicated by: Lagna lord - Yamakantaka, Lagna+Sun+Mandi aggregate
- Rasi/Navamsa indicated by Jupiter+Rahu sum
- Rasi indicated by difference of lord of rising sign and Yamakantaka

Death when Sun transits:
- His own Dwadasamsa Rasi
- Navamsa of 8th lord or Lagna lord
- 6th, 7th, or 12th from Venus at birth

Death when Moon transits:
- Rasi/Navamsa of 8th lord or Sun
- Rasi of 22nd decanate lord (from Moon at birth) or its trines
- Lagna Rasi, 8th house, or 12th house

## Chapter VI: Balarishta (Early Death)

### Stanza 1: Sandhyakala Birth
If birth occurs in Sandhyakala (twilight periods — junctions of day/night) at the end of the 8th or the beginning of the 9th Navamsa of signs, the child would die soon after birth.

If at birth the Moon and three malefic planets occupy the 6th, 7th, or 8th houses — the child will die soon after birth.

### Stanza 2: Malefics in Kendras
If malefic planets at birth occupy the Lagna or the 4th or the 7th house — child will die soon after birth.

If malefic planets occupy the 12th house and no benefic planet aspects — child dies.

### Stanza 3-4: Balarishta Degrees
If Moon in 6th, 7th, or 8th house with malefics occupying certain degrees from Moon — child dies.

If Moon in 8th house at birth — child will not live long.

### Stanzas 5-6: Balarishta by House Occupation
- Benefics in Kendras → child lives
- Malefics in Kendras without benefic aspect → child dies soon after birth
- Benefic planet in 5th house → child dies 4 years after birth (but if benefic aspects 5th lord → a month after birth)

### Stanzas 8-10: Moon and Planet Combinations for Balarishta
- Moon in last Navamsa of a sign and malefics in 7th → dies soon after birth
- Eclipsed Moon in Lagna or 7th → dies soon after birth
- Saturn, Sun, Moon in 8th → dies soon after birth (if no benefic aspect)

### Stanza 11: Moon with Malefics
Moon accompanied by malefics in anyone house → child dies soon after birth. Unless aspected by Jupiter.

### Stanza 12: Balarishta Protection
If Moon in Lagna or 7th with malefics, but benefics aspect the Moon or Lagna → child protected.

### Stanza 13: Waning Moon
If waning Moon at birth or birth occurs during Krishna Paksha (dark fortnight) — Balarishta indicated if no benefic aspect.

### Stanza 14-15: Benefic Breakthroughs
If at birth a benefic planet successfully aspects the Moon or Lagna — death overcome. Seven types of Balarishta cancellation are described.

## Chapter XXV: Death

### Stanza 1: Death Timing
If at birth the 8th house is occupied by a malefic planet, death will occur in the Dasa of that planet. If occupied by a benefic, death occurs in the Dasa of a planet in the 8th or in the sign of the 8th lord, whichever is powerful.

### Stanzas 2-5: Death Significators
- Sun and Moon in 8th → death by royal punishment (if aspected by malefics)
- Saturn in 8th → death by chronic disease
- 5th or 9th lord with malefics → death by snake bite or poison
- 4th lord in 8th with malefics → death by water

### Stanzas 6-8: Manner of Death
- 8th, 10th, 4th lords connected → death in battle
- 4th, 7th, 10th lords connected → death by drowning
- Sun in 8th with malefic → death by weapons/fire
- Moon in 8th with malefic → death by water

### Stanzas 10-15: Post-Death Destination (Transmigration)
Determined from:
- Lord of Drekkana of powerful planet (Sun or Moon) → origin of soul
- If Jupiter → from Devaloka
- If Moon or Venus → from Pitriloka
- If Sun or Mars → from lower animal world
- If Saturn or Mercury → from hell region

**Formula:**
```
Nakshatras_passed_by_Graha_from_Aswini ÷ 3
= Remainder × 4 = Years contributed by Graha
```

**Step-by-step:**
1. Count how many Nakshatras (from Aswini) the Graha has already passed through
2. Divide by 3, keep the **remainder** (discard quotient)
3. Multiply remainder by **4** → number of years contributed
4. For months/days: use the portion of the current Nakshatra Pada the Graha has traversed

Lagna_years = (Ghatikas_elapsed_in_Lagna × 9) ÷ Rising_period_of_Rasi
