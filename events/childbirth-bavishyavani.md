### Rule 1: Nakshatra Verification (Birth Constellation Check)

**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology, Ch.11 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-11-Birth-Verification-and-Rectification.html
**Rule:** "Multiply the time of birth in vighatis by 4 and divide the product by 9. The remainder must give the ruling constellation when counted from Aswini, Makha or Moola." — B.V. Raman

**Formula:** (Vighatis × 4) ÷ 9 → remainder → count from Aswini/Makha/Moola = birth nakshatra
**Purpose:** Confirm birth time is consistent with the Moon's birth star
**Worked example:** 2033 vighatis × 4 = 8132 ÷ 9 = remainder 5 → 5th from Aswini = Mrigasira ✓
**Rectification:** Work backwards — determine what vighati value produces the correct remainder for the known nakshatra, adjust birth time accordingly.

**Three Nakshatra Groups:**

| Remainder | From Aswini (G1) | From Makha (G2) | From Moola (G3) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Aswini | Makha | Moola |
| 2 | Bharani | Pubba | Poorvashadha |
| 3 | Krittika | Uttara | Uttarashadha |
| 4 | Rohini | Hasta | Sravana |
| 5 | Mrigasira | Chitta | Dhanishta |
| 6 | Aridra | Swati | Satabhisha |
| 7 | Punarvasu | Visakha | Poorvabhadra |
| 8 | Pushyami | Anuradha | Uttarabhadra |
| 9 (or 0) | Aslesha | Jyeshta | Revati |

### Rule 2: Lagna Verification (Ascendant Check)

**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology, Ch.11 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-11-Birth-Verification-and-Rectification.html
**Rule:** "Multiply the number of ghatis from birth by 6 and add the longitude of the Sun (the number of degrees passed in the sign). Divide the sum by 30. The quotient plus 1 counted from the Sun's sign will give the rising sign." — B.V. Raman

**Formula (ghatis):** (Ghatis × 6 + Sun° in sign) ÷ 30 → quotient + 1 from Sun's sign = Ascendant
**Formula (hours):** (Hours × 15 + Sun° in sign) ÷ 30 → quotient + 1 from Sun's sign = Ascendant
**Worked example:** 33.88 ghatis × 6 = 203.28 + 24° (Sun in Cancer) = 227.28 ÷ 30 = q=7 → 8 from Cancer = Aquarius ✓
**Caveat:** Approximate check to verify Ascendant **sign**, not exact degrees.

---

### Rule 3: Trinal Relationship Check

**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology, Ch.11 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-11-Birth-Verification-and-Rectification.html
**Rule:** "The 5th or the 9th sign from the house occupied by the lord of the sign in which the Moon is placed becomes the Janma Lagna or the ascendant. The 7th from the sign occupied by the lord of the Moon's sign or the 5th or the 9th from the similar sign and in some cases the sign where the Moon is at radix itself becomes the ascendant." — B.V. Raman

**Check:** Asc should be 5th, 9th, or 7th from the Moon's sign lord's position
**Rationale:** Houses 1, 5, 9 form the Dharma Trikona — the Ascendant lord and Moon's sign lord should be harmoniously related.
**Usage:** Less mechanical than Rules 1 & 2. Allows multiple valid Ascendant signs. Best used as a filtering tool — failure strongly suggests error.

| Time Error | Ascendant Effect | Dasa Balance Effect | Divisional Chart Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1-2 min | Degree shifts slightly | Days change | Usually unchanged |

## Rule: Cuspal Sub Lord as Full Significator for Childbirth
**Source:** KP Reader Part 3, Chapter 39 (lines 20240-20285)
**Rule:** "According to our K.P. System, the sub lord of the 5th cusp should signify houses 2 or 5 or 11th for the prospects of child birth."

**The "Secret of Cuspal Sub Lord" — Full Significator Rule:**
Even if the 5th cusp sub lord negates 5th house matters (e.g., Ketu in a barren sign, in 12th house), it qualifies itself as a FULL SIGNIFICATOR of that cuspal results IN THE ABSENCE OF PLANETS IN ITS STARS.

**Worked Example from KP Part 3:**
- 5th cusp: Aries — Sign Lord Mars, Star Lord Venus, Sub Lord Ketu
- Ketu in 12th house, in sign of Mars, in star of Mercury (lord of 10th)
- Ketu is normally abortive, in barren sign → appears to deny childbirth
- BUT: No planets in Ketu's stars (Aswini, Makam, Moolam)
- THEREFORE: Ketu becomes a full significator of 5th cusp results
- RESULT: Female child born on 10-8-1990 during Venus Dasa Jupiter Bhukti Saturn Anthra

**BTR Application:**
1. Check if 5th CSL signifies 2, 5, or 11 → childbirth ALLOWED
2. If NOT, check if any planet occupies the 5th CSL's nakshatras
3. If NO planets in its stars → CSL itself becomes full significator → childbirth STILL ALLOWED
4. If planets ARE in its stars → childbirth DENIED for that candidate time

## Rule: 11th Cusp Sub Lord Connection to Childbirth from Spouse's Perspective
**Source:** KP Reader Part 3, Chapter 39 (lines 20279-20282)
**Rule:** "The 11th cusp (5 to 7th) sub lord is also Kethu." — The 11th house from the native is the 5th house from the 7th (spouse's 5th house). When the SAME planet rules both the 5th and 11th cusp subs, it doubly confirms the childbirth potential.

**BTR Application:** When analyzing childbirth, always also check the 11th cusp sub lord. If it matches the 5th cusp sub lord AND both have no planets in their stars, the childbirth is doubly confirmed.

## Rule: Time of Delivery Prediction from Nisheka (Conception) Chart

**Source:** Saravali Ch.8 (Conception), Slokas 44-52
**Source File:** `/data/btr-knowledge/chunks/saravali.txt`
**Classical Citation:** Saravali Ch.8, Adhanadhyaya

**Rule:**
1. Sun in Movable Sign at Nisheka → delivery in 10th lunar month
2. Sun in Fixed Sign at Nisheka → delivery in 11th lunar month
3. Sun in Dual Sign at Nisheka → delivery in 12th lunar month
4. Same rules apply to Nisheka Lagna sign type
5. Fraction of day/night for delivery determined by Nisheka Lagna being day/night sign
6. Natal Moon in 7th from Adhana Moon (per Badarayana) or at Dwadasamsa-indicated position (Kalyanavarma's accepted method)

**BTR Application:** To predict or verify childbirth timing from the native's chart, compute the Dasha period and check if it activates the 5th house (children) AND corresponds to transit configurations that mirror the Nisheka→Janma progression rules.

**Houses/Planets:** 5th house (children), Sun, Moon, Nisheka Lagna

## Rule: Delivery Month — Sign Type Classification

**Source:** Saravali Ch.8 (Conception), Slokas 44-45
**Source File:** `/data/btr-knowledge/chunks/saravali.txt`
**Classical Citation:** Saravali Ch.8

**Rule:** The month of delivery corresponds to the nature of the Nisheka (conception) Lagna:
| Nisheka Lagna Type | Delivery Month |
|-------------------|----------------|
| Movable (Chara) | 10th lunar month |
| Fixed (Sthira) | 11th lunar month |
| Dual (Dwiswabhava) | 12th lunar month |
The same classification applies to the Sun's sign at Nisheka.

**BTR Application:** When verifying a past childbirth event, the Adhana chart's Lagna type (Movable/Fixed/Dual) determines whether the gestation was normal (~10 months = ~295 days), slightly extended (~11 months = ~325 days), or longer (~12 months = ~354 days). The actual gestation period must be consistent.

## Rule: Antenatal Complications — Monthly Lord Analysis

**Source:** Saravali Ch.8 (Conception), Slokas 29-31, 35-43
**Source File:** `/data/btr-knowledge/chunks/saravali.txt`
**Classical Citation:** Saravali Ch.8

**Rule:** The ten monthly lords from conception to delivery are: Venus (1st, blood), Mars (2nd, flesh), Jupiter (3rd, limbs), Sun (4th, bones), Moon (5th, skin), Saturn (6th, hair), Mercury (7th, movement), Nisheka Lord (8th, hunger/thirst), Moon (9th, excitement), Sun (10th, ripeness). The dispositions of these lords indicate good or bad development. Known complications during specific months should correspond to affliction of the corresponding monthly lord.

**BTR Application:** Known complications during specific months of pregnancy can be mapped to particular monthly lords in the Adhana chart, providing a fine-grained verification of the conception time.
