## Overview

The D60 (Shashtiamsa) is constructed by dividing each 30° zodiac sign into **60 equal parts of exactly 0°30'** (half a degree). Because the Ascendant of this chart shifts completely **every two minutes**, it is the most birth-time-sensitive chart in all of Vedic astrology — more precise than even a fingerprint. This makes it one of the most powerful tools for Birth Time Rectification.

> Maharishi Parashara gave the Shashtiamsa a higher **Vishwabal** (weightage of strength) than even the D1 natal chart or the D9 Navamsha. — *Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra*

The D60 encodes **Prarabdha Karma** — the specific accumulated past-life deeds (from Sanchita Karma) selected for experience in the current incarnation.

**Source:** Paragraph 3

## Rule 5: Benefic vs. Malefic Shashtiamsa — Karmic Quality Filter

**Source:** Section "Benefic vs. Malefic Shashtiamsas: Your Karmic Filter"
**Rule:** Each of the 60 Shashtiamsas is ruled by a specific deity and classified into two definitive categories:

| Category | Sanskrit Names | Karmic Meaning |
|----------|---------------|----------------|
| **Benefic (Shubha)** | Saumya, Sudha, Amrita, Deva | Meritorious past-life deeds; nullifies D1 afflictions |
| **Malefic (Ashubha)** | Kantaka, Vishadagdha, Ghora, Yama | Deep karmic debt; houses ruled by such planets will suffer |

**Benefic Shashtiamsa:**
- Planets in Saumya, Sudha, Amrita, or Deva divisions indicate **meritorious past-life deeds** in that planet's domain.
- Even if the planet looks afflicted in D1, a benefic Shashtiamsa greatly nullifies the evil and ensures auspicious results eventually manifest.

**Malefic Shashtiamsa:**
- Planets in Kantaka, Vishadagdha, Ghora, or Yama divisions signal a **deep karmic debt**.
- The houses ruled by such a planet will suffer — the native may face sudden litigation, hidden enemies, heavy financial debts, or disease during that planet's dasha, **regardless of surface-level strength in D1**.

**BTR Application:**
- After rectifying a candidate time, check: do the D60 house placements of key planets (lords of 1st, 10th, 7th, 5th, etc.) align with the native's actual life experience in those areas?
- A native with consistent success in a life area should have the relevant planet in a **Benefic Shashtiamsa**.
- A native with chronic struggle in a life area should have the relevant planet in a **Malefic Shashtiamsa**.
- If the D60 Shashtiamsa classification contradicts the native's known life pattern, the birth time may need further adjustment.

**BTR Application:**

## 1. D-60 Computation (Page 72)

> **Example 26 (Page 72)**: "Each rasi is divided into 60 equal parts of 30' each. Bodies in the 60 parts of a rasi go into the 60 rasis starting from the rasi itself."

**Computation Method**:
1. Take the planet's longitude from the beginning of its occupied rasi
2. Multiply by 2
3. Take the degrees portion (ignore minutes)
4. Add 1 to get the part number (1-60)
5. Count that many rasis from the occupied rasi (removing multiples of 12)

> "To see the part occupied by a body, we can take its longitude from the beginning of the occupied rasi, multiply it by 2, take degrees and ignore minutes, add 1 to it."

**Example**: Jupiter at 222°58' (12°58' in Scorpio):
- 12°58' × 2 = 25°56'
- Degrees portion = 25
- Part number = 25 + 1 = 26
- Count 26 signs from Scorpio: 26 − 24 = 2 → 2nd from Scorpio = Sagittarius
- So Jupiter is in **Sagittarius** in D-60
