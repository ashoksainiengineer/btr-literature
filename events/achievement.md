## B.V. Raman Chapter 11 — Foundation Rules for Birth Time Verification

The following general BTR verification rules from Raman's Chapter 11 apply to **all event types**, including this one. ---

### Rule 1: Nakshatra Verification (Birth Constellation Check)

**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology, Ch.11 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-11-Birth-Verification-and-Rectification.html
**Rule:** "Multiply the time of birth in vighatis by 4 and divide the product by 9. The remainder must give the ruling constellation when counted from Aswini, Makha or Moola." — B.V. Raman

**Formula:** (Vighatis × 4) ÷ 9 → remainder → count from Aswini/Makha/Moola = birth nakshatra
**Purpose:** Confirm birth time is consistent with the Moon's birth star
**Worked example:** 2033 vighatis × 4 = 8132 ÷ 9 = remainder 5 → 5th from Aswini = Mrigasira ✓

### Three Nakshatra Groups

| Remainder | From Aswini (G1) | From Makha (G2) | From Moola (G3) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Aswini | Makha | Moola |
| 2 | Bharani | Pubba | Poorvashadha |
| 3 | Krittika | Uttara | Uttarashadha |
| 4 | Rohini | Hasta | Sravana |
| 5 | Mrigasira | Chitta | Dhanishta |

| 7 | Punarvasu | Visakha | Poorvabhadra |

| 9 (or 0) | Aslesha | Jyeshta | Revati |

### Rule 2: Lagna Verification (Ascendant Check)

**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology, Ch.11 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-11-Birth-Verification-and-Rectification.html
**Rule:** "Multiply the number of ghatis from birth by 6 and add the longitude of the Sun (the number of degrees passed in the sign). Divide the sum by 30. The quotient plus 1 counted from the Sun's sign will give the rising sign." — B.V. Raman

**Formula (ghatis):** (Ghatis × 6 + Sun° in sign) ÷ 30 → quotient + 1 from Sun's sign = Ascendant
**Formula (hours):** (Hours × 15 + Sun° in sign) ÷ 30 → quotient + 1 from Sun's sign = Ascendant
**Worked example:** 33.88 ghatis × 6 = 203.28 + 24° (Sun in Cancer) = 227.28 ÷ 30 = q=7 → 8 from Cancer = Aquarius ✓
**Caveat:** Approximate check to verify Ascendant **sign**, not exact degrees.

---

### Rule 3: Trinal Relationship Check

**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology, Ch.11 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-11-Birth-Verification-and-Rectification.html
**Rule:** "The 5th or the 9th sign from the house occupied by the lord of the sign in which the Moon is placed becomes the Janma Lagna or the ascendant. The 7th from the sign occupied by the lord of the Moon's sign or the 5th or the 9th from the similar sign and in some cases the sign where the Moon is at radix itself becomes the ascendant." — B.V. Raman

**Check:** Asc should be 5th, 9th, or 7th from the Moon's sign lord's position
**Rationale:** Houses 1, 5, 9 form the Dharma Trikona — the Ascendant lord and Moon's sign lord should be harmoniously related.

### Cascade Effect of Time Error

| Time Error | Ascendant Effect | Dasa Balance Effect | Divisional Chart Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1-2 min | Degree shifts slightly | Days change | Usually unchanged |
| 5-10 min | Degree shifts noticeably | Weeks to months change | Navamsa (D-9) may change |
| 15-30 min | May cross Navamsa boundary | Months change significantly | Multiple divisional charts shift |
| 1-2 hrs | Likely changes sign entirely | Entire Dasa may change | Nearly all charts affected |
| 4+ hrs | Completely different Lagna | Different Dasa lord | Entirely different horoscope |

### Practical BTR Workflow

1. **Rule 1** (Nakshatra check) — narrow time range if it fails
2. **Rule 2** (Lagna check) — verify Ascendant sign
3. **Rule 3** (Trinal check) — confirm harmonic relationship
4. **Check Navamsa Lagna** (±15 min window) — test stability; use known life events to determine best fit
5. **Test Dasa transitions** — major Dasa changes should correspond to known life turning points
6. **Verify with transits** — Saturn returns, Jupiter transits to angles should coincide with major events

# BPHS Classical Raja Yoga Rules — Achievement BTR

**Source:** Brihat Parasara Hora Sastra (BPHS), Chunk 5/5, Chapters 39–40
**Translation:** R. Santhanam
**Extracted:** 2026-05-24

---

## 1. Maha Raja Yoga (BPHS Ch.39, Slokas 6-7)

> Should the ascendant lord and the 5th lord exchange their signs or if Atma Karaka and Putra Karaka (Chara) are in the ascendant, the 5th, exaltation sign, own sign or own Navamsa in aspect to a benefic, Maha Raja yoga is produced.

**BTR Application:** For individuals with major life achievements, verify that either:
- (a) Ascendant lord and 5th lord exchange signs, OR
- (b) Atma Karaka and Putra Karaka are in the ascendant/5th/exaltation/own sign/own Navamsa with benefic aspect

**Example (Mrs. Indira Gandhi):** Atma Karaka = Saturn (21°50'), Putra Karaka = Mercury (13°11'). Saturn in ascendant, Mercury in 10th from Karakamsa Lagna. Saturn in own Navamsa. Ascendant lord Moon in 7th in exchange with Saturn. Jupiter aspects from 11th.

---

## 2. Ascendant Lord + Atmakaraka in 1st/5th/7th (BPHS Ch.39, Sloka 8)

> If the ascendant lord and Atmakaraka are in the ascendant, 5th or 7th in conjunction with or in aspect to a benefic, there obtains a Raja yoga.

**Key:** If ascendant lord IS the Atmakaraka, mere placement in 1st/5th/7th with a benefic causes Raja yoga.

---

## 3. Benefics in 2nd/4th/5th from Ascendant Lord or Atmakaraka (BPHS Ch.39, Slokas 9-10)

> Should there be benefics in the 2nd, 4th and 5th counted either from the ascendant lord or from Atmakaraka sign, one will become a king. Similarly malefics in the 3rd and 6th from the said places.

---

## 4. Venus in Karakamsa/5th/Ascendant/Arudha + Jupiter/Moon (BPHS Ch.39, Sloka 11)

> One will be related to royal circles if Venus is in the Karakamsa, or in the 5th therefrom or in the ascendant or in Arudha ascendant in aspect to or in the company of Jupiter or the Moon.

---

## 5. Single Planet Aspecting Three Ascendants (BPHS Ch.39, Sloka 12)

> Even if a single planet aspects the natal ascendant or Hora Lagna or Ghatika Lagna, the native will become a king.

**BTR Note:** For achievement BTR, check if one planet aspects at least one of: natal ascendant, Hora Lagna, or Ghatika Lagna.

---

## 6. Shadvarga Raja Yoga (BPHS Ch.39, Slokas 13-14)

> If the six divisions (shadvargas) of the ascendant is occupied or aspected by one and the same planet, a Raja yoga is doubtlessly formed.

**Six divisions:** Rasi, Hora, Drekkana, Trimsamsa, Navamsa, Dvadasamsa. If a planet occupies or aspects the ascendant in ALL six divisional charts, a powerful Raja yoga results. Aspect strength determines result: full/half/quarter aspect → full/medium/negligible results.

---

## 7. Three Ascendant Combinations (BPHS Ch.39, Slokas 15-17)

> If the 3 ascendants (natal ascendant, Hora Lagna, and Ghatika Lagna) are occupied by planets in exaltation/own sign, Raja yoga is formed.

> If the Moon and a benefic are in the Arudha Lagna, as Jupiter is in the 2nd from natal ascendant and both are aspected by exalted/own-sign planets → Raja yoga.

> If the ascendant, 2nd and 4th are occupied by benefics while a malefic is in the 3rd → king or equal.

---

## 8. 10th Lord in Own/Exaltation Aspecting Ascendant (BPHS Ch.39, Sloka 21)

> If the 10th lord, placed in his own house or in exaltation, aspects the ascendant, a Raja yoga is formed. Similar is the case if benefics are in angles.

---

## 9. Atmakaraka in Benefic Sign (BPHS Ch.39, Sloka 22)

> If the Atmakaraka is in a benefic's Rasi/Navamsa, the native will be wealthy. If there are benefics in angles from Karakamsa Lagna, he will become a king.

---

## 10. Arudha Lagna + Darapada Relationship (BPHS Ch.39, Sloka 23)

> If the Arudha Lagna and Darapada are in mutual angles or in mutual 3rd/11th or in mutual trines, the native will doubtlessly become a king.

**BTR Application:** For achievement events, verify Arudha Pada and Dara Pada are WELL related (angles/trines/3-11). If in 6th/8th or 2nd/12th, Raja yogas fail to mature despite other combinations.

---

## 11. Multiple Exalted Planets (BPHS Ch.39, Slokas 44-48)

| Exalted Planets | Result |
|----------------|--------|
| 1-3 | King (royal scion) or wealthy |
| 4-5 (or Moolatrikona) | Even base-born becomes king |
| 6 | Emperor |
| One among Jupiter/Venus/Mercury exalted + benefic in angle | King |

> If all benefics are in angles while malefics are in the 3rd, 6th and 11th → ascends throne even if mean descent.

---

## 12. Angular Trinal Lord Relationships (BPHS Ch.39, Slokas 33-39 & Ch.41, Sloka 28)

> The 9th lord is akin to a minister, more especially the 5th lord. If these two mutually aspect, the native obtains a kingdom.

**Angular-Trinal relationship types (strongest first):**
1. Exchange between angular and trinal lord
2. Mutual aspects
3. Conjunction
4. Mutual angular placement
5. Mutual trinal placement
6. (Extended) Navamsa exchange

**Key combinations:**
- 4th lord in 10th + 10th lord in 4th + aspected by 5th/9th lords → kingdom
- Lords of 5th/10th/4th/ascendant in 9th → ruler with fame
- 4th or 10th lord joins 5th or 9th lord → kingdom
- 5th lord in ascendant/4th/10th with 9th lord or ascendant lord → king
- Jupiter in own 9th house + Venus or 5th lord → kinghood

---

## 13. Dinardha/Nisardha Birth — Kingly Birth Time (BPHS Ch.39, Sloka 40)

> Two and half ghatis (60 minutes) from mid-day or from mid-night is auspicious time. Birth during such time causes one to be a king.

**Calculation:**
- Dinardha = half day duration (from sunrise to sunset, halved, added to sunrise)
- Nisardha = half night duration (from sunset to sunrise, halved, added to sunset)
- Birth within ~60 mins of Dinardha or Nisardha → Koteeswara yoga (exalted planet in 2nd) or Lakshadhikari yoga (own-sign planet in 2nd)

**BTR Application:** For high-achievement BTR, check if rectified birth time falls within Dinardha or Nisardha ± 60 mins (LMT).

## 3. Award Timing — Real-life Example 4: Oscar Nomination Analysis

> **Source section**: Part 6 — Real-life Example 4 (pages 508-511)

**Case:** Native nominated for an Academy Award (Oscar), announced March 26, 2000.

**Analysis method:**
1. Regular dasas too coarse — Oscar nomination itself is a major achievement regardless of win
2. **Patyayini dasa** in Tajaka monthly chart (March 14-April 14, 2000): Saturn's dasa runs March 26-April 3. Saturn is 6th/7th lord in D-10 (rivals and contacts). Exaltation of 6th lord unfavorable.
3. **Tajaka sixty-hour chart** (March 26-28, 2000): Lagna has Rahu, lagna lord debilitated in 5th (emotions). 10th strong (Oscar attendance) but lagna lord weak → disappointment. In D-10, lagna lord in 8th, 8th lord in lagna.
4. **Prediction:** Unlikely to win. **Result:** Did not win.

**BTR Application:** For award/recognition events, use Tajaka monthly and sixty-hour charts to pinpoint the exact outcome day. The Patyayini dasa of the sixty-hour period must show either success or disappointment through lord strengths.
