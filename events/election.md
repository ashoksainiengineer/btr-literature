## Rule: Hora Table — Which Planet Rules Each Hour

**Source:** Astro Secrets & KP Part IV, Chunk 1
**Rule:** "From the table it may be observed that the planets rule the hours in an order which is the alternate days in the reverse order. Suppose Sun rules a particular hour. Then note, in the reverse order, the days of the week. The alternate one is Friday ruled by Venus. Hence Venus rules the hour, after that of the Sun."
**Key Principle:** The planetary hours follow a fixed sequence: the order of hora rulers is the alternate days in reverse order. After 24 hours, the planet ruling 6-7 AM is the same as the day lord.
**KP Application:** For any election, the hora table provides the primary planetary hour. The sequence is: Sun, Venus, Mercury, Moon, Saturn, Jupiter, Mars (for each successive 2-hour period starting from sunrise on each day).
**Houses/Planets:** All planets, Hora system

## Rule: Western/Chaldean Hora Calculation Method

**Source:** Astro Secrets & KP Part IV, Chunk 1
**Rule:** "Westerners and Chaldeans calculate in a different manner. They divide the interval between the time of sunrise and noon into 6 equal parts and each will be little more or less than an hour depending upon the interval between sunrise and noon. Similarly the interval between Moon and sunset is divided into 6 equal parts. Likewise, the time from sunset to midnight and from midnight to sunrise is worked out."
**Key Principle:** Western astrology divides the day into 4 segments (sunrise-noon, noon-sunset, sunset-midnight, midnight-sunrise), each divided into 6 equal planetary hours. The length of each "hour" varies by season.
**KP Application:** For election events where precision is needed beyond the fixed table, use the Western method to compute exact hora boundaries based on actual sunrise/sunset times for the locality.
**Houses/Planets:** All planets, Hora system

## Rule: Finding Saturn's Hour on a Given Day

**Source:** Astro Secrets & KP Part IV, Chunk 1
**Rule:** "Adopt the following principle: (1) What is the Indian Standard Time of Sunrise in that locality on that day? (2) What is the Indian Standard Time at noon? **Key Principle:** To find when a specific planet rules its hour on a given day: compute the sunrise-to-noon interval, divide by 6, and count the hour rulers starting from the day lord at sunrise.
**KP Application:** For any election, this method gives exact timing of planetary hours for the specific locality and date.

### Rule 1: Nakshatra Verification (Birth Constellation Check)

**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology, Ch.11 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-11-Birth-Verification-and-Rectification.html
**Rule:** "Multiply the time of birth in vighatis by 4 and divide the product by 9. The remainder must give the ruling constellation when counted from Aswini, Makha or Moola." — B.V. Raman

**Formula:** (Vighatis × 4) ÷ 9 → remainder → count from Aswini/Makha/Moola = birth nakshatra
**Purpose:** Confirm birth time is consistent with the Moon's birth star
**Worked example:** 2033 vighatis × 4 = 8132 ÷ 9 = remainder 5 → 5th from Aswini = Mrigasira ✓

### Rule 2: Lagna Verification (Ascendant Check)

**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology, Ch.11 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-11-Birth-Verification-and-Rectification.html
**Rule:** "Multiply the number of ghatis from birth by 6 and add the longitude of the Sun (the number of degrees passed in the sign). Divide the sum by 30. The quotient plus 1 counted from the Sun's sign will give the rising sign." — B.V. Raman

**Formula (ghatis):** (Ghatis × 6 + Sun° in sign) ÷ 30 → quotient + 1 from Sun's sign = Ascendant
**Formula (hours):** (Hours × 15 + Sun° in sign) ÷ 30 → quotient + 1 from Sun's sign = Ascendant
**Worked example:** 33.88 ghatis × 6 = 203.28 + 24° (Sun in Cancer) = 227.28 ÷ 30 = q=7 → 8 from Cancer = Aquarius ✓
**Caveat:** Approximate check to verify Ascendant **sign**, not exact degrees.

### Rule 3: Trinal Relationship Check

**Source:** Hindu Predictive Astrology, Ch.11 — B.V. Raman
**URL:** https://vedastro.org/blog/Hindu-Predictive-Astrology-Chapter-11-Birth-Verification-and-Rectification.html
**Rule:** "The 5th or the 9th sign from the house occupied by the lord of the sign in which the Moon is placed becomes the Janma Lagna or the ascendant. The 7th from the sign occupied by the lord of the Moon's sign or the 5th or the 9th from the similar sign and in some cases the sign where the Moon is at radix itself becomes the ascendant." — B.V. Raman

**Check:** Asc should be 5th, 9th, or 7th from the Moon's sign lord's position
**Rationale:** Houses 1, 5, 9 form the Dharma Trikona — the Ascendant lord and Moon's sign lord should be harmoniously related.

### Cascade Effect of Time Error

| Time Error | Ascendant Effect | Dasa Balance Effect | Divisional Chart Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1-2 min | Degree shifts slightly | Days change | Usually unchanged |
| 5-10 min | Degree shifts noticeably | Weeks to months change | Navamsa (D-9) may change |
| 15-30 min | May cross Navamsa boundary | Months change significantly | Multiple divisional charts shift |
| 1-2 hrs | Likely changes sign entirely | Entire Dasa may change | Nearly all charts affected |
| 4+ hrs | Completely different Lagna | Different Dasa lord | Entirely different horoscope |

## 2. Hora — Planetary Hours for Election Timing (Page 29)

> "Each day starts at sunrise and ends at next day's sunrise. This period is divided into 24 equal parts and they are called horas. A hora is almost equal to an hour."

**Hora Lord Order**: Saturn → Jupiter → Mars → Sun → Venus → Mercury → Moon (then repeat)

**First Hora Rule**: The first hora of any day (following sunrise) is ruled by the weekday lord:
- Sunday → Sun's hora first
- Monday → Moon's hora first
- Tuesday → Mars' hora first
- Wednesday → Mercury's hora first
- Thursday → Jupiter's hora first
- Friday → Venus' hora first
- Saturday → Saturn's hora first

**Computing the Running Hora**:
> "The first hora of any day is ruled by the lord of the weekday. After that, we list planets in the order: Saturn, Jupiter, Mars, Sun, Venus, Mercury, Moon."

### BTR Application
For an important elected event (marriage, business opening), the hora at the chosen time should be ruled by a planet favorable for the event:
- Marriage → Venus hora (love), Jupiter hora (blessings)
- Business → Mercury hora (commerce), Jupiter hora (expansion)
- Spiritual initiation → Sun hora (authority), Moon hora (mind)
- Property purchase → Saturn hora (permanence), Venus hora (comfort)
