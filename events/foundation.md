## 1. Rectification as Systematic Hypothesis Testing

**Source section:** What Rectification Is

**Rule:** Rectification is the process of determining an unknown or uncertain birth time by working backward from known life events. Each candidate birth time generates a hypothesis: "If this person were born at 7:14 AM, then at the time of their marriage, Saturn should have been transiting their 7th house cusp." The transit data is checked against the hypothesis. A single event can narrow the candidates; five or more events, tested simultaneously, typically converge on a single time window.

**Key principle:** Multiple independent lines of evidence converging on the same birth time produce substantial confidence.

## 3. Transit Analysis: The Foundation of Rectification

**Source section:** Transit Analysis

### 3.1 Why Outer Planets Matter Most

**Rule:** The slower a planet moves, the more discriminating it is as a rectification tool. Outer planets' positions change negligibly between candidate birth times on the same day — but the house cusps they aspect change significantly. This makes outer planet transits to house cusps the most valuable discriminator.

| Planet | Time per Sign | Usefulness for BTR |
|--------|--------------|---------------------|
| Moon | ~2.5 days | Low (changes too fast) |
| Sun | ~30 days | Moderate |
| Mercury | ~30-60 days | Moderate |
| Venus | ~30-60 days | Moderate |
| Mars | ~45-60 days | Moderate-High |
| Jupiter | ~1 year | High |
| Saturn | ~2.5 years | Very High |
| Rahu/Ketu | ~18 months | Very High |
| Uranus | ~7 years | Extremely High |
| Neptune | ~14 years | Extremely High |
| Pluto | ~20 years | Extremely High |

### 3.2 Transit-to-Cusp Scoring

**Rule:** For each life event, check every transit planet's angular relationship to the house cusps relevant to that event type. The tighter the aspect (conjunction, opposition, square, trine) with the relevant cusp, the higher the score.

Transit scoring weights:
- **Speed weighting**: Slower planet = higher weight
- **Aspect tightness**: Closer to exact = higher score
- **Relevant aspects**: Conjunction, opposition, square, trine

### 3.3 Transit-to-Angle Sensitivity

**Rule:** Beyond event-specific house cusps, transit planets aspecting the four angles (ASC, MC, DSC, IC) are scored for every event. The angles are the most sensitive points in the chart — any major life event, regardless of type, tends to coincide with significant transits to at least one angle.

### 3.4 The ASC Ruler Connection

**Rule:** Transit planets forming aspects to the natal chart ruler at the time of significant events provide an additional scoring layer. The chart ruler carries the native's life energy — any major biographical event should register as a transit activation of this point.

## 5. Solar Arc Directions: Precision Tool

**Source section:** Solar Arc Directions and Primary Directions

### 5.1 Solar Arc Method

**Rule:** The solar arc is the distance the progressed Sun has traveled from its natal position (~1°/year). This arc is applied uniformly to every planet and point in the natal chart. Directed planets within **1.5-degree orb** of natal angles or event cusps score.

**Key advantage:** Because every planet moves by exactly the same arc, the technique produces a clean, uniform timing mechanism. A directed planet landing on a natal angle within 1.5° at event time is difficult to attribute to chance.

### 5.2 Primary Directions

**Rule:** The Midheaven (MC) advances at approximately one degree per year of life through the primary direction framework. Primary directions use the **tightest orb of any technique: 1.0 degree**.

**Weighting:**
- Sun, Moon, Saturn, Jupiter contacts: **3x weight**
- Mercury, Venus, Mars contacts: **1.5x weight**

**Rationale:** The MC position is entirely determined by birth time, making a primary direction hit strong evidence for the candidate time that produced it.

### 5.3 Why Stage 3 Uses Both

**Rule:** Solar arcs (1.5° orb) and primary directions (1.0° orb) are computationally intensive and produce fewer hits — but the hits carry high evidential weight. They are reserved for the top 15 candidates after transit-based filtering, when remaining candidates are separated by only a few minutes.

## 9. Event Selection Principles

**Source section:** Three Principles for Selecting Events

**Principle 1 — Exact dates outperform approximate dates:** An event dated to the specific day carries far more weight than "sometime in fall 2015." Approximate dates use 15th of month as fallback, with reduced discriminating power.

**Principle 2 — Involuntary events outperform planned events:** Accidents, deaths, and sudden crises produce sharper astrological signatures than deliberately timed events. A wedding date may be chosen for social convenience; a car accident is not.

**Principle 3 — More events yield higher confidence:** Three events → reasonable estimate. Five+ events with exact dates → high confidence. Each additional event adds independent evidence.

## 10. Sign-Boundary Ambiguity

**Rule:** When the correct birth time falls near an Ascendant sign boundary, two adjacent rising signs may produce candidates with similar event fitness scores. The algorithm may return medium or low confidence even with good data. Solution: provide more precisely dated events to resolve the ambiguity.

---

## 11. Rectification Is Not Verification

**Rule:** A rectified birth time is the best estimate given available evidence, not established fact. It should be treated as a working hypothesis. If new evidence emerges (hospital record found, family member recalls time), that evidence takes precedence over the rectified time.

## 1. Birth Time Sensitivity

### 1.1 Ascendant (Rising Sign) Precision

**Rule:** The Ascendant (Lagna) determines the entire house structure — which sign rules each house and which planets govern which life domains. The Ascendant moves through all 12 signs in 24 hours, spending approximately 2 hours in each sign.

**Time sensitivity:** 4 minutes of clock time = approximately 1 degree of Ascendant movement.

**Critical zone:** Near sign boundaries, even 5 minutes of clock error can push the Ascendant into the adjacent sign. A person born at 6:58 AM vs 7:02 AM on the same day may have entirely different Ascendants.

### 1.2 Sidereal Time Conversion

**Rule:** Birth time must be converted into **sidereal time** for the latitude of the birthplace to calculate house cusps. The Midheaven (MC — 10th house cusp) is calculated from this conversion. Both Ascendant and MC determine career, relationships, life journey, and social status.

### 1.3 Effects of Wrong Birth Time

Without exact birth time:
- **Ascendant (rising sign) cannot be accurately identified** — it moves through all 12 signs in a day
- **Midheaven (MC) cannot be accurately identified** — similarly fast-moving
- The entire house cusp system is unreliable

## 2. Birthplace (Latitude/Longitude) Sensitivity

### 2.1 Latitude Error Effect on Ascendant

**Rule:** An error of **1 degree in latitude** causes approximately **0.5 degree error** in the Ascendant calculation. This is significant because the Ascendant determines the 1st house cusp and the entire house system.

### 2.2 Longitude Error Effect on Time

**Rule:** An error in **longitude** causes an equivalent error of **4 minutes in birth time** per degree of longitude error. This compounds with any existing birth time uncertainty.

### 2.3 Combined Effect

**Rule:** Both birth time AND birthplace (latitude + longitude) must be accurate for correct Kundali calculation. The ecliptic is a 360-degree circle divided into 12 × 30-degree zodiac signs. These govern the 12 houses based on exact birth time AND geographic coordinates.
