## Rule: 9th Cusp Sub in 12th House = Strained Relationship with Father

**Source:** Lex's BTR Analysis — Characteristics Verification
**URL:** https://phpbb.lightonvedicastrology.com/viewtopic.php?t=33958
**Rule:** "Again 9th cusp sub... in 12th... Strained relation with father."

**Anatomy of the Rule:**
1. **9th Cusp** = Dharma Bhava — governs father, guru, fortune, spiritual guidance
2. **9th Cusp Sub** = The sub-lord of the 9th cusp determines the nature of father relationship
3. **9th cusp sub placed in 12th house** = Father-related significations are in the house of loss, separation, and expenditure — resulting in strained/distant relationship

**Mechanism:** When the sub-lord of the 9th cusp (father) is connected to the **12th house** (loss, separation, moksha), it indicates:
- Physical or emotional distance from father
- Loss of father's support or presence
- Strained communication with father
- Father's absence or diminished role in life

**KP Application:** For BTR, when the native reports a difficult relationship with their father, verify that:
1. The 9th cusp sub-lord is in the 12th house, OR
2. The 9th cusp sub-lord signifies the 12th house (through ownership/aspect), OR
3. The 9th lord is placed in 12th house or connected to 12th significators

**Houses/Planets:** 9th cusp (father), 9th cusp sub-lord, 12th house (loss/separation)

## Rule: 4th Cusp in Dual Sign with Guru (Jupiter) Sub = Excellent Mother Relationship

**Source:** Lex's BTR Analysis — Characteristics Verification
**URL:** https://phpbb.lightonvedicastrology.com/viewtopic.php?t=33958
**Rule:** "4th cusp in Dual sign in Chitra nk and in sub of Guru. Guru in 10th, also 6th and 10th cusp lord. Excellent relation with Mother, and you will be a Rich person too."

**Anatomy of the Rule:**
1. **4th Cusp** = Sukha Bhava — governs mother, home, emotional well-being, vehicles
2. **Dual sign placement** (the 4th cusp falls in a Dwiswabhava/dual sign) = adaptability, balanced relationship
3. **Chitra Nakshatra** — ruled by Mars (Tvashtar); gives creative, nurturing, protective energy
4. **Sub of Guru (Jupiter)** — wisdom, protection, expansion, benevolence
5. **Guru in 10th house** — Guru (Jupiter) placed in the 10th house (career/public standing) amplifies the 4th house signification through the house lordship chain
6. **Guru as 6th and 10th cusp lord** — Guru being lord of both 6th (service/disease) and 10th (career) contributes to wealth generation

**The "Rich Person" Connection:**
- Guru (Jupiter) = wealth, expansion
- Guru in 10th = career success, public prosperity
- Chitra Nakshatra = creative wealth, craftsmanship
- 4th cusp sub = Guru → mother relationship is foundation of emotional security → enabling wealth creation

**KP Application:** For BTR, when the native reports a close/nurturing relationship with their mother:
1. The 4th cusp sub-lord (Guru/Jupiter in this case) should be a benefic planet
2. The 4th lord should be well-placed (not in 6th/8th/12th dusthanas)
3. A dual sign on the 4th cusp indicates balanced, adaptable relationship
4. Guru as 4th cusp sub-lord with Guru in Kendra (10th) strengthens both mother relationship and wealth

**Houses/Planets:** 4th cusp (mother, home, emotional foundation), Chitra Nakshatra, Guru/Jupiter (4th CSL, 6th & 10th lord), dual sign, 10th house (Guru's placement)

**Classical Reference:** Parasara (BPHS) — D-12 for parents

---

### 1. D-12 (Dwadasamsa) for Parents, Grandparents, Uncles, and Aunts

> **Page 182:**
> "For parents, grandparents, uncles and aunts, we should see D-12."

**Rule:** The Dwadasamsa chart (D-12) is the primary chart for all matters related to parents and extended family elders.

---

### 2. Father in D-12

> **Page 182:**
> "The 9th lord or the arudha pada of 9th house in D-12 shows father."

**Rule:** To analyze the father's fortune from the native's chart:
- Use D-12
- Examine the **9th lord** (rasi containing 9th lord as lagna for father)
- Alternatively, examine **A9** (arudha pada of 9th house) in D-12
- The condition of these indicators reveals the father's life circumstances

**BTR Application:** Father's significant life events (marriage, career changes, health crises, death) should correspond with Dasas of planets connected to the 9th lord or A9 in D-12.

---

### 3. Mother in D-12

> **Page 182:**
> "The 4th lord or the arudha pada of 4th house in D-12 shows mother."

**Rule:** To analyze the mother's fortune:
- Examine the **4th lord** in D-12
- Alternatively, examine **A4** (arudha pada of 4th house) in D-12

**BTR Application:** Mother's significant life events should correspond with Dasas of planets connected to the 4th lord or A4 in D-12.

### BTR Application — Parent Death Verification
When verifying a parent's death during BTR:
1. Identify the appropriate sthira karaka (Sun/Venus for father; Moon/Mars for mother)
2. Check that the sthira karaka is connected to the 8th house (death), 12th house (loss) or a maraka house (2nd/7th) in the relevant divisional chart
3. Verify the running dasha at time of death involves these significations
4. Use D-12 (Dwadasamsa) for parent-specific events

## Rule PVR-PAR-1: D-12 Narayana Dasa for Parent Relations Timing
**Source:** PVR, pp. 256-257, 267 (Sections 18.5, 18.7)
**Rule:** D-12 (Dwadasamsa) shows the lineage one belongs to and relations with parents. Narayana dasa of D-12 can be used to predict events related to parents. The seed of D-12 is the 12th house (showing the evolution of self in the physical plane, i.e., the lineage).
**BTR Application:** For significant parent-related events (parent's illness, parent's death, inheritance from parents), compute D-12 Narayana dasa. The seed is the 12th house in rasi chart. Find the 12th lord, see where he is in D-12, treat that as lagna, and compute ND. A favorable dasa supports positive parent relations; an unfavorable dasa may indicate parent-related challenges.

---

## Rule PVR-PAR-2: Different Seeds for Native-Centric vs. Parent-Centric View
**Source:** PVR, p. 267 (Section 18.7)
**Rule:** Multiple Narayana dasas can be computed for D-12:
- **Seeded from 12th lord (native-centric):** Shows events in the native's life related to parents, taking the native as the reference
- **Using different seeds:** Shows events in the lives of parents directly

**BTR Application:** For parent-related event verification, choose the appropriate seed:
- For how a parent's life event affects the native → use 12th lord seed (native-centric)
- For direct parent events (parent's perspective) → use different seeds (e.g., 4th house for mother, 9th for father)

---

## Rule PVR-PAR-3: Pitri Shoola Dasa and Matri Shoola Dasa for Parent Death
**Source:** PVR, pp. 297-300 (Section 23.5)
**Rule:** Parental death events are timed using specialized Shoola dasas:
- Father: Pitri Shoola dasa (stronger of 9th/3rd)
- Mother: Matri Shoola dasa (stronger of 4th/10th)

Death occurs when Shiva's force strikes trines from the sthira karaka of the respective parent. Sthira karakas are used for matters controlled by Shiva (suffering and death), not the natural karakas (Sun for father, Moon for mother).
**BTR Application:** For any parent death event, use the appropriate specialized Shoola dasa rather than the native's regular Shoola dasa. The sthira karaka approach (not natural karaka) is critical for correct timing.
**Example:** Example 94/Indira Gandhi — father died in Le dasa (trine from Venus = sthira pitri karaka), not in a Sun trine.

## 1. Pitri Saham — Father Point

**Source**: Ch.28, Table 74 (#10), page 395
**Page**: 395

**Pitri Saham Formula**: Saturn – Sun + Lagna (day); Sun – Saturn + Lagna (night)

**BTR Application**:
- Calculate Pitri Saham in the natal chart
- For father-related events (death, illness, milestone), check transit activations of Pitri Saham
- Malefics (Saturn, Mars, Rahu) transiting over Pitri Saham → father-related challenges
- Benefics (Jupiter, Venus) transiting over Pitri Saham → father's well-being

## 2. Matri Saham — Mother Point

**Source**: Ch.28, Table 74 (#12), page 395
**Page**: 395

**Matri Saham Formula**: Moon – Venus + Lagna (day); Venus – Moon + Lagna (night)

**BTR Application**:
- Calculate Matri Saham for mother-related events
- At mother's death or illness, verify that malefics transited over or aspected Matri Saham
- In Tajaka annual chart, if Lord of the Year is connected to Matri Saham, mother-related events are likely in that year

## 3. Father's Death via Kalachakra Dasa & D-12

**Source**: Ch.24, Example 100, pages 313-314
**Pages**: 313-314

**Rule**: Father's death timed with Kalachakra dasa and D-12 (Dwadasamsa):
- D-12 shows the concept of father. 9th house in D-12 = relation with father
- A9 (arudha pada of 9th) → physical body of father
- From A9's rasi as lagna, 7th house = death
- Mandooki gati (frog's leap) in Kalachakra involving the dasa rasi → distress to father

**Example**: Father passed away in Cn-Cn antardasa (Kalachakra). A9 in Cp. Taking Cp as lagna, Cn is 7th house of death. Moon (Cn lord) exalted, afflicts Sun (8th lord from Cp).

**BTR Application**: For father's death:
1. Check Kalachakra dasa rasi = 7th from A9 in D-12
2. Check for gatis (leaps) indicating distress to father
3. Verify SAV strength of the dasa rasi

## 4. Mother's Death via Kalachakra Dasa

**Source**: Ch.24, Exercise 35, page 314, answer page 322
**Pages**: 314, 322

**Rule**: Mother's death occurred in Li-Li antardasa (Kalachakra).
- A4 (arudha pada of 4th) in Vi
- From Vi, Li is 2nd house (maraka) — Venus (Li lord) exalted in 7th, afflicts debilitated 1st lord
- Li is a strong maraka rasi from A4

**BTR Application**: For mother's death:
1. Find A4
2. From A4, maraka rasis (2nd and 7th) indicate death timing
3. Check if Kalachakra antardasa at death falls in a maraka rasi from A4

# VedAstro.org — B.V. Raman's Family Assessment Rules (Chapter 16)

## Family Member Significators

| Family Member | Significator Planet | House | Positive Configuration | Negative Configuration |
|--------------|-------------------|-------|----------------------|----------------------|
| Father | Sun | 9th house | Sun well-placed in friendly Navamsa | Sun + Saturn in 9th without benefic aspect |
| Mother | Moon | 4th house | Moon well-placed in friendly Navamsa | Moon + Saturn in 4th without benefic aspect |
| Children | Jupiter (Putrakaraka) | 5th house (Putrasthana) | Jupiter well-placed in friendly Navamsa | Jupiter + Saturn in 5th; Mercury + Mars in 5th |

> *"An examination of a number of horoscopes has revealed that the Moon, Jupiter or the Sun in the 4th, the 5th or the 9th with Saturn has caused death to mother, children or father respectively, early in life."*
> — B.V. Raman, Chapter 16

**Sun with Saturn in 9th:** Early death of father. The Sun-Saturn combination is naturally antagonistic and when this enmity plays out in the house of the father, separation occurs.

**Moon with Saturn in 4th** without any favourable aspect: Death of mother within one year of child's birth. Both significator (Moon) and house (4th) are afflicted simultaneously.

**BTR Application:** For known parent death events:
1. Check if the significator planet (Sun for father, Moon for mother) occupies its corresponding house with Saturn
2. Verify no benefic aspect mitigates this conjunction
3. Confirm the Dasa at parent's death involves the significator or Saturn
4. When Sun (father) or Moon (mother) as Dasa lord at the time of parent's death, verify correct house lordship connections

## Jupiter in Ascendant — Family Protection

> *"Jupiter in the ascendant is the best gift in a horoscope and the native gets dutiful children and long-lived parents."*
> — B.V. Raman, Chapter 16

**BTR Application:** If Jupiter is in the ascendant at the rectified birth time, it aspects 5th (children), 7th (spouse), and 9th (father/fortune) simultaneously. This creates a protective canopy. Parent death events should be later in life when Jupiter's protective influence is counterbalanced by other factors.
