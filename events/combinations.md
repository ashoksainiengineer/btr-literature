## Jataka Parijata: Planetary Combinations (Adhyaya VIII)

**Source:** Jataka Parijata Vol I, Vaidyanatha Dikshita (tr. V. Subrahmanya Sastri, 1932)
**Source URL:** https://archive.org/details/JatakaParijata1932

### Multi-Planet Conjunctions (Adhyaya VIII, Sloka 1-38)

**Dvigraha Yoga (Sloka 1-5):** Two planets in one bhava.

**Trigraha Yoga (Sloka 6-14):** Three planets in one bhava.

**Chaturgraha Yoga (Sloka 15-25):** Four planets in one bhava.

**Panchagraha Yoga (Sloka 26-35):** Five planets in one bhava.

**Shadgraha Yoga (Sloka 36-38):** Six planets in one bhava.

> **General Rule:** The more planets in one bhava, the more powerful and often disturbing the effect. Results depend on the nature of the planets (benefic/malefic), their strength, and the house involved.

### Five Kinds of Mutual Relation (Adhyaya II, Sloka 41-46)

1. **Adhimitra** — Friend both naturally and temporarily → great friend
2. **Mitra** — Friendly naturally, neutral temporarily, or vice versa → friend
3. **Sama** — Neutral naturally and temporarily → neutral
4. **Sathru** — Neutral naturally, inimical temporarily, or vice versa → enemy
5. **Adhisathru** — Enemy both ways → deadly foe

### Exaltation Effects — Uchcha Phalam (Adhyaya VIII, Sloka 100-103)

Planets in exaltation signs produce superior results in the matters of the house they occupy. These effects are particularly strong and beneficial.

### Debilitation Effects — Neecha Phalam (Sloka 114)

Planets in depression produce weak or negative effects. These can be cancelled (Neecha Bhanga) if:
- The planet is in own Navamsa
- The lord of the depression sign is in Kendra
- A benefic planet aspects the debilitated planet

### Own House Effects — Swashetra Phalam (Sloka 105-106)

Planets in own houses produce consistent, steady results. They act according to their pure nature.

### Friendly House Effects — Mitrakshetra Phalam (Sloka 107-111)

Planets in friendly signs produce moderately good results, modified by the friendship intensity.

### Inimical House Effects — Satrukshetra Phalam (Sloka 112-113)

Planets in inimical signs produce disturbed results, often creating obstacles in the matters of the house.

### Moolatrikona Effects (Sloka 104)

Planets in Moolatrikona produce results even better than own house — this is considered a position of great strength.

### Planetary War & Combustion (Adhyaya II, Sloka 37)

Planets win in planetary war (graha yuddha) when:
- They are retrograde
- They are in conjunction with the Moon
- They are in northerly latitude

The Sun and Moon are strong in Uttarayana (northern course).

### Aspects — Drishti (Adhyaya II, Sloka 30-31)

| Aspect Type | Houses Aspected | Strength |
|-------------|-----------------|----------|
| Quarter (Drishti) | 3rd, 10th | Saturn powerful |
| Half (ardha) | 5th, 9th | Jupiter auspicious |
| Three-quarters (pada) | 4th, 8th | Mars potent |
| Full (purna) | 7th | All planets strong |

### Special Planetary Look (Sloka 32)

- Sun and Mars: Look upwards
- Venus and Mercury: Look sideways
- Moon and Jupiter: Look evenly
- Rahu and Ketu: Look downwards

### Planets Becoming Troublesome (Adhyaya II, Sloka 48)

In moveable, immoveable and dual signs, planets occupying respectively the 11th, 9th and 7th houses from them or their lords → prove exceedingly troublesome if they also own the houses occupied by the lord of Khata or Mandi.

### Counteracting Malefic Effects (Adhyaya II, Sloka 73-74)

Chain of counteraction:
- Mercury can counteract Rahu's evil
- Saturn can counteract Mercury + Rahu combined
- Mars can counteract the above three
- Venus can counteract the above four
- Jupiter can counteract the above five
- Moon can counteract the above six
- Sun can counteract the above seven (especially strong in northward course)

### Planets' Special Effects When Malefic (Adhyaya II, Sloka 75-81)

See [Health BTR Rules](../health.md) for detailed disease associations.


# Rules from Horasara of Prithuyasas

## Rule: Adverse Combinations for Janma Kundali (Ch. 24)
**Source:** Horasara of Prithuyasas, Ch. 24
**Rule:** This chapter specifically covers combinations that hinder or harm a birth chart. Key categories include:
- A weak Lagna lord with malefic occupation of key houses
- Grahas in debilitation with specific inter-relationships
- Combinations indicating poverty, disease, or misfortune
- Exchange between Lagna lord and Bandhu lord (otherwise a Raja Yoga, but listed under adverse if other factors apply)

---

## Rule: Dasha Effect Verification (Ch. 8)
**Source:** Horasara of Prithuyasas, Ch. 8
**Rule:** Dasha effects should be predicted in the same order from the balance of Janma Dasha obtained. Effects should be calculated from the stronger of the two — Candr and Lagna at birth. Surya in transit reaching the Rasi occupied by the Bhukti lord at birth triggers marked effects for that Dasha.

---

## Rule: Dasha Timing of Results by Graha (Ch. 32)
**Source:** Horasara of Prithuyasas, Ch. 32
**Rule:**
- Surya and Mangala → results revealed at the beginning
- Budha, Guru, and Sani → effects at the end
- Candr → results while rising
- Sukra → effects depend on its motion (retrograde vs direct)

---

## Rule: Rahu-Ketu Dasha Effects (Ch. 8, Notes)
**Source:** Horasara of Prithuyasas, Ch. 8
**Rule:** The results of Ketu will be like that of Mangala, while Rahu's effects will be like Sani's. Other Grahas produce even effects due to their nature. The Vimshottari Dasha system (Nakshatra-based) is acceptable to all as stated.

## Laghu Parashari: Lordship-Based Classification System

**Source:** Laghu Parashari (Ududaya Pradeepa), Chapter 1 (Samjna Adhyaya)
**Source URL:** https://archive.org/details/laghu-parashari
**Reference:** `/home/jovyan/ai-pandit-app/data/btr-knowledge/classical/laghu-parashari.md`

### Two Types of Benefic/Malefic

Laghu Parashari uniquely divides planetary nature into two:

1. **Svabhavika (Naisargika)** — Natural, inherent nature
2. **Tatkalika** — Temporary, based on house lordship

The **combined assessment** determines a planet's true effect:

| Natural | Temporary (Lordship) | Final Classification |
|---------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Benefic | Benefic (trikona lord) | **Ati-shubha** (very benefic) |
| Benefic | Malefic (trishadaya lord) | **Sama** (neutral) |
| Malefic | Malefic (trishadaya lord) | **Ati-papa** (very malefic) |
| Malefic | Benefic (trikona lord) | **Sama** (neutral) |
| Benefic | Neutral (kendra lord) | **Forgets nature, neutral** |
| Malefic | Neutral (kendra lord) | **Forgets nature, neutral** |

### Four-Fold Lordship Classification

| Lordship | Houses | Effect |
|----------|--------|--------|
| **Trikona** (Shubha) | 1, 5, 9 | **Benefic** — even malefics become benefic |
| **Trishadaya** (Papa) | 3, 6, 11 | **Malefic** — even benefics become malefic |
| **Kendra** (Sama) | 4, 7, 10 | **Neutral** — all planets forget their nature |
| **Dusthana-mixed** | 2, 8, 12 | **Context-dependent** (see below) |

### Contextual Rules for 2nd, 8th, 12th Lords

**2nd Lord and 12th Lord (Sloka 8):**
Give results based on:
1. **Sahacarya** — Which planets they associate with
2. **Sthanantara** — Their other house lordship
3. Their position in the chart

They do NOT have fixed benefic/malefic nature — entirely contextual.

**8th Lord (Sloka 9):**
- **Generally malefic** (rules "expenditure of bhagya/destruction of fortune")
- **Exception:** If also Lagnesh (1st lord) or Trikona lord → becomes **Shubha-sandhata** (organizer of good results)
- If ruling ONLY 8th → mildly malefic

### Natural (Naisargika) Classification

| Nature | Planets |
|--------|---------|
| **Papa** (Malefic) | Sun, Saturn, Mars |
| **Shubha** (Benefic) | Jupiter, Venus |
| **Sama** (Neutral) | Moon, Mercury |
| **Tamasa** (Shadow) | Rahu, Ketu |

> **Note:** Moon is benefic when waxing, malefic when waning. Mercury becomes benefic with benefics, malefic with malefics. Ketu's results depend entirely on association.

### Strength Order Within Groups

**Among Kendras:** 10th > 4th > 7th > 1st (where 1st is also Trikona)
**Among Trikonas:** 9th > 5th > 1st
**Among Trishadaya:** 11th > 6th > 3rd

| Trikona Strength Order | Kendra Strength Order |
|------------------------|----------------------|
| 9th (Dharma) — strongest | 10th (Karma) — strongest |
| 5th (Putra) — medium | 4th (Sukha) — medium |
| 1st (Tanu) — least among trikona | 7th (Jaya) — weaker |
| | 1st (Tanu) — not counted as kendra here |

### Special Drishti (Aspect) Rules

**Standard 7th house aspect:** All planets
**Additional aspects:**
- **Saturn:** 3rd and 10th (as servant overseeing valor and kingdom)
- **Jupiter:** 5th and 9th (as preceptor overseeing learning and dharma)
- **Mars:** 4th and 8th (as commander overseeing comfort and longevity)

> **Laghu Parashari distinction:** Unlike other texts that assign partial aspects (1 pada, 2 padas, 3 padas) to all planets, this text gives **FULL aspect only** to these specific planets for their additional houses.

## Rule: Moon + 6th/8th/12th Signification — Negative Time Combination

**Source:** Sushmajee KP BTR
**URL:** http://www.sushmajee.com/astrology/kp-system/birth-time.htm

**Rule:** When the Moon at the rectification moment signifies any TWO of the three dusthana houses (6th, 8th, or 12th), this forms a **negative time combination** that blocks all BTR work. This combination must be checked at Sign and Star level only — the Moon's Sign Lord and Star Lord are evaluated for connection to 6th/8th/12th houses.

**Mechanism of the Combination:**
| Component | Role | How it Signifies Negative Houses |
|-----------|------|----------------------------------|
| Moon's **Sign Lord** | Primary indicator | By owning or occupying 6th, 8th, or 12th |
| Moon's **Star Lord** | Secondary indicator | By owning or occupying 6th, 8th, or 12th |
| **Outer planet aspecting Moon** | Trigger | If the aspecting planet itself signifies any two of 6th/8th/12th |

**Threshold:** Any TWO of the three negative houses must be signified across the Moon's Sign Lord and Star Lord.

**Examples of the Combination:**
- Moon in Scorpio (Sign Lord Mars), Star Lord Mercury (in Jyeshta): Mars owns 6th, Mercury owns 12th → **6th + 12th signified → NEGATIVE** ✗
- Moon in Taurus (Sign Lord Venus), Star Lord Moon (in Rohini): Venus owns 7th/12th, Moon owns 1st → Venus gives 12th ✓ but Moon gives 1st ✗ → only 1 negative → **NOT negative** ✓ (passes filter)
- Moon in Virgo (Sign Lord Mercury), Star Lord Sun (in Uttara Phalguni): Mercury owns 3rd/6th, Sun owns 5th/occupies 12th → **6th + 12th signified → NEGATIVE** ✗

**KP Application:** This combination acts as a gatekeeper — if present at the rectification moment, the astrologer must wait. Any BTR attempted under this combination will yield unreliable results regardless of the astrologer's skill or software accuracy.

## Rule: Outer Planet Aspect + Negative Signification Combination

**Source:** Sushmajee KP BTR
**URL:** http://www.sushmajee.com/astrology/kp-system/birth-time.htm

**Rule:** A secondary combination that blocks BTR: an outer planet (Saturn, Rahu, Ketu, Jupiter) aspects the Moon AND that same outer planet signifies any two of 6th, 8th, or 12th houses. This combination is valid only within 3.33° orb.

**Combination Breakdown:**
1. **Outer Planet → Moon aspect** within 3.33° orb
2. **Outer Planet's house significations**: must include at least two of {6, 8, 12}
3. **Effect**: Moon "inherits" the negative house significations through the aspect, even if Moon's own Sign/Star Lords are clean

**Example:**
- Moon in 10th house, Sign Lord Mercury (owns 1st/4th) → clean
- Saturn in 6th, owns 11th/12th, aspects Moon at 2.5° orb
- Saturn signifies 6th (occupation) + 12th (ownership) → TWO negative houses
- **Combination triggered → NEGATIVE TIME** ✗

---

## Rule: Absence of Negative Combination — Green Light for BTR

**Source:** Sushmajee KP BTR
**URL:** http://www.sushmajee.com/astrology/kp-system/birth-time.htm

**Rule:** The time is suitable for BTR when:
1. Moon's Sign Lord and Star Lord signify ONE or ZERO of {6th, 8th, 12th} houses
2. No outer planet aspects Moon while signifying two of {6th, 8th, 12th}
3. The RP Interchange condition (RP Lag = Birth Mon, RP Mon = Birth Lag) is satisfied

When these conditions are met, the combination is absent, and the rectification can proceed.

## Jataka Tattva (Mahadeva) — Key Yogas & Combinations

**Source:** Jataka Tattva (1871 CE)

### Rule: Bhava Growth & Destruction (Prakirna Tatva C.1-6)
- A Bhava occupied/aspected by its lord or benefics → prosperity
- A Bhava occupied/aspected by malefics → destruction
- Both benefic & malefic → mixed effect
- Planet in depression/inimical Rasi → destroys the Bhava
- Planet in own/friendly/exaltation sign → promotes Bhava

- Moon in 5th + Jupiter in Trikona + Mars in 10th → long life
- Lord of 8th in own sign, or Saturn in 8th → long life
- Lords of 8th/1st/10th in Kendra/Kona/11th → long life
- Saturn in own/exaltation/Upachaya or owning 8th in 5th → long life

### Rule: Raja Yogas (Appendix examples)
- Lords of Lagna & Moon's sign exchange places → Rajayoga
- Strong benefic in Lagna + another in 4th + others in 9th/2nd/3rd/6th/10th/11th → kingship

### Rule: Bhava Destruction Timing (Prakirna Tatva C.7)
Strongest among: (1) lord of 8th from any Bhava, (2) Sun, (3) Saturn, (4) lord of Gulika's sign, (5) lord of Gulika's Navamsa → in that planet's Dasa, loss of that Bhava occurs.

## Sarvartha Chintamani — Raja Yogas & Dasha Rules (Ch. 9, 13-17)

**Source:** Sarvartha Chintamani, Chapters 9 (Raja Yogas), 13-16 (Dasha Systems), 17 (Miscellaneous)

### Rule SC-CO1: Raja Yogas — Status Verification

Sarvartha Chintamani provides the most exhaustive Raja Yoga classification. The key principle for BTR:

**Raja Yoga forms when Kendra (angular) and Kona (trinal) lords combine:**

| Conjunction | Result |
|------------|--------|
| 1st lord + 9th lord | Fame, wealth, authority |
| 4th lord + 10th lord | Lands, vehicles, government position |
| 5th lord + 9th lord | Dharmakarmadhipati Yoga — ethical leadership |
| 9th lord + 10th lord | High government position |
| 5th lord in 9th or 9th in 5th | Inherited status |
| All 4 Kendra lords strong | Maharaja Yoga |

**BTR Application:** The rectified birth time must produce Raja Yoga combinations consistent with the native's known social/professional status.

### Rule SC-CO2: Dasha Event Principle

Events occur when the **Dasha lord** and **Bhukti lord** both signify the event's house(s). Specifically:

1. **Dasha lord** must own, occupy, or aspect the event house
2. **Bhukti lord** must own, occupy, or aspect the event house or its lord
3. The Antardasha lord activates the precise timing within the Bhukti

### Rule SC-CO3: Vimshottari Dasha Years

| Planet | Years | Key Events |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Sun | 6 | Authority, health events, father events |
| Moon | 10 | Mind, mother events, public life |
| Mars | 7 | Career action, accidents, land |
| Rahu | 18 | Sudden changes, foreign, unconventional |

| Saturn | 19 | Consolidation, struggle, longevity |

| Ketu | 7 | Spiritual, endings, detachment |
| Venus | 20 | Marriage, luxury, relationships |

### Rule SC-CO4: House-Lord Karaka Principle

Each house's matters are judged by three factors:
1. **Bhava** (the house itself)
2. **Bhava Lord** (lord of the house)
3. **Karaka** (natural significator)

All three must be considered for complete event analysis. If the Bhava Lord is weak but the Karaka is strong, the event may still occur but with modifications.

**BTR Application:** When verifying events, check all three factors. A rectified time must produce consistent signification across all three for known major events.

# Classical Rules from Jataka Bharanam (Dhundiraja)

## Rule 1: Yogas from Planetary Pairs (Dvanda Yogas)

The text extensively covers **all possible planetary pairs** (2, 3, 4-planet conjunctions) with specific effects:

**Surya + Chandra:** Royal favor, powerful position
**Surya + Mangala:** Courage, authority but conflicts
**Surya + Budha:** Intellect, administrative skill
**Surya + Guru:** Wisdom, royal advisor
**Surya + Shukra:** Wealth, artistic inclination
**Surya + Shani:** Struggle, service, delayed success
**Chandra + Mangala:** Impulsive, courageous, accident-prone
**Chandra + Budha:** Intellectual, communicative, versatile
**Chandra + Guru:** Auspicious, Gaja-Kesari yoga, wisdom
**Chandra + Shukra:** Artistic, comfortable life
**Chandra + Shani:** Depressive tendency, delayed results
**Mangala + Budha:** Strategic, calculative
**Mangala + Guru:** Leadership, dharma-protector
**Mangala + Shukra:** Passionate, artistic with aggression
**Mangala + Shani:** Cruel, enduring, struggles
**Budha + Guru:** Supreme wisdom, teacher
**Budha + Shukra:** Poetry, arts, eloquence
**Budha + Shani:** Logic, deep thinking, investigative
**Guru + Shukra:** Wealth, knowledge, spirituality
**Guru + Shani:** Dharma-karma balance, philosopher
**Shukra + Shani:** Creativity with discipline

## Rule 3: Shad-varga-based Strength

**BTR RULE:** The text emphasizes that yoga results depend on **Shad-varga** (six divisional chart) strength. Vargottama planets (same sign in Rasi and Navamsha) give stronger results.

---

## Rule 4: Combination with Raja Yoga Bhanga

> **Source:** Jataka Bharanam, Raja Yoga Bhanga Adhyaya

Even powerful Yogas can be cancelled if:
1. All planets are in enemy signs
2. Multiple planets are debilitated
3. No planet aspects Lagna or Chandra
4. Birth during natural portents (Udakapata, Vyatipata, etc.)
5. Sun is in Tula at parama-nichamsha

---

## Rule 5: Karya Siddhi Yogas (Success Combinations)

**BTR RULE:** Success in endeavors is assured when:
- Lagna lord is in kendra/trikona
- Karma (10th) lord is strong
- Artha (2nd/11th) lords are connected
- Strong benefic influences the 10th and Lagna

---

## Rule 6: Shubha-Ashubha Yoga Mixture

**BTR RULE:** When both auspicious and inauspicious yogas are present, the result depends on:
1. **Number** of planets involved
2. **Strength** (Graha Bala) of the planets
3. **Dasha sequence** — the stronger dasha manifests first
4. **Shad-varga** placement
5. **Drishti** (aspects) modifying the yoga

---

## Rule 7: Nakshatra-based Yogas

**BTR RULE:** Some yogas form based on nakshatra placement:
- **Janma Tara** (birth nakshatra lord) effects
- **Nakshatra-based dashas** modify yoga results
- **Moola/Ashlesha** pada-specific effects modify planetary combinations

## 1. Ashtakavarga — The Benefic Dot System

### Introduction
Ashtakavarga is a system of benefic dots (Bindus) and malefic strokes (Rekhas) calculated from the 7 planets and Lagna — 8 sources total, hence "Ashtaka-varga."

Each planet moving from its birth position produces effects varying with its progress through the 12 Rasis. The 8 seats (7 planets + Lagna) form the basis for all good and evil calculations.

## 2. Gochara Interpretation (Sloka 3-9)

**Key Rule:** A planet's transit effect is:
- **Benefic** if benefic dots in the Rasi > 4
- **Mixed** if benefic dots = 4
- **Malefic** if benefic dots < 4

**5+ dots** → benefic effect despite unfavorable position
**3 or fewer dots** → malefic effect despite favorable position

## 3. Upachaya vs. Apachaya (Sloka 10-11)
- **Upachaya houses (3, 6, 10, 11):** Planets here with more dots ENHANCE good effects
- **Apachaya houses (1, 2, 4, 5, 7, 8, 9, 12):** Planets here REDUCE good effects even with many dots
- These are determined relative to Lagna or Moon at birth, NOT transit positions

---

## 4. Minimum Dots (Sloka 10)
Dots short of the prescribed minimum → untoward effect despite favorable position.

---

## 5. Excess Dots (Sloka 11)
Dots in excess of minimum → good effect despite unfavorable position.

### Verifying Horoscope Accuracy — Foundational Principle

**Source:** Sooraj Krishna Shastri — Bhagwat Darshan

Chart accuracy is the most fundamental requirement in Vedic astrology. Even a slight error in planets, Lagna, or house positions makes the entire prediction wrong. Before analyzing any birth chart, the following six checks are mandatory.

---

## Rule 1: Verify Lagna Using Sun's Position

**Source:** Sooraj Krishna Shastri — Bhagwat Darshan

Sun moves through 12 houses in 24 hours. Check Sun's position relative to Lagna using this table:

| Time (from Sunrise) | Sun's Position | Corresponding House |
|--------------------|----------------|-------------------|
| At Sunrise | On Lagna (Ascendant) | 1st House |
| 2 hours after sunrise | On 12th House | 12th House |
| 4 hours after sunrise | On 11th House | 11th House |
| 6 hours after sunrise | On 10th House | 10th House |
| 8 hours after sunrise | On 9th House | 9th House |
| 10 hours after sunrise | On 8th House | 8th House |
| 12 hours after sunrise (Sunset) | On 7th House | 7th House |
| Before sunrise | On 2nd House | 2nd House |

If Sun's position in the chart does not match this rule, the birth time or Lagna is incorrect.

---

## Rule 2: Verify Mercury's Proximity to Sun

**Source:** Sooraj Krishna Shastri — Bhagwat Darshan

Mercury is always near the Sun:
- In the **same sign** as Sun, OR
- **One sign ahead** of Sun, OR
- **One sign behind** Sun

If Mercury appears beyond one sign's distance from Sun, the planetary positions or Lagna are erroneous.

---

## Rule 3: Verify Venus's Proximity to Sun

**Source:** Sooraj Krishna Shastri — Bhagwat Darshan

Venus is also constrained near the Sun:
- In the **same sign** as Sun, OR
- **Two signs ahead** of Sun, OR
- **Two signs behind** Sun

If Venus exceeds two signs' distance from Sun, the chart determination is certainly wrong.

---

## Rule 4: Verify Rahu-Ketu Opposition (180°)

**Source:** Sooraj Krishna Shastri — Bhagwat Darshan

Rahu and Ketu are always exactly **opposite (180° apart)** in the zodiac.
- If Rahu is in the 1st house, Ketu must be in the 7th house
- If Rahu is in the 2nd house, Ketu must be in the 8th house

This is an **invariable rule**. If Rahu-Ketu opposition is broken, the chart is definitely incorrect.

---

## Rule 5: Verify Moon's Sign as Janma Rashi

**Source:** Sooraj Krishna Shastri — Bhagwat Darshan

The sign occupied by Moon is the native's **Janma Rashi** (birth Moon sign). Verify:
- The Moon sign matches the native's stated rashi
- The Nakshatra (birth star) matches the expected star for that time
- If the Moon's position does not conform, the chart is inaccurate

---

## Rule 6: Verify Sun's Annual Sign Transit

**Source:** Sooraj Krishna Shastri — Bhagwat Darshan

Sun moves ~30° per month into the next sign. Use this transit calendar to verify Sun's position:

If Sun's sign in the chart doesn't match this annual motion, the date or time is erroneous.

## Summary: Six Verification Rules

**Source:** Sooraj Krishna Shastri — Bhagwat Darshan

> **"An accurate chart alone gives accurate predictions."**

| # | Check | Rule | If Failed |

# PVR Narasimha Rao — Tajaka (Annual Chart) Yoga Combinations

**Source:** "Vedic Astrology: An Integrated Approach" by P.V.R. Narasimha Rao (2000), Part 4, Chapter 29.2 — Tajaka Yogas, pages 401-407

---

## 1. Ithasala Yoga (Primary Event Yoga)

> **Pages 401-402**

**Definition:** When two planets have a rasi aspect (drishti) and the faster-moving planet has a lower advancement in its rasi than the slower-moving planet (i.e., the faster is behind the slower), they will eventually form an exact aspect as the faster catches up. This is Ithasala yoga.

**Formula:** Faster planet's advancement < slower planet's advancement AND they have an aspect.

**Results:** Fulfillment of matters signified by the two planets.

**BTR Application:** In annual charts, an Ithasala between lagna lord and 7th lord/vivaha saham lord confirms marriage in that year. Between lagna lord and 10th lord → career advancement.

**Retrograde exception (page 401):** If faster planet is retrograde, the normal rule reverses. If a faster planet is about to become retrograde, it may not catch up even if it appears to be behind.

---

## 2. Eesarpha Yoga (Negation of Ithasala)

> **Page 401**

**Definition:** If two planets have an aspect and the faster-moving planet has a HIGHER advancement in its rasi than the slower-moving planet, we have Eesarpha yoga.

**Results:** Bad yoga — failures, disappointments.
- Lagna lord with Eesarpha with 5th lord or putra saham lord or Jupiter → disappointments related to children
- Lagna lord with Eesarpha with 10th lord or 5th lord or raajya saham lord or Sun (for leaders) → loss of power

**Retrograde modification (page 402):** If faster planet is retrograde and less advanced → Eesarpha. If retrograde and more advanced → Ithasala.

**BTR Application:** Eesarpha in the annual chart of a year when a known negative event occurred provides validation. For example, Eesarpha between lagna lord and 10th lord in a year of career setback.

---

## 3. Nakta Yoga (Fulfillment Through a Third Party)

> **Page 402**

**Definition:** Two planets have an aspect but no Ithasala/Eesarpha. A faster-moving third planet has Ithasala with both. This shows fulfillment through the person shown by the third planet.

**Example:** Lagna in Ta, Venus in Ge, Mars in Sc. Venus (lagna lord in 2nd) and Mars (7th lord in 7th) have no aspect. Moon in Cn has Ithasala with both Venus and Mars → marriage with help of younger sibling (Moon = 3rd lord).

**BTR Application:** When a major life event was mediated by a third person, check if Nakta yoga is present in the annual chart involving the mediating planet.

---

## 4. Yamaya Yoga (Fulfillment After Delays)

> **Pages 402-403**

**Definition:** Same situation as Nakta, but the third planet is slower than both planets. Shows fulfillment after obstacles and delays.

**Example:** Venus-Mars situation without aspect, Jupiter (slower) forms Ithasala with both → marriage with help of elders after delays.

**BTR Application:** Events that occurred after significant obstacles correlate with Yamaya yoga mediated by a slower planet (Jupiter, Saturn) in the annual chart.

---

## 5. Manahoo Yoga (Cancellation of Ithasala)

> **Page 403**

**Definition:** Two planets have Ithasala. If Saturn or Mars is in conjunction with (or aspects, according to some) the faster moving planet within deeptaamsa (orb), Manahoo yoga cancels the Ithasala and gives failures, quarrels, loss of wealth.

**Example:** Jupiter-Moon Ithasala. Saturn at 19° in Cn (same sign as Moon) cancels it.

**BTR Application:** Ithasala promising a positive event that didn't materialize may be explained by Manahoo yoga cancellation from Saturn/Mars.

---

## 6. Kamboola Yoga (Empowered Ithasala)

> **Pages 403-404**

**Definition:** Two planets have Ithasala. If Moon has Ithasala with either (or both), Kamboola yoga adds power. Moon's strength determines how much.

**BTR Application:** Major life events coinciding with Kamboola-enhanced Ithasala indicate a powerful, decisive event manifestation.

---

## 7. Gairi-Kamboola Yoga (Delayed Empowerment)

> **Page 404**

**Definition:** Moon is in the last degree of a rasi and will form Ithasala after moving to the next rasi with one of the planets in an existing Ithasala.

**Conditions:** Moon must not be exalted, debilitated, or in own navamsa/drekkana/hadda.

**Result:** Realization with help from others, after some delay.

---

## 8. Khallasara Yoga (Destruction of Significations)

> **Page 404**

**Definition:** Lagna lord is between Moon and another planet X without Ithasala with either. Destroys the significations of planet X.

**Example:** Lagna in Vi, Moon in Ar, Mercury (lagna lord) in Ta, Jupiter in Ge. Mercury between Moon and Jupiter without Ithasala → Jupiter's significations (mother, education, house, vehicles, marriage) destroyed.
